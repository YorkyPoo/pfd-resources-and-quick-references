    PERSONNEL POLICY City of Poquoson, Virginia

    Policy Title: General Provisions Effective Date: Section No.: 1 Supersedes Policy:

    ACCESSIBILITY OF MANUAL:

    Copies of the City of Poquoson Personnel Manual shall be made available on the City’s website and a hard copy provided to each Department Head to be readily available for review by any employee.

    PURPOSE OF THESE RULES:

    These rules set forth the principles and procedures to be followed by the City of Poquoson in the administration of personnel policies. The objective of these rules is to provide reasonable, fair, and equitable treatment for all City employees and to ensure that the citizens served by the City derive the benefits and advantages which can be expected from a competent staff of City employees.

    POSITIONS COVERED BY THESE RULES:

    These rules shall apply to all positions and offices in the City’s merit system service. The following exceptions are not covered:

    Members of City Council

    City Manager

    City Attorney

    Contractual Employees

    Appointed Members of Boards or Commissions Constitutional Officers

    Registrar

    AMENDMENT AND REVISION OF RULES:

    Amendments and revisions of these rules shall be initiated by the City Manager, with recommendations made to the City Council for adoption.

    APPLICATION OF THE RULES:

    All positions existing and hereinafter created within the City’s service, except those listed above, are placed within the jurisdiction of the City and shall be subject to the rules and procedures as

    are indicated in this Manual, or as set forth by action of the City Council. This document is not to be construed as creating a contract or expectation of employment for a definite term. An employee is free to terminate employment at any time, preferably with notice, for any reason.

    IMPLEMENTATION:

    The responsibility for implementing the provisions of this Manual is hereby vested in the City Manager, or his/her designee, except as specifically designated within the rules.

    SAVINGS CLAUSE:

    If any section or part of a section of these rules is held by any Court or legislation to be invalid or unconstitutional, the same shal! not invalidate or impair the validity, force and effect of any other section or part of a section of these rules unless it clearly appears that such other section or part of a section is wholly or necessarily dependent for its operation upon a section or part of a section so held invalid or unconstitutional.

    Policy Title: Definitions Effective Date: Section No.: 2 Supersedes Policy: PURPOSE:

    As used in these policies, unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the words included in these definitions shall have the meaning herein given to them. DEFINITIONS:

    Allocate

    The act of assigning each position to its proper classification.

    Anniversary Date

    The recurring date of original appointment or the date of promotion, except that such shall be adjusted for suspensions, unauthorized leaves of absence and separation and re- employment.

    Appeal

    An application for review of an alleged grievance by disciplinary action. Appointment

    The offer and acceptance by a person of a position either on a full-time, part-time, temporary or seasonal basis. A temporary appointment shall terminate automatically upon completion of the project necessitating the appointment.

    Class/Classification

    A position or group of positions having similar duties and responsibilities, requiring

    similar qualifications, which can be properly designated by one title indicative of the nature of the work and which carry the same salary range.

    Class Specification

    A written statement describing the duties and responsibilities and minimum qualification requirements of a position.

    Continuous Service

    Employment in the classified service which is uninterrupted except for authorized leave(s) of absence.

    Demotion

    The assignment of an employee to a position in a lower class.

    Exempt Employee — Fair Labor Standards Act

    Exempt employees generally fall into three major categories: executive, administrative, and professional. To qualify, employecs must meet prescribed requirements regarding salary, duties and responsibilities. These employees are not entitled to overtime compensation.

    Full-Time Permanent Position

    A full-time permanent position is defined as a position in which the incumbent works a full-time, 35 hour per week schedule for every seven calendar day period on a continuous basis. Full-time permanent positions have no time limitation with respect to the duration of job assignment. Full-time permanent employees are eligible to receive full fringe benefits.

    Grievance

    A grievance shall be a complaint or dispute relative to an employee’s employment.

    Lay-off

    The involuntary, non-disciplinary separation of an employee from employment with the City.

    Non-Exempt Employee — Fair Labor Standards Act

    Non-exempt employees are all employees that are not classified as “exempt” and who must be compensated for all hours of work in excess of their identified work week.

    Overtime

    Overtime occurs when a non-exempt employee works more than 40 hours within a work week in accordance with State and Federal law at a rate of time and a half. For Policemen and Firemen overtime calculations will be performed in accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act as it pertains specifically to employees whose work period is defined in Section 207(k) of the Act.

    Part-Time Permanent Position

    A part-time permanent position is defined as a position in which the incumbent works less than a 30 hour per week schedule on a part-time, continuous basis. Hours will be calculated on an annual basis over a 12-month period. Part-time permanent positions have no time limitation with respect to the duration of job assignment. Part-time permanent employees are not eligible to receive fringe benefits.

    Temporary Part-Time Position

    A temporary part-time position is defined as a position in which the incumbent works less than 30 hours per week for a maximum period not to exceed twelve (12) months. Temporary employees are not eligible to receive fringe benefits.

    Seasonal Position

    A seasonal position is short-term not to exceed four (4) months such as a lifeguard at the Municipal pool. Hours worked are “‘as necessary and appropriate”.

    Position

    A group of duties assigned to one person or job.

    Probationary Employee

    Any employee who is serving a probationary period following original appointment (hiring) or following promotion, prior to being regularly appointed to a regular position and class in the classified service, with City Manager’s approval.

    Probationary Period - New Hires

    A period of twelve (12) months following hiring whereby the employee’s performance is carefully evaluated in order to attain regular status. At any time during the probationary period, the Department Head and City Manager may terminate the employee. Any

    termination prior to expiration of the probationary period shall be final with no right of appeal and with no severance compensation due to the employee.

    Probationary Period - Promotions

    A period of six (6) months following a promotion during which the employee’s performance is carefully evaluated in order to attain regular status. At any time during the probationary period, the Department Head and City Manager may return the employee to their pre-promotional position based on unsatisfactory performance. Probationary Period — Unsatisfactory Performance

    A twelve (12) month period whereby an employee whose overall annual performance evaluation is rated unsatisfactory or needs improvement. Should the employee receive a second consecutive annual performance evaluation with an overall rating of needs improvement or unsatisfactory, the employee’s service with the City shall be terminated upon recommendation of the Department Head and with the concurrence of the City Manager.

    Promotion

    The assignment of an employee to a position in a higher class than the position from which the assignment is made.

    Transfer

    The voluntary or involuntary change of an employee’s assignment from one position to another.

    Work Day

    The scheduled number of hours an employee is assigned to work in a 24-hour period, which may vary depending on position.

    Work Cycle

    The scheduled number of hours an employee is assigned to work during a work cycle, for which the employee receives a paycheck. This may also be known as the Pay Period.

    Work Week The scheduled number of hours an employee is assigned to work during a calendar week. Vacancy

    A position existing or newly created, which is not occupied, and for which funds are available,

    EMPLOYEE CLASSIFICATIONS:

    Employment classifications are based on job assignments and responsibilities. They are used to promote consistency and uniformity. Poquoson has adopted a comprehensive position Classification Plan and Pay Schedule which encompasses all full-time regular, and regular part-time positions within the City.

    The Position Classification Plan groups into classes those positions which are essentially similar in type of work performed, difficulty, and responsibility, so as to warrant being placed in the same pay range. The Classification Plan provides a list of job titles, descriptions of the nature and requirements of the work in each class, and the allocation of specific positions to the appropriate classes. The job titles as listed in the Classification Plan will be used in all personnel records and transactions. It is the duty of the City Manager to amend the classification specifications when necessary so that specifications for each class will accurately reflect the current duties and responsibilities of the position. If a Department Head or individual employee feels that the Classification Plan is inaccurate or that a position is improperly classified, the problem should be directed to the attention of the City Manager. The City Manager may then make a further investigation of the matter and take appropriate action.

    RECLASSIFICATIONS:

    Reclassification can occur in several ways. The most common is the reclassification of an entire class of workers. Reclassification can also take place when the work performed on a particular job changes substantially over a period of time. Often these changes take place when new technology is introduced, or a mission of a department is changed in response to changing legislation. A third type of reclassification is done for individuals whose jobs change through design or evolution. Reclassification is generally initiated through independent, outside review, but can be initiated at the request of the individual, supervisor, Department Head or City Manager.

    If an individual, supervisor, or Department Head initiates a request for a reclassification, it will be accompanied by written supporting documentation. Reclassifications do not take place independent of consideration of other positions within the overall classification system.

    When a request for reclassification is received a Position Analysis Questionnaire (PAQ) should be completed by the incumbent(s) under review. Key questions concerning critical job factors should be answered to assist in determining the proper level within the overall pay and classification system. The analysis of all the information is then compared to the current position descriptions for the various classification levels and weighed to determine the correct classification assignment. If it is determined that a reclassification of an individual is justified, the City Manager will direct appropriate action to finalize the request.

    UPGRADES: An employee whose position is reclassified to a higher grade shall receive an increase in

    pay per grade, with a maximum of 10 percent increase, or the beginning pay of the new salary range, whichever is greater in accordance with the schedule below:

    Grade Increase Salary Increase Percentage 1 2.5 2 5.0 3 7.5 4 or more 10

    In cases where an employee would be reclassified from non-exempt to exempt status additional flexibility in pay may be granted by the City Manager.

    DOWNGRADES:

    An employee whose position is reclassified to a lower grade shall retain his/her current rate of pay if it falls within the salary range of the new position.

    An employee whose position is reclassified to a lower grade and whose salary is above the range of the new position shall retain his/her current rate of pay for a period of not more than six (6) months. At the end of the six (6) month period, the employee’s salary shall be adjusted to the end of the range of the new position.

    PAY PLAN:

    Poquoson has adopted a pay plan wherein jobs are graded and reviewed periodically. When an employee is hired by the City he/she should understand that the City agrees to review his/her job and pay status within a classification periodically. Further information regarding the Pay Plan and the employee’s status therein may be obtained from the City Manager’s office.

    It is the policy of the City of Poquoson not to make advances on employee compensation. The exception would be only for extreme emergencies with prior written approval of the City Manager.

    Policy Title: Code of Ethics Effective Date: Section No.: 4 Supersedes Policy: PURPOSE:

    It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to establish a written Code of Ethics Policy to serve as a guide for employee conduct in supporting the City mission of providing outstanding, professional public service to the residents and businesses of our community.

    SCOPE:

    This policy applies to all City of Poquoson permanent full-time, permanent part-time and seasonal part-time employees.

    CODE OF ETHICS Employees of the City of Poquoson shall be dedicated to the concepts of effective and democratic local government and the essential partnership between responsible elected

    officials and dedicated staff.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall always conduct themselves, on and off duty, in a manner that positively reflects on themselves and the organization.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall accept responsibility for their actions and inactions and for the consequences thereof.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall support the concept of faimess and the value of diverse thoughts and opinions.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall avoid situations that would adversely affect their credibility or the credibility of the organization.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall be truthful and honest at all times.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall conduct their personal affairs in a manner that does not improperly influence the performance of their official duties or bring discredit to the organization.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall recognize that they serve in a position of public trust that requires stewardship in the honest and efficient use of publicly owned resources and that these resources are to be protected from misuse, abuse and theft.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall demonstrate professionalism, integrity and competence in the performance of their duties and use information obtained by virtue of their position only to the benefit of the community.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall avoid financial investment, outside employment, outside business interests and activities that create the perception of impropriety.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall not propose or accept personal rewards, special privileges, gifts or other benefits that may create a conflict of interest, or the appearance thereof.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall not engage in activities involving alcohol or other substance use or abuse that will impair their ability to perform official duties or compromise the safety of others.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall treat all residents, visitors and fellow employees with respect and in a non-discriminatory manner.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall not harass, intimidate or threaten any person and shall promptly report persons engaging in such behavior.

    Employees of the City of Poquoson shall responsibly use social networking, electronic communication or other media in a manner that does not discredit, dishonor or embarrass the organization and promptly report such activities by fellow employees.

    Policy Title: Americans With Disabilities Act Effective Date: Section No.: 6 Supersedes Policy: PURPOSE:

    It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to comply with the provisions of the Americans With Disabilities Act (ADA).

    SCOPE:

    This policy applies to all City of Poquoson employees and applicants for employment. DEFINITIONS:

    Americans With Disabilities Act (ADA)

    The Americans With Disabilities Act is defined as the federal law that prohibits discrimination against a qualified individual with a disability who can perform the essential functions of a position, with or without accommodation.

    Disability

    A disability is defined as a temporary or permanent physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities (i.e., sitting, standing, lifting, reaching, hearing, speaking, seeing, walking, breathing, learning, caring for oneself or working).

    Essential Functions

    Essential functions are defined as primary job duties that are fundamental to a position. Reasonable Accommodation

    Reasonable accommodation is defined as a modification to the application process, a job,

    or work environment that permits a qualified applicant or employee with a disability to participate in the job application process, to perform the essential functions of a job, or to

    partake of the same benefits and privileges of employment as are enjoyed by employees without disabilities.

    Undue Hardship

    Undue hardship is defined as any accommodation that would be unduly costly, extensive, substantial or disruptive or would fundamentally alter the nature of operation of the City’s business.

    GENERAL PROVISIONS:

    Qualified Individual

    An individual is entitled to employment rights under the ADA if he/she is a qualified individual with a disability. A qualified individual with a disability

    1. possesses the skills, education, employment experience and/or licenses required for a particular job and

    2. is able to perform the essential functions of a particular job. Disability Under the ADA, a person has a disability if he/she:

    3. has a temporary or permanent physical or mental impairment that substantially limits a major life activity;

    4. has a record of such an impairment; or

    5. is regarded as having such an impairment. Reasonable Accommodation Reasonable accommodation is any change or adjustment to a job or work environment that permits a qualified applicant or employee with a disability to participate in the job application process, perform essential functions of a job, or enjoy benefits and privileges of employment equal to those enjoyed by employees without disabilities.

    6. A reasonable accommodation may include:

    a. acquiring or modifying equipment or devices;

    b. job restructuring;

    c. part-time or modified work schedules,

    d. reassignment to a vacant position; e. adjusting or modifying examinations, training material or policies; f. providing readers and interpreters; and

    g. making the workplace readily accessible to and usable by people with disabilities.

    1. Identifying a Reasonable Accommodation The process of determining an appropriate reasonable accommodation is an interactive, problem solving technique involving both the employer and the

    qualified individual with a disability which involves:

    a. analyzing the particular job involved and determining its purpose and essential functions;

    b. determining the precise job-related limitations imposed by the individual’s disability and how those limitations could be overcome with a reasonable accommodation; and

    c. identifying potential accommodations and assessing the effectiveness each would have in enabling the individual to perform the essential functions of the position.

    Undue Hardship

    |. The ADA does not require the provision of a reasonable accommodation if doing so would cause an undue hardship.

    1. Factors considered in determining whether an accommodation constitutes an undue hardship include:

    a. the cost of the accommodation;

    b. the City’s size;

    c. financial resources; and

    d. the nature and structure of the City’s operation.

    1. The City Manager shall make final decisions regarding reasonable accommodation and undue hardship.

    Policy Title: Recruitment, Hiring & Promotion Effective Date: Section No.: 7 Supersedes Policy: PURPOSE:

    The objective of the City of Poquoson is to provide for an efficient and effective hiring process that promotes equal employment opportunity and ensures a qualified workforce.

    SCOPE: This policy applies to all City of Poquoson full-time and permanent part-time positions. RECRUITMENT PROCEDURES:

    Department Heads wishing to initiate a hiring process shall submit a written request to initiate recruitment with the City Manager for all permanent positions. Part-time seasonal positions are exempt from this requirement, shall be initiated by the Department Head and shall require external recruitment in all cases. The request shall detail the necessity for filling the position, proposed recruitment process, including a request for internal, external and promotional recruitment, proposed interview questions, selection panel and any special selection processes such as physical agility testing, medical examination, credit check, polygraph examinations, etc. appropriate to the position. All testing procedures shall be consistent with State and Federal regulations concerning employee selection. The costs of any pre-employment testing shall be borne by the City of Poquoson.

    The City Manager will approve or modify the request to initiate the hiring process.

    Positions approved for external recruitment shall be advertised for a minimum of two weeks and shall be advertised in a local newspaper, the City website and the Virginia Employment Commission. In no case shall an initial position application period be established as “until filled.” The second advertisement for the same position shall be classified as “open until filled” if approved by the City Manager.

    Positions approved for internal recruitment shall be advertised to all City employees through inclusion in the City Manager’s weekly report to the employees. Notices for

    promotional recruitment shall be provided to each member of the hiring department. In both cases the minimum advertisement period shall be one week.

    SELECTION PROCEDURES:

    All persons applying for a vacant City position shall complete the appropriate City of Poquoson Employment Application. Applications shall be accepted for posted vacancies only. Falsification or omission of required information shall result in immediate disqualification of the applicant and/or termination of employment. Applicants for permanent City positions must be at least 18 years of age with the exception of applicants for sworn law enforcement positions who must be at least 21 years old. Complete applications must be received by the City Manager’s Office by the close of business on the application period’s closing date.

    The selection committee shall carefully review each application for employment. Applications shall be evaluated and ranked based upon the qualifications, knowledge, skills and ability necessary to perform those duties outlined for the position. Those applicants whose qualifications, knowledge, skills and abilities most closely match those established for the position shall be invited for an interview. In addition, applicants who are residents of the City of Poquoson whose qualifications, knowledge, skills and abilities meet or exceed those established for the position shall also be invited to interview. The minimum number of persons to be interviewed shall be three.

    All interview questions must be approved by the City Manager as part of the approved request to initiate recruitment so as to ensure that there are no violations of the Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) laws.

    When evaluating equally qualified applicants, preference shall be given to City residents. HIRING PROCEDURES:

    The Hiring Department Head shall prepare a written hiring recommendation for consideration by the City Manager.     PROMOTION:

    Promotions may be either inter- or intra-departmental and must be approved by the Department Head(s) and the City Manager. All promotions shall be based on merit. A promotion may take the form of a transfer from one department to another.

    DEMOTION:

    A demotion is the assignment of an employee to a position in a lower classification having a lower maximum salary.

    The City Manager shall have the authority to demote an employee. If it becomes necessary to recommend demotion of an employee, the Department Head should make the recommendation to the City Manager, in writing, citing the reasons. The causes for demotion shall normally be due to the removal of higher-level duties and responsibilities or the inability of the employee to assume or perform duties of the position, although the City reserves the right to demote an employee for disciplinary, non-disciplinary or any other reasons.

    Employees demoted for reasons of reduction-in-force initiated to increase efficiency or to meet budget restrictions should be assigned at the same dollar level in the new classification range. Should the present salary of the employee exceed the maximum of the assigned range, the employee’s salary should be capped or frozen at the top of the range, with continued eligibility for any cost-of-living raises granted to general employees.

    An employee not on a probationary status in the position from which he has been demoted has the right to appeal demotions through the grievance procedure as outlined in the Policy Manual.

    An employee who has not completed his/her probationary period prior to the demotion will be required to serve a new probationary period. Regular employees may be required to serve a new probationary period upon the recommendation of the Department Head.

    LATERAL TRANSFER

    A lateral transfer is the switching of an employee to a job on an equal level in another department. An employee may submit a written request for consideration for a transfer to another department or to another section within the department, if a position is vacant. A transfer must be approved by the affected Department Head(s) and the City Manager. Employee requests for transfer to another department shall be made, in writing, to the Department Head with a copy to the City Manager.

    The City Manager may transfer an employee temporarily, or permanently, when said transfer is deemed to be in the best interests of the City following determination that the employee possesses the minimum knowledge, skills and abilities (KSAs) for the new job classification for which they are being considered.

    NON-LATERAL TRANSFER

    There are two types of non-lateral transfers, both involving reduction in classification level: voluntary and involuntary.

    Voluntary Transfer

    An employee who requests a voluntary transfer to another position which involves a reduction in classification levels should, with the approval of the City Manager, be placed in the new range at the same step or percentage into the new range at his/her present level in the former pay grade. If the employee’s present salary is above the maximum salary level for the range, the salary of the employee should be reduced to the maximum salary level indicated by the new range.

    Involuntary Transfer

    There are two types of involuntary transfer: one is for the good of the employee and the other serves the best interest of the department. An example of a transfer for the good of the employee would be an individual who is no longer physically capable of performing his/her duties, and a reasonable accommodation cannot be made to allow the individual to continue to perform the essential functions of the current position. Rather than terminating such an employee, the department may determine that an involuntary transfer to a less strenuous position would be acceptable. In this instance, should the transfer be to a position classified at a lower level, the employee’s salary would be reduced to the same percentage into the new range as he/she was in the former pay range.

    Involuntary transfers for the best interest of the City are initiated by the City rather than by the employee. Transfers may be of a temporary nature to address a particular problem such as a personnel shortage. Assignments usually exceed thirty (30) days in length and should not be confused with the routine assumption of higher level duties which occurs in the absence of a supervisor on personal leave. Employees assigned, through this type of involuntary transfer to a lower classification level, should not be penalized by a reduction of the current salary. However, if the current salary is higher than the maximum amount of the new pay range, the salary would be frozen until the range would be adjusted to allow for an increase.

    A work week is defined as seven consecutive calendar days; currently identified as Friday through Thursday.

    Full-Time Work Week

    A full-time work week is defined as a work week consisting of 30 or more hours. Permanent Part-Time Work Week

    A part-time work week is defined as a work week consisting of less than 30 hours. Routine Work Schedule

    A routine work schedule is defined as a work week where work hours are the same for each of the days worked as determined by the City Manager. The routine work week

    schedule hours for City employees are 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. with a one hour paid lunch break. Short-term variations can be approved by the Department Head.

    Alternative Work Schedule

    An Alternative Work Schedule is defined as a work week that diverges from the routine work schedule which has been approved by the City Manager as permanent.

    Compressed Work Schedule

    A compressed work week is defined as an alternate work schedule that allows an employee to work a full-time schedule in fewer than five (5) days during a seven (7) day work period or fewer than ten (10) days during a fourteen (14) day work period, by working longer hours each day. Permanent, compressed work schedules shall be approved by the City Manager. Whenever a non-exempt employee participates in a compressed work week schedule, the department head shall be responsible for ensuring that applicable FLSA regulations and personnel policies and procedures are strictly adhered to.

    Certain employees in the Fire and Police Departments are assigned to a compressed work schedule due to the nature of their positions. These employees include those permanent, full-time public safety employees who are primarily responsible for responding to emergencies, answering 911 calls or work in positions where the compressed work schedule meets the operational needs of the department.

    Flexible Work Schedule

    Department Heads have authority to implement flexible work schedules.

    Non-exempt Employee

    A non-exempt employee is defined as an employee who is subject to the overtime provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act.

    Exempt Employee

    An exempt employee is defined as an employee who is not subject to the overtime provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act.

    WORK SCHEDULES

    Routine Work Schedules

    1. Full-time employees shall work a regularly scheduled work week consisting of 30 hours or more, depending upon their specific job positions.

    2. Department Heads/Constitutional Officers may establish specific work hours to meet the requirements of their departments. Any adjustment from the routine work schedule that affects service to the public must be approved by the City Manager.

    Alternative Work Schedules

    With the approval of the City Manager, Department Heads/Constitutional Officers may implement an Alternative Work Schedule for an employee, or a group of employees, that does not affect service to the public or the department’s customers. The approval process is as follows:

    !. An employee or group of employees wishing to obtain an alternative work schedule shall make written request to his/her immediate supervisor for review and approval or disapproval. A non-exempt employee shall not be permitted to request an alternative work schedule that exceeds 40 work hours within a work week.

    1. Upon approval of the request by the immediate supervisor, the request will be submitted by the supervisor to the Department Head/Constitutional Officer for approval or disapproval.

    2. Upon approval of the request by the Department Head/Constitutional Officer, the request is submitted to the City Manager for approval or disapproval.

    3. Once a determination is made by the City Manager, the alternative work schedule request will be returned to the Department Head/Constitutional Officer for implementation, revision, or dismissal of the request.

    An approved, alternative work schedule will be implemented at an agreed upon date between the Department Head/Constitutional Officer and the employee or group of employees, which must be at the beginning of a forthcoming pay cycle.

    Department Heads/Constitutional Officers shall have final authority to resolve any matters that occur as a result of implementation of an alternative work schedule to ensure effective service to the public and the department’s customers.

    Termination of an alternative work schedule may occur in any of the following manners:

    1. Department Heads/Constitutional Officers may terminate an alternative work schedule at their discretion by providing a written notice to the employee or group of employees at least two weeks in advance, except in the case of a department emergency.

    2. Anemployee must provide a written request, at least two weeks in advance, to the immediate supervisor to terminate an alternative work schedule.

    3. Emergency requests for termination of an alternative work schedule initiated by the employee shall be considered by the immediate supervisor and Department Head/Constitutional Officer on the merits of each request.

    4. Anemployee’s or group of employees’ return to a routine work schedule must occur at the beginning of a forthcoming pay cycle.

    Participants of an alternative work schedule shall comply with all applicable statutes, rules, policies and procedures. Failure to do so may result in termination of the alternative work schedule for that participant and may include disciplinary action.

    Attendance

    1. Employees are required to adhere to their work schedules by reporting to work at the specified starting time, departing at the specified ending time, and taking lunch periods and breaks as authorized.

    2. Should an illness or personal emergency prevent an employee from reporting for work or result in a later report time, the employee is expected to notify his/her supervisor prior to the employce starting time, or as soon as possible thereafter.

    Breaks

    1. Breaks are not mandated by law and are granted at the discretion of the Department Head/Constitutional Officer.

    2. A one hour lunch is recommended for full-time employees during their work day.

    3. Breaks cannot be used to change an employee’s start or ending time.

    4. Breaks cannot be combined with an employee’s lunch break to extend lunch privileges.

Employees violating this policy will be subject to appropriate disciplinary action. Except as otherwise provided by law, exempt employees required to work during declared emergencies shall receive one hour of compensatory time for each hour worked.

Once the employee returns to active duty in the Fire Department the normal leave accrual for that Department will be restored.

    Job Restoration

    At the conclusion of the FMLA leave, an employee is entitled to be restored to the same position that was held before the start of the FMLA leave, or to an equivalent position with equivalent benefits, pay, and other terms and conditions of employment. An employee is entitled to reinstatement only if he/she would have continued to be employed had FMLA leave not been taken. Thus, an employee is not entitled to reinstatement if, because of a layoff, reduction in force or other reason, the employee would not be employed at the time job restoration is sought.

    Key employees are entitled to FMLA leave but are not entitled to job restoration if re- employment after the conclusion of the leave would cause a substantial and grievous economic injury to the City. A key employee is a salaried employee who is among the highest paid 10 percent of the workforce. A key employee will be notified in writing of his or her status in response to the employee’s notice of intent to take FMLA leave, unless circumstances do not permit such notice. Ifa key employee is already on FMLA

    leave when notice of key-employee designation is received, the employee will be given a reasonable time to return to work before losing the right to job restoration.

If the employee fails to make the employee’s share of the health plan payments, the employee will be provided written notice of this failure and will be given an additional 15 days to make payment in full. If payment is not made after this notice, health benefit coverage will cease.

For services of more than 180 days, the employee must either return to work or submit notice of intent to return to work within 90 days of release from service.     It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to provide a work environment conducive to the performance of job duties and free from intimidation or coercion in any form. The City upholds a stringent policy against all forms of discrimination, including sexual harassment.

    SCOPE:

    This policy applies to all City of Poquoson employees and applicants for employment. DEFINITIONS:

    Sexual Harassment

    Sexual harassment is defined as a violation under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 that could subject the City, departments and individuals to legal and financial liability. Sexual harassment includes sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and

    other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:

    1. Submission to such conduct is made explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of an individual’s employment;

    2. Submission to or rejection of such conduct by an individual is used as a basis for employment decisions affecting that individual; and/or

    3. Such conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual’s work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive work environment.

    PROCEDURES: Prohibited Conduct 1. Sexual harassment is a prohibited employment practice.

    1. The following examples of conduct may or may not constitute sexual harassment under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 depending upon the circumstances under which they occur. These examples do, however, represent inappropriate behavior and shall constitute misconduct under the Personnel Policies of the City of Poquoson for which appropriate disciplinary action shall be taken. The examples do not represent all forms in which misconduct under this policy or sexual harassment may occur.

    a. Explicit or implicit promises of career advancement or preferential treatment in return for sexual favors including, but not limited to, hiring, promotion, training and other opportunities or benefits; acceptance of a lower standard of work performance; providing higher performance evaluations; and lax time- keepingy when similarly qualified persons are not afforded such benefits or opportunities.

    b. Explicit or implicit threats that an employee’s job shall be adversely affected if sexual demands are rejected, including, but not limited to, lower evaluations, denial of promotions or other employment opportunities, punitive transfers, terminations, and altered or increased work assignments.

    c. Denial of employment opportunities or otherwise adversely affecting a person’s employment based on rejection of sexual attention or demands.

    Education Plan The education plan shall list and describe:

    e The courses to be taken to achieve a degree or professional accreditation. The courses and program listed must enhance the skills of the employee in his or her immediate position, or prepare the employee for another position in the city;

    ¢ The degree/accreditation program to be entered; and

    e The name of the institution where classes will be taken.

    Eligible Courses

    e Courses that are directly related to an employee’s present position, a promotional objective, or another position in the City as confirmed by the Department Head/Constitutional Officer’s concurrence, and are part of an undergraduate, graduate or professional accreditation program.

    e Eligible courses are available only through an accredited college, university, business school, graduate school, trade school, or a technical/professional institute.

    e Electives which are necessary in order to obtain a degree, or other professional accreditation, and complete the education plan but are not directly applicable to an employee’s occupation, are eligible for reimbursement.

    e A grade of “C” (or 2.0) or better for an undergraduate course e A passing grade or better for a graduate course e A grade of “Pass” for a pass/fail course

    An establishment whose sole or primary function is education and training. It does not include professional associations.

    Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program

    The Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program is defined as a training program that is funded out of a dedicated City reserve and administered by the City Manager.

    PROCEDURES

    Requests for Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance

    1. Prior to registering for courses, an employee must make the request for tuition assistance by submitting an education plan through his/her

    immediate supervisor and the Department Head/Constitutional Officer to the City Manager.

    The City Manager shall review the request for participation in the tuition assistance program and shall approve/deny the request. Upon City Manager approval, and pending availability of funds, the employee will be eligible to submit requests for reimbursement as he/she progresses through the degree/accreditation program.

    Requests for individual course reimbursement must be submitted to the City Manager prior to registering for that course. The request must include the total cost of the course and all information concerning the course, such as the name of the institution offering the course, course title, number of credit hours pursued, degree/certification program, etc.

    A copy of all education and training courses completed by employees requiring official City approval for expenditure of City funds shall be placed in the employee’s official personnel record.

    The administration of the City’s Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program shall be the responsibility of the City Manager.

    PERSONNEL POLICY City of Poquoson, Virginia

    Policy Title: Drug and Alcohol Policy Effective Date: Section No.: 40 Supersedes Policy: PURPOSE:

    It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to ensure that all employees are mentally and physically fit for duty during working hours. Strict adherence to this policy will reduce the potential for accidents, absenteeism, substandard performance, poor employee morale and will increase the public’s confidence in the City’s workforce.

    SCOPE:

    The City of Poquoson is committed to maintaining a work environment that is completely free from illegal drugs and alcohol and their effects. City employees also have a personal obligation to refrain from use of illegal drugs and alcoholic beverages on the job in order to be fit for duty while performing employment duties for the City. City employees who use or are under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs while on the job pose serious safety and health risks, not only to themselves, but also to individuals who work or come into contact with such employees. Accordingly, any City employee who possesses, uses, consumes, purchases, sells, transfers, distributes, or manufactures illegal drugs or alcoholic beverages while at work or on the job shall be immediately terminated from the City’s employment. Any City employee who is under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol while at work or on the job shall be immediately terminated from the City’s employment. The illegal use, sale or possession of narcotics, drugs, alcohol or controlled substances while on the job or on City property is prohibited and is a dischargeable offense. Any illegal substance will be turned over to the appropriate law enforcement agency and may result in criminal prosecution.

    Off-the-job illegal drug use which could adversely affect an employee’s job performance or which could jeopardize the safety of others, the public, or City equipment, is proper cause for administrative or disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

    Employees who are arrested for off-the-job drug activity may be considered to be in violation of this policy. In deciding what action to take, management will take into consideration the nature of the charges, the employee’s present job assignment, the

    employee’s record with the City and other factors relating to the impact and circumstances of the employee’s arrest.

    Use of illegal drugs at any time is prohibited. Off duty use of alcohol that is detected just prior, during or immediately following performance of duties for the City, at a level of .04 BAC/BRAC or higher is prohibited. Use of prescription medications without a valid prescription issued to the tested employee is also prohibited. Use of any legal drug whether it is prescribed by a physician or over the counter, is prohibited, if the drug renders the employee unsafe in the employee’s job performance.

    DEFINITIONS: Alcohol or Alcoholic Beverages

    Alcohol, spirits, wine, and beer and any one or more of such varieties containing one-half of one percent or more of alcohol by volume, including mixed alcoholic beverages and every liquid or solid, patented or not, containing alcohol, spirits, wine, or beer and capable of being consumed by a human being.

    At Work/On the Job

    Period of time when an employee (i) is being paid to work, or (ii) has been notified of being subject to being called into work, or (iii) is operating any vehicle or piece of equipment owned or leased by the City, or (iv) is on unpaid breaks or lunch periods while on the job. The words include all on-duty functions performed from the time an employee begins work or is required to be ready to work until he/she is relieved from work and all responsibility for performing work.

    All buildings and other facilities used by the City to conduct its operations plus all work sites to which employces are assigned in the course of the performance of their duties for the City.

    Employee

    Any person employed by the City, in either a full or part-time capacity and also includes such non-City employees as volunteer firefighters and auxiliary police.

    Illegal Drugs

    Those substances identified as “controlled substances”, “marijuana” and “Schedules I, II, Ill, IV, V and VI” as those terms are used or defined in the Drug Control Act, Chapter 34 of Title 54.1 of the Code of Virginia (1950), as amended or in Title 18.2 of the Code of Virginia (1950), as amended. The terms include any substance whose possession is prohibited by law or which is used in a manner different from that which is authorized by law or which has been illegally obtained.

    Any drug (a) not legally obtainable or (b) legally obtainable but not legally obtained or used. Therefore, the term includes prescription drugs obtained illegally and prescription drugs not being used for prescribed purposes. It also includes marijuana, cocaine and heroin and derivative of those drugs, among other illegal drugs.

    Legal Drug

    Includes prescribed and over-the-counter drugs legally obtained and being used for the purpose for which they were prescribed and/or manufactured.

    Reasonable Suspicion

    A belief based on observed, specific, objective facts where the natural inference to be drawn under the circumstances is that the person is under the influence of drugs or alcohol. An unexplained workplace accident may be considered to provide reasonable suspicion.

    Safety Sensitive All employment positions whose work duties involve:

    (i) hazardous or potentially hazardous functions or requires the use or handling of hazardous or potentially hazardous equipment or materials that could cause injury to an employee or to the public

    (ii) direct responsibility for the care or safety of children (i.e. 18 years of age or younger)

    (iii) duties of such a nature that a lapse in constant attention or alertness may jeopardize the health or safety of other employees or the public

    Under the Influence

    A condition in which a person has ingested, injected, inhaled or otherwise consumed enough alcohol or illegal drugs to observably affect his manner, disposition, speech, muscular movement, general appearance or behavior.

    The employee is affected by a drug or alcohol or the combination of a drug and alcohol in any detectable manner. The symptoms of influence are not confined to those consistent with misbehavior, or to obvious impairment of physical or mental ability, such as slurred speech or difficulty in maintaining balance.

    APPLICATION OF POLICY:

    Post-Offer/Pre-Employment Testing for Employees Performing Safety-Sensitive Functions

    After an offer of employment has been made, all applicants for City employment involving the performance of safety-sensitive functions shall be required to be tested for illegal drugs. These tests shall be required of a job applicant only after he/she has been offered employment; however, as part of the job application process, all applicants shall execute releases and consent forms which authorize the City to obtain the results of such testing. All applicants for safety-sensitive positions shall also be required to execute releases and consent forms which authorize the City to contact all employers whom the applicant has worked for during the prior two (2) years in order to obtain all records pertaining to the applicant’s drug and alcohol use during that time, including any records regarding the applicant’s refusal to submit to testing for drug and alcohol use. Any offer of employment will be withdrawn from an applicant who refuses to submit to the required pre-employment drug test or who tests positive for illegal drugs in such test.

    Each applicant for employment will be required as a condition of employment to undergo a urine drug screen. Applicants will be asked to read the policy and sign the Pre- Employment offer and Employee Consent to Alcohol and Drug Screening. If an applicant tests positive and is determined to be in violation of this Policy, applicant will be ineligible for employment until; (a) the expiration of 60 days following the date of the test; and (b) the applicant submits to a second urine drug screen which does not disclose that the applicant is in violation of this Policy. If an applicant as described above is hired, another urine drug screen test may be conducted within sixty (60) days following the date of employment and the employee may be subjected to unannounced drug testing for the first twelve (12) months as a condition of employment. Thereafter, the employee will be subject to testing, the same as current employees.

    It is preferred that a new employee be tested prior to reporting for work. However, it is realized that under certain circumstances a new employee may start before test results are known. In such circumstances the employee's hiring is conditional upon passing the drug test.

    All employment offer letters are to contain a statement that employment is conditional upon passing a drug test. Employees will be required to read and sign the pre- Employment Offer and Employee Consent to Alcohol and Drug Screening (Exhibit A) immediately prior to the first day of employment.

    Safety-Sensitive Position Transfer Drug Testing

    Employees shall be subject to drug testing when initially promoted, demoted or otherwise transferred to a safety-sensitive position.

    Post-Accident Testing

    Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted as soon after a vehicular accident as practicable on any employee who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle involved in the accident if:

    1. the accident involved loss of human life; or

    2. the accident resulted in bodily injury to a person who, as a result of the injury, immediately received medical treatment away from the accident scene; or

    3, the accident caused disabling damage to one or more motor vehicles which required the vehicles to be transported away from the scene of the accident by a tow truck or similar vehicle and the employee received a citation under state or local law for a moving traffic violation arising from the accident.

    The term “accident” shall not include occurrences which:

    1. involve only boarding and alighting from a stationary motor vehicle; or
    2. involve only the loading or unloading of cargo.

    In the absence of the need for immediate medical treatment following an accident, all employees shall make themselves readily available for the required testing. Any employee involved in a motor vehicle accident which requires post-accident testing pursuant to this policy shall not use alcohol for eight hours after the accident or until he/she undergoes a post-accident alcohol test, whichever occurs first. Employees will not be tested, however, unless the test for alcohol can be administered within eight hours afier the accident and the test for drugs can be administered within thirty-two hours. If an alcohol test is not administered to an employee within two hours after the accident or if a drug test is not administered within thirty-two hours, the employee’s immediate supervisor shall prepare and maintain records explaining why such tests were not conducted. Tests conducted by authorized federal, state or local officials shall fulfill post-accident testing requirements provided such tests conform to applicable legal requirements and the results of such tests are obtained by the City in a timely manner.

    Reasonable Suspicion Alcohol/Drug Testing

    All employees of the City shall be subject to testing for drugs and/or alcohol based upon a reasonable suspicion of the employee’s violation of this policy. “Reasonable suspicion” shall be based on specific, contemporaneous, articulable observations concerning the employee’s appearance, behavior, speech or body odors. Such observations may include indications of the chronic and withdrawal effects of controlled substances, Reasonable suspicion tests shall be authorized only if the required observations are made during, just before or just after the period of the work day. A reasonable suspicion test may not be conducted by the person who determines that

    reasonable suspicion exists to conduct such a test. No attempt to conduct alcohol tests shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of eight hours after the determination of reasonable suspicion. No attempt to conduct drug tests shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of thirty-two hours after the determination of reasonable suspicion. If an alcohol test is not performed within two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion or a drug test is not performed within thirty-two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion, the employee’s immediate supervisor shall prepare and maintain a record explaining why such a test was not given. A supervisor who makes observations leading to a reasonable suspicion test for alcohol or drugs shall make a written record of his/her observations within twenty-four hours of the observed behavior or before the results of the drug test are released, whichever is earlier.

    Testing Following Rehabilitation and/or Rehire

    An employee who returns to work following rehabilitation shall be required to submit to a return to duty drug/alcohol test prior to returning to work for the City. Furthermore, such employee shall be subject to unannounced, follow-up, fitness-for-duty testing during the year following the employee’s return to work. The frequency of such testing shall be determined by a substance abuse professional. Follow-up testing may extend beyond a year but shall not exceed a sixty month period.

    EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES: No employee shall:

    1. Report to work or perform work duties when their ability to perform job duties is impaired by alcohol or illegal drugs or they are under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs, or a combination of either.

    2. Drink alcoholic beverages for four hours prior to reporting for work.

    3. Report to work or perform work duties after taking any medication or legal drugs, whether prescription or over-the-counter, which, by indication on the prescription label or packaging, may interfere with the employee’s performance of his job duties, without immediately notifying his immediate supervisor of the matter. Employees shall inform their supervisors if they are using a controlled substance which their physician has prescribed for therapeutic purposes.

    4. Possess or use illegal drugs or alcohol at work or on the job.

    5. Refuse to immediately submit to directives for the alcohol and/or drug testing required and to complete all paperwork associated with such testing.

    6. Consume alcoholic beverages during the eight hours following an accident for

    which post-accident testing is required, if an alcohol/drug test has not been performed following the accident.

    1. Refuse to provide, within twenty-four hours after a drug test indicates the presence of a drug, proof of a valid, current prescription, in the employee’s name, for any drug identified.

    2. Fail to notify the employee’s immediate supervisor, within five days, of any conviction of a criminal law involving alcohol or illegal drugs.

    3. Not attend and satisfactorily complete any required substance abuse treatment program.

    SUPERVISOR RESPONSIBILITIES:

    Supervisors are responsible for the fair and consistent enforcement of this policy. Any supervisor who knowingly permits a violation of this policy by City employees under his/her direct supervision shall be subject to discipline, up to and including, dismissal. Upon reasonable suspicion and after an accident requiring post-accident testing, supervisors shall direct an employee to participate in a drug/alcohol test. In conjunction with such directive, the supervisor shall:

    1. Document, in writing, the facts constituting the reasonable suspicion that the employee is using or under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

    2. Communicate the facts giving rise to the suspicion to the employee.

    3. Advise the employee of the consequences of refusal to submit to a required drug and/or alcohol test.

    4. Provide for the employee to be transported to a testing facility.

    5. Notify the Police Department when a reasonable suspicion exists that the employee has possession of an illegal drug.

    EMPLOYEE REQUEST TO RETEST URINE SPECIMEN:

    Urine specimens collected from employees shall be split into two specimens, a “primary” and a “split” specimen. If the analysis of the primary specimen confirms the presence of illegal drugs, the employee will have seventy-two hours to request the split specimen be sent to another approved and certified laboratory for testing. The employee shall pay the cost of the additional test and all handling and shipping costs associated with the additional test at the time such test is requested. Any costs associated with such split

    sample testing which are not paid by an employee who tests positive for alcohol or drugs shall be deducted from any funds or pay due to the employee.

    SUSPENSION PENDING RECEIPT OF TEST RESULTS:

    Employees will be suspended, with pay, pending receipt of the “primary” drug/alcohol test results.

    ENFORCEMENT OF POLICY:

    Applicants for employment performing safety-sensitive functions with the City who test positive for illegal drugs shall be denied employment. Such applicants shall be notified, in writing, that they were rejected on the basis of a positive test result. Employees who test positive for drugs or alcohol under the provisions of this policy shall be terminated from employment. Employees who refuse to participate in a drug or alcohol test or refuse to execute the required consent forms or fail to complete all appropriate paperwork shall be terminated from employment. Refusing to produce sufficient urine or breath for analysis shall be deemed to be a refusal to participate in the test. Any attempt to alter, substitute or tamper with the testing procedure or the test specimen shall also be considered a refusal to participate in the test.

    UNPAID LEAVE FOR EVALUATION AND TREATMENT:

    Employees who voluntarily admit having a problem with alcohol or illegal drugs prior to being apprehended for a violation of this policy may be allowed to take annual leave, sick leave or leave without pay to participate in a rehabilitation program if the City will not suffer any undue hardship as a consequence of granting such leave. If such leave is granted, the employce shall immediately be evaluated by a substance abuse professional who shall determine what help, if any, the employee needs in resolving his/her problem. The employee shall execute a consent form allowing the substance abuse professional to release information concerning the employee’s evaluation and rehabilitation program to the City. Prior to returning to work, a substance abuse professional familiar with the employee’s evaluation and treatment shall render a report to the City certifying that the employee has properly followed and completed any prescribed rehabilitation program.

    REHIRING TERMINATED EMPLOYEES:

    Former employees who are terminated as a result of a violation of this policy may be eligible for consideration for future job openings in accordance with routine City hiring practices provided the applicant presents; (1) a statement from a qualified substance abuse professional which certifies that the former employee has successfully completed a proper rehabilitation program, and (2) satisfactory evidence that the former employee has not used the problem substance(s) during the preceding six months.

    Such evidence may be in the form of rehabilitation program reports, support group attendance records, test results, successful employment in another job without

    performance, conduct or attendance deficiencies or similar information. This paragraph does not, however, impose any obligation upon the City to rehire any former employee or give any former employee any preference for employment.

    TESTING RECORDS:

    Employee drug and alcohol test results and related records shall be maintained under strict confidentiality. Upon written request, an employee shall receive copies of any records pertaining to his/her use of drugs or alcohol, including any records pertaining to his/her drug or alcohol tests. Records shall be made available to subsequent employers or other identified persons only as expressly requested in writing by the employee.

    City of Poquoson employees who are required to possess a commercial driver’s license as part of their job duties and all other employees of the City of Poquoson (“the City”) who perform “safety-sensitive functions” (“‘covered employees”) shall be subject to a drug and alcohol testing program that fulfills the requirements of Title 49, Part 382 of the Code of Federal Regulations. Testing procedures and facilities used for such tests shall conform with the requirements of Title 49, Section 40, et seq. of the Code of Federal Regulations.

    Pre-Employment Drug Tests

    A drug test shall be conducted prior to an applicant for employment as a covered employee being hired to perform any safety-sensitive function for the City. The definition of “safety-sensitive function” includes all on-duty functions performed from the time an employee begins work or is required to be ready to work until he/she is relieved from work and all responsibility for performing work. It includes driving; waiting to be dispatched; inspecting and servicing equipment; supervising, performing or assisting in loading and unloading; repairing or obtaining and waiting for help with a disabled vehicle; performing driver requirements related to accidents; and performing any other work for the City.

    The drug test shall be required of a job applicant only after he/she has been offered employment, however, as part of the job application process, all applicants shall execute releases and consent forms which authorize the City to obtain the results of such testing. All applicants shall also be required to execute releases and consent forms which authorize the City to contact all employers whom the applicant has worked for during the prior two years in order to obtain all records pertaining to the applicant’s drug and alcohol use during that time, including any records regarding the applicant’s refusal to submit to testing for drug and alcohol use. Any offer of employment will be withdrawn from an applicant who refuses to submit to the required pre-employment drug test or who tests positive or drugs in such test.

    Post-Accident Tests

    Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted as soon after a vehicular accident as practicable on any employee:

    1. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle involved in the accident if the accident involved loss of human life; or

    2. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle involved in the accident if the accident resulted in bodily injury to a person who, as a result of the injury, immediately received medical treatment away from the accident scene; or

    3. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle involved in the accident if the accident caused disabling damage to one or more motor vehicles which required the vehicle(s) to be transported away from the scene of the accident by a tow truck or similar vehicle and the employee received a citation under state or local law for a moving traffic violation arising from the accident.

    The term “accident” shall not include occurrences which: 1. Involve only boarding and alighting from a stationary motor vehicle; or 2. involve only the loading or unloading of cargo.

    In the absence of the need for immediate medical treatment following an accident, all covered employees shall make themselves readily available for the required testing. Any covered employee involved in a motor vehicle accident which requires post-accident testing pursuant to this policy shall not use alcohol for eight hours after the accident, or until he/she undergoes a post-accident alcohol test, whichever occurs first. Employees will not be tested, however, unless the test for alcohol can be administered within eight hours after the accident and the test for drugs can be administered within thirty-two hours. If an alcohol test is not administered to a covered employee within two hours after the accident or if a drug test is not administered within thirty-two hours, the City shall prepare and maintain records explaining why such tests were not conducted. Tests conducted by authorized federal, state or local officials shall fulfill post-accident testing requirements provided such tests conform to applicable legal requirements and the results of such tests are obtained by the City in a timely manner. Breath tests will only be used to determine the presence or absence of alcohol and will! not constitute a test for the presence or abuse of drugs.

    Random Tests

    Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted on covered employees on a random basis at unannounced times throughout the year. Tests for alcohol shall be conducted just before, during or just after the performance of safety-sensitive functions. The number of random alcohol tests annually shall equal at least 25% of the average number of employee positions. The number of random drug tests annually shall equal at least 50% of the average number of covered employee positions. Covered employees shall be selected by

    a scientifically valid random process and each employee shall have an equal chance of being tested each time selections are made.

    Reasonable Suspicion Tests

    Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted when a supervisor or City official, trained in accordance with the requirements of Section 382.201 et seq of Title 49 of the Code of Federal Regulations has reasonable suspicion that a covered employee has violated the City’s alcohol or drug prohibitions. “Reasonable suspicion” shall be based on specific, contemporaneous, articulable observations concerning the employee’s appearance, behavior, speech or body odors. Such observations may include indications of the chronic use and withdrawal effects of controlled substances. Alcohol tests are authorized for reasonable suspicion only if the required observations are made during, just before or just after the period of the workday when the covered employee must comply with alcohol prohibitions. An alcohol test may not be conducted by the person who determines that reasonable suspicion exists to conduct such a test. If an alcohol test is not administered within two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion, the City shall prepare and maintain a record explaining why this was not done. No attempt to conduct alcohol tests shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of eight hours after the determination of reasonable suspicion.

    A supervisor or City official who makes observations leading to a reasonable suspicion test for drugs shall make a written record of his/her observations within twenty-four hours of the observed behavior or before the results of the drug test are released, whichever is earlier.

    Enforcement

    Any covered employee who refuses to submit to a post-accident, random, reasonable suspicion, return to duty or follow-up tests shall not perform or continue to perform safety-sensitive functions and will be subject to discipline up to, and including, dismissal from employment. Covered employees who test positive for alcohol or drugs shall be subject to disciplinary action up to, and including, dismissal from employment. Covered employees who test positive for an alcohol concentration of 0.02 or greater but less than 0.04 shall not be allowed to perform safety-sensitive functions for twenty-four hours following administration of the test. At a minimum, a covered employee will be suspended from work without pay for that time period.

    Referral, Evaluation and Treatment

    A covered employee who violates the City’s prohibitions related to drugs and alcohol shall receive from the City the names, addresses and telephone numbers of substance abuse professionals and counseling and treatment programs available to evaluate and resolve drug and alcohol-related problems. Such information shall be provided even if the employee is terminated from employment with the city. If an employee is not terminated after a violation of this policy, the employee shall be evaluated by a substance

    abuse professional who shall determine what help, if any, the employee needs in resolving his/her problem. Any substance abuse professional who determines that an employee needs assistance shall not refer the employee to a private practice, person or organization from which the substance abuse professional receives remuneration or in which he/she has a financial interest. Any covered employee identified as needing help in resolving a drug or alcohol problem shal! be evaluated by a substance abuse professional to determine that he/she has properly followed the prescribed rehabilitation program and shall be subject to unannounced follow-up tests after returning to duty.

    Return-to-Duty Tests

    A drug or alcohol test shall be conducted if a covered employee who has violated the City’s drug or alcohol prohibition returns to performing safety-sensitive duties. Employees whose conduct involved alcohol will not be allowed to return to duty in a safety-sensitive function until the return-to-duty alcohol test produces a verified result that meets all federal, state and local standards. Covered employees whose conduct involved drugs shall not be allowed to return to duty in a safety-sensitive function until the return-to-duty drug test produces a verified, negative result.

    Follow-up Tests

    A covered employee who violates the City’s drug or alcohol prohibition and is subsequently identified by a substance abuse professional as needing assistance in resolving a drug or alcohol problem shall be subject to unannounced, follow-up testing as directed by the substance abuse professional in accordance with the provisions of Section 382.605 of the Code of Federal Regulations. Follow-up alcohol testing shall be conducted just before, during or just after the time when the covered employee is performing safety-sensitive functions.

    Employee Request To Retest Urine Specimen

    Urine specimens collected from covered employees shall be split into two specimens, a “primary” and a “split” specimen. If the analysis of the primary specimen confirms the presence of illegal, controlled substances, the employee will have seventy-two hours to request the split specimen be sent to another approved and certified laboratory for testing. The employee shall pay the cost of the additional test and all handling and shipping costs associated therewith. Any costs associated with such split sample testing which are not paid by an employee who tests “positive” for alcohol or drugs shall be deducted from any funds or pay due to the employee.

    Records Employee drug and alcohol test results and records shall be maintained under strict

    confidentiality and released only in accordance with Section 382.401 et seq of the Code of Federal Regulations and other applicable legal requirements. Upon written request, a

    covered employee shall receive copies of any records pertaining to his/her use of drugs or alcohol, including any records pertaining to his/her drug or alcohol tests. Records shall be made available to a subsequent employer or other identified persons only as expressly requested in writing by the employee.

    Notifications

    Each covered employee shall receive educational materials that explain the requirements of Title 49, part 382 of the Code of Federal Regulations, together with a copy of the City’s policy and regulations for meeting these requirements. Any representatives of employee organizations shall be notified of the availability of this information. Covered employees shall also receive information about legal requirements, City policies and disciplinary consequences related to the use of alcohol and drugs. Each Covered employee shall sign a statement certifying that he/she has received a copy of such materials. Before drug and alcohol tests are performed, the City shall inform drivers that the tests are given pursuant to Title 49, part 382 of the Code of Federal Regulations. This notice shall be provided only after the compliance date specified in law.

    The City shall notify a covered employee of the results of a pre-employment drug test if the employee requests such results within six calendar days of being notified of the disposition of his/her employment application. The City shall notify a covered employee of the results of random, reasonable suspicion and post-accident drug tests if the test results are verified positive. The City shall also notify the employee which controlled substance(s) were verified as positive.

    Covered employees shall inform their supervisors if at any time they are using a controlled substance which their physician has prescribed for therapeutic purposes. Such a substance may be uscd only if the prescribing physician certifies in writing that use of the medication will not adversely affect the employee’s ability to safely operate a vehicle and perform safety-sensitive functions.

    Before any covered employee operates a commercial motor vehicle, the City shall provide him/her with post-accident procedures that will make it possible to comply with post-accident testing requirements.

    Consumption of Alcohol While on Duty

    Any on-the-job consumption of alcohol, without regard to whether it exceeds the tolerance levels prescribed by this policy will not be tolerated and will subject the employee to discipline up to, and including, dismissal from employment.

    involved in the accident if the accident resulted in bodily injury to a person who, as a result of the injury, immediately received medical treatment away from the accident scene; or

    1. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle involved in the accident if the accident caused disabling damage to one or more motor vehicles which required the vehicle(s) to be transported away from the scene of the accident by a tow truck or similar vehicle and the employee received a citation under state or local law for a moving traffic violation arising from the accident.

    The term “accident” shall not include occurrences which: 1. Involve only boarding and alighting from a stationary motor vehicle; or 2. involve only the loading or unloading of cargo.

    In the absence of the need for immediate medical treatment following an accident, all covered employees shall make themsclves readily available for the required testing. Any covered employee involved in a motor vehicle accident which requires post-accident testing pursuant to this policy shall not use alcohol for eight hours after the accident, or until he/she undergoes a post-accident alcohol test, whichever occurs first. Employees will not be tested, however, unless the test for alcohol can be administered within eight hours after the accident and the test for drugs can be administered within thirty-two hours. If an alcohol test is not administered to a covered employee within two hours after the accident or if a drug test is not administered within thirty-two hours, the City shall prepare and maintain records explaining why such tests were not conducted. Tests conducted by authorized federal, state or local officials shall fulfill post-accident testing requirements provided such tests conform to applicable legal requirements and the results of such tests are obtained by the City in a timely manner. Breath tests will only be used to determine the presence or absence of alcohol and will not constitute a test for the presence or abuse of drugs.

    Random Tests

    Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted on covered employees on a random basis at unannounced times throughout the year. Tests for alcohol shall be conducted just before, during or just after the performance of safety-sensitive functions. The number of random alcohol tests annually shall equal at least 25% of the average number of employee positions. The number of random drug tests annually shall equal at least 50% of the average number of covered employee positions. Covered employees shall be selected by a scientifically valid random process and each employee shall have an equal chance of being tested each time selections are made.

    Reasonable Suspicion Tests

    Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted when a supervisor or City official, trained in accordance with the requirements of Section 382.201 et seq of Title 49 of the Code of Federal Regulations has reasonable suspicion that a covered employee has violated the City’s alcohol or drug prohibitions. “Reasonable suspicion” shall be based on specific, contemporaneous, articulable observations concerning the employee’s appearance, behavior, speech or body odors. Such observations may include indications of the chronic use and withdrawal effects of controlled substances. Alcohol tests are authorized for reasonable suspicion only if the required observations are made during, just before or just after the period of the workday when the covered employee must comply with alcohol prohibitions. An alcohol test may not be conducted by the person who determines that reasonable suspicion exists to conduct such a test. If an alcohol test is not administered within two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion, the City shall prepare and maintain a record explaining why this was not done. No attempt to conduct alcohol tests shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of eight hours after the determination of reasonable suspicion.

    A supervisor or City official who makes observations leading to a reasonable suspicion test for drugs shall make a written record of his/her observations within twenty-four hours of the observed behavior or before the results of the drug test are released, whichever is earlier.

    Enforcement

    Any covered employee who refuses to submit to a post-accident, random, reasonable suspicion, return to duty or follow-up tests shall not perform or continue to perform safety-sensitive functions and will be subject to discipline up to, and including, dismissal from employment. Covered employees who test positive for alcohol or drugs shall be subject to disciplinary action up to, and including, dismissal from employment. Covered employees who test positive for an alcohol concentration of 0.02 or greater but less than 0.04 shall not be allowed to perform safety-sensitive functions for twenty-four hours following administration of the test. At a minimum, a covered employee will be suspended from work without pay for that time period.

    Referral, Evaluation and Treatment

    A covered employee who violates the City’s prohibitions related to drugs and alcohol shall receive from the City the names, addresses and telephone numbers of substance abuse professionals and counseling and treatment programs available to evaluate and resolve drug and alcohol-related problems. Such information shall be provided even if the employee is terminated from employment with the city. If an employee is not terminated after a violation of this policy, the employee shall be evaluated by a substance abuse professional who shall determine what help, if any, the employee needs in resolving his/her problem. Any substance abuse professional who determines that an employee needs assistance shall not refer the employee to a private practice, person or

    organization from which the substance abuse professional receives remuneration or in which he/she has a financial interest. Any covered employee identified as needing help in resolving a drug or alcohol problem shall be evaluated by a substance abuse professional to determine that he/she has properly followed the prescribed rehabilitation program and shall be subject to unannounced follow-up tests after returning to duty.

    Return-to-Duty Tests

    A drug or alcohol test shall be conducted if a covered employee who has violated the City’s drug or alcohol prohibition returns to performing safety-sensitive duties. Employees whose conduct involved alcohol will not be allowed to return to duty in a safety-sensitive function until the return-to-duty alcohol test produces a verified result that meets all federal, state and local standards. Covered employees whose conduct involved drugs shall not be allowed to return to duty in a safety-sensitive function until the return-to-duty drug test produces a verified, negative result.

    Follow-up Tests

    A covered employee who violates the City’s drug or alcohol prohibition and is subsequently identified by a substance abuse professional as needing assistance in resolving a drug or alcohol problem shall be subject to unannounced, follow-up testing as directed by the substance abuse professional in accordance with the provisions of Section 382.605 of the Code of Federal Regulations. Follow-up alcohol testing shall be conducted just before, during or just after the time when the covered employee is performing safety-sensitive functions.

    Employee Request To Retest Urine Specimen

    Urine specimens collected from covered employees shall be split into two specimens, a “primary” and a “split” specimen. If the analysis of the primary specimen confirms the presence of illegal, controlled substances, the employee will have seventy-two hours to request the split specimen be sent to another approved and certified laboratory for testing. The employee shall pay the cost of the additional test and all handling and shipping costs associated therewith. Any costs associated with such split sample testing which are not paid by an employee who tests “positive” for alcohol or drugs shall be deducted from any funds or pay due to the employee.

    Records

    Employee drug and alcohol test results and records shal! be maintained under strict confidentiality and released only in accordance with Section 382.401 et seq of the Code of Federal Regulations and other applicable legal requirements. Upon written request, a covered employee shall receive copies of any records pertaining to his/her use of drugs or alcohol, including any records pertaining to his/her drug or alcohol tests. Records shall be made available to a subsequent employer or other identified persons only as expressly requested in writing by the employee.

    Notifications

    Each covered employee shall receive educational materials that explain the requirements of Title 49, part 382 of the Code of Federal Regulations, together with a copy of the City’s policy and regulations for meeting these requirements. Any representatives of employee organizations shall be notified of the availability of this information. Covered employees shall also receive information about legal requirements, City policies and disciplinary consequences related to the use of alcohol and drugs. Each Covered employee shall sign a statement certifying that he/she has received a copy of such materials. Before drug and alcohol tests are performed, the City shall inform drivers that the tests are given pursuant to Title 49, part 382 of the Code of Federal Regulations. This notice shall be provided only after the compliance date specified in law.

    The City shall notify a covered employee of the results of a pre-employment drug test if the employee requests such results within six calendar days of being notified of the disposition of his/her employment application. The City shall notify a covered employee of the results of random, reasonable suspicion and post-accident drug tests if the test results are verified positive. The City shall also notify the employee which controlled substance(s) were verified as positive.

    Covered employees shall inform their supervisors if at any time they are using a controlled substance which their physician has prescribed for therapeutic purposes. Such a substance may be used only if the prescribing physician certifies in writing that use of the medication will not adversely affect the employee’s ability to safely operate a vehicle and perform safety-sensitive functions.

    Before any covered employee operates a commercial motor vehicle, the City shall provide hinv/her with post-accident procedures that will make it possible to comply with post-accident testing requirements.

    Consumption of Alcohol While on Duty

    Any on-the-job consumption of alcohol, without regard to whether it exceeds the tolerance levels prescribed by this policy will not be tolerated and will subject the employee to discipline up to, and including, dismissal from employment.

    City Reservation of Rights

    The City expressly reserves the right to modify this policy from time to time as needed to comply with the law and the management of the policy. If any conflict arises between any part of this policy and federal, state or local requirements, the City will conform to the applicable laws. All other provisions of the policy, which are not in conflict, however, will remain effective.

The following policies govern administration of this plan. Any policies not specifically addressed here will be as delineated in the policy by the Administrator of the City’s Health Insurance Plan.

    Retirees Under 65

    1. Eligibility

    In order for the retired employee to be eligible for health insurance coverage he/she must have a combination of years of service with the City of Poquoson and age which add up to seventy. For example, an employee at the age 50 must have 20 years of service, at age 55 must have 15 years of service, and at age 60 must have 10 years of service. Retired employees in all cases must have a minimum of 5 years of service to become eligible for post retirement health benefits. When the retiree turns 65, the type of coverage and employee contribution for retirees over 65 become effective.

    1. Type of Coverage

    The retired employee may continue enrollment in the health insurance plan offered by the City under which he/she is covered at the time of retirement.

    Ie

    Employee Contribution

    The retired employee pays the full amount of the retiree premium

    Enhanced Retiree Health Insurance Benefit 1. Eligibility

    On or after July 1, 2017 the retiring employee must have a minimum of twenty (20) years of creditable VRS service with the City of Poquoson and a minimum of 90 days of unused sick leave at the time of retirement from City service. Further, the retiring employee must give the employer a minimum of six (6) months’ written notice of their intention to retire and must have participated in the City health care plan for a minimum of five (5) years immediately prior to retirement.

    1. Employer Contribution

    The City will contribute fifty (50) percent of the employee health insurance

    premium for retirees wishing to continue coverage under the City health care plan up to age 65. The City’s fifty (50) percent contribution will be reduced by the amount of any employer-paid health insurance credit that the retiree may qualify for under the VRS program.

    Retirees Over 65 1. Eligibility

    The retired employee must be age 65 or older or otherwise eligible for Medicare. Retired employees in all cases must have a minimum of 5 years of service to become eligible for post retirement health benefits. The City has clected to participate in the VRS Health Insurance Credit Program. For more information contact the Finance Department.

    1. Type of Coverage

    The employee may only enroll in the Medicare Complementary Plan or an HMO Medicare Plan offered by the administrator of the City’s Plan.

    1. Employee Contribution

    The retired employee pays the full amount of the retiree premium.

    Retirees on Work Related Disability 1. Eligibility

    The retired employee must be retired on a job related disability as defined by the Virginia Retirement System and be eligible for workers’ compensation for the job related disability. The retired employee must be totally disabled. Ifa retired disabled employee works in another job where eligible for group medical benefits, then eligibility in the City plan will cease. Retired disabled employees in all cases must have a minimum of 5 years of service to become eligible for post retirement health benefits. When the retiree turns 65, the type of coverage and employee contribution for retirees over 65 will become effective.

    1. Type of Coverage

    The retired disabled employee may only enroll in an HMO plan offered by the administrator of the City’s plan.

    1. Employee Contribution

    The City will contribute towards health insurance premiums on a graduated scale depending on years of service as follows:

    20 plus years - 80% 15-19 years - 70% 10-14 years - 60% 5-9 years - 50%

    1. Negotiations

    The City Manager may negotiate with the retired disabled employee to supplement the employee’s payment to another health insurance plan in lieu of paying the City’s share of premiums if it is beneficial for the City to do so.

    Premium Payments

    Retirees will pay by cash, check or money order prior to the first of the month of coverage. Retirees who fail to pay their premium by the last day of the month preceding the first of their coverage month will be given a five-calendar day grace period. If the

    premium is not paid by then they will be notified by certified mail of the cancellation of their medical benefits.

    Enrollment in Plan

    The only opportunity for the employee to enroll in the plan is upon retirement, with one exception. The exception to this rule is the retiree who is enrolled in a spouse’s active employee health benefits membership through the City of Poquoson. When the active employee terminates or retires, the retired spouse may elect to come into the Retiree Group within 31 days of the event. Retiring employees who do not wish to enroll in the City’s Retiree Health Insurance Plan should sign the waiver portion of the Enrollment Form.

    Employees must submit an application for retiree health insurance coverage within 31 days of the retirement application or notice.

    Employees who terminate from active service and defer retirement will not be eligible for the City’s Retiree Health Insurance Plan.

    A retiree may change plans at retirement, during an open enrollment period or at the time of an eligibility status change. However, an employee may change plans upon becoming eligible for Medicare.

    Coverage of Dependents

    An employee may apply for employee plus one or family membership at retirement, whether or not the eligible family members were covered in the active employee group while the retiree was actively employed. A retiree may change their type of coverage upon enrollment or during any open enrollment period. The retired employee will pay for the cost of the premium for family coverage, except in the case of the job related disabled employce where the City will contribute towards health insurance premiums on a graduated scale depending on years of service.
