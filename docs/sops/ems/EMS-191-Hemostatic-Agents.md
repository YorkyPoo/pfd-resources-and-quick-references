IV. INDICATIONS

Hemostatic agents can be used to control exsanguinating

hemorrhage when use of direct pressure and tourniquets fail. This is
most likely to involve wounds of the axilla, groin, neck, face, or scalp.

V. CONTRAINDICATIONS

Minor bleeding

Bleeding that can be controlled by direct pressure.

Bleeding that can be controlled by application of a tourniquet.
Open abdominal or chest wounds.
