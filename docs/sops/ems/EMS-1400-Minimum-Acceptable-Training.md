EMS OPERATIONS

Minimum Acceptable Training

I. PURPOSE

The purpose of this policy is to provide the
minimum acceptable training for a career
or volunteer member to ride on an OEMS
licensed transport vehicle.

This SOP is not all-inclusive and cannot
encompass all situations that may be
encountered.

Il. APPLICABILITY

All Career and Volunteer Staff

IHW. POLICY

It shall be the policy of Poquoson
Fire/Rescue Department to require a
minimum level of training for any persons
wishing to ride on an OEMS licensed
transport vehicle as a member of either
the Career or Volunteer organizations.



Standard Operating Procedures Manual

EMS 14.0 Minimum Acceptable Training

The minimum acceptable training for any members wishing to
ride on an OEMS licensed transport vehicle shall be as follows:

Driver/Operator:

e BLS for the Healthcare Provider

e Documented training in Medical/Legal (to include HIPAA and
confidentiality) and Infection Control/Bloodborne Pathogens

e VAOEMS approved Emergency Vehicle Operator Course or
Department of Fire Programs S-2 Certification

e Approved to drive medic units by the Deputy Chief per
department training policy

e Amember serving as Driver/Operator who does not
meet one of the below categories should NOT be
involved in patient care.

Student:

e BLS for the Healthcare Provider

e Documented training in Medical/Legal (to include HIPPA and
confidentiality) and Infection Control/Bloodborne Pathogens

e Must be enrolled in a VAOEMS approved Emergency Medical
Technician (EMT) course

e May only perform under the direct supervision ofa
released provider.

Certified Provider:
e BLS for the Healthcare Provider
e Documented training in Infection Control/Bloodborne
Pathogens
e Current VAOEMS or Department of Fire Programs S-2
certification, and EMT, EMT-Enhanced, Advanced EMT,
Intermediate, or Paramedic

It shall be the responsibility of the Officer in Charge or the
Attendant in Charge to ensure that any persons on an OEMS licensed
transport vehicle meet these minimum requirements.
