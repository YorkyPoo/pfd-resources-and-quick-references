PAGING-DAY/NIGHT SWITCH

I. PURPOSE

To provide for unnecessary and
uninterrupted sleep for fire personnel on
duty by separating out page alerts and
radio traffic by stations for the night time.

This SOP is not all-inclusive and cannot
encompass all situations that may be
encountered.

Il. POLICY/POSITION

Reducing firefighter sleep deprivation is a
national initiative. Utilizing the station
paging allows for staff at the unneeded
station to continue to rest and be ready for
the next call for service.

Proper paging procedures are paramount
for crew resource management.

II. RESPONSIBILITIES

e Shift Lieutenant or Acting Officer at Station 2 will be
responsible for making sure switch is in proper mode for
Station 2.

e The Battalion Chief or Master Firefighter at Station 1 will be
responsible for ensuring the day/night switch is in proper mode
for Station 1. If the Master Firefighter and Battalion Chief are
not present, the most Senior Firefighter or Acting Officer will be
responsible for this duty.

e The Battalion Chief or Acting Battalion Chief will monitor the
F.D. frequency while in “Night Mode.”

IV. PROCEDURES
1. Switch will be set to “Day” mode at 0700 every morning for

radio check.
2. Switch may be set to “Night” mode after 2100.
