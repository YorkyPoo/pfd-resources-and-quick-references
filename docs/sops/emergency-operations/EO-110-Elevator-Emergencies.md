Elevator Emergencies

I. PURPOSE

To provide safe guidelines, and operational
procedures used at EMERGENCIES and
INCIDENTS in which people are trapped in
stalled elevators

This SOP is not all-inclusive and cannot
encompass all situations that may be
encountered.

II. POLICY/POSITION

This policy applies to all uniformed and
investigatory personnel of the Poquoson Fire
Department.

III. RESPONSIBILITIES

All members of the PFD, when dealing with a
stalled elevator, are limited to the safe removal of
persons trapped in the elevator car or hoistway.
Repairs to and reactivation of elevators
shall never be carried out by members of
the PFD.

IV. PROCEDURES

Response: First Due Company

Stalled elevator operations are divided into two categories;
INCIDENTS and EMERGENCIES.

INCIDENT:

Astalled elevator with passengers trapped inside, not in immediate danger and
no evidence of injury. They are merely inconvenienced

Note: Conditions must be constantly monitored; an INCIDENT may escalate to
an EMERGENCY.

EMERGENCY:
A situation where one or more of the following exist:
e Fire endangering passengers in a stalled elevator.
e Passengers who are sick, ill or injured in a stalled elevator.
e Passengers who are in panic, in a stalled elevator.
Important:
Wait for an elevator mechanic to arrive if possible (20-minute maximum

wait-time). The following situations will prohibit PFD members in waiting for
an elevator mechanic:

e Emergency situation (see above)

e Availability & response time of elevator mechanic.
e PFD apparatus availability & call volume.

Operating Procedures:

At a minimum, all PFD members shall follow these important safe procedures at
all stalled elevator operations:
e Initial Response Steps (see below) shall be initiated upon arrival of stalled
elevator.
e The power to the stalled elevator shall be removed along with all battery
and generator back-ups (using lock-out/tag-out procedures) from all

Initial Response Steps:

1. Upon arrival the Incident Commander shall meet with the complainant, if
possible, to determine if there is a person trapped in the stalled elevator and
how many persons are affected.

2. Upon the determination that a rescue is necessary, the Incident Commander
shall request an elevator repair service to respond. The IC shall consider
requesting a technical rescue team.

3. Obtain Knox Box key. The Knox Box shall contain, the machine room key,
hoistway door key, and the fireman service key.

4. Two firefighters, when staffing permits, shall be sent to the elevator machine

room equipped with keys for the room (if available), hand lights, forcible
entry tools, radios, extinguisher, lock-out/tag-out kit. The machine room may

Occupant Removal

The safest way to remove a passenger from a stalled elevator is through the
normal entranceway and floor level. Sometimes this is not always possible.
Whenever possible remove trapped occupant(s) from the floor above (minimizes
some of the falling hazard). If the opening through the normal entranceway has
less than 3 feet of clearance, remove occupant(s) via the top escape hatch. Never
have a victim slide out through a narrow opening, the results may be
deadly.

Safe order of removal:

1. Floor level through the normal entranceway (safest)

2. Floor above through the normal entranceway

3. Floor below through the normal entranceway (barricade opening to shaft)
4. Top escape hatch (must use fall arrest system)

PASSENGERS VIA THE TOP ESCAPE HATCH. THIS MUST BE CONSIDERED
A TECHNICAL RESCUE AND APPROPRIATE MUTUAL AID RESOURCES

be disconnected along with all battery and generator back-ups. Lock-out/tag-out
procedures shall be implemented. Confirmation shall be received before further

Incident Termination:

After occupant(s) have been safely removed from the stalled elevator car, the
following procedures shall be followed:

1. PFD members shall secure the hoistway door. An unsecured hoistway door is
one of the most common causes of elevator fatalities.

2. Power to all elevators that were used during the rescue operation shall be

restored.

3. Power to the stalled elevator shall not be restored by PFD members. Lock-

out/tag-out shall remain in place.

4. The key for the lock-out/tag-out shall be placed in the building’s Knox Box.

5. The elevator company and/or building management shall be briefed on what

actions were taken to free the occupant(s). They shall also be told what power was

turned off, stop switches were thrown and if any damage was made to the

elevator(s).
