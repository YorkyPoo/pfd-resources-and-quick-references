SHIFT TRADES

I. PURPOSE

To establish guidelines for shift trading
between career Fire/Rescue personnel.

This SOP is not all-inclusive and cannot
encompass all situations that may be
encountered.

II. POLICY/POSITION

Because of staffing requirements and
personnel shortages, this Department
recognizes the need/merit for shift
trading.

It is up to the individuals trading shifts to
honor these agreements and to protect
this privilege.

Ill. RESPONSIBILITIES

e Personnel are responsible for
following the procedures as
contained within this directive.

e Shift trading shall not impair the
operational effectiveness, nor create
a logistical or financial burden to the
department.

e Shift trading is an agreement solely between the trading
individuals and is allowed for their convenience.

e The employee agreeing to work shall accept all responsibilities
and obligations of the employee who is scheduled to work.

e The trading individuals are responsible for tracking traded
time. The department assumes no responsibility for tracking
hours owed to individuals.

e As required under [air Labor Standards Act (FLSA) provisions,
shift/time trades shall be “paid back” within 12 months of the
traded time.

e The trading of shifts may result in a work period that exceeds
that which is allowed by FLSA. For your convenience, however,
the City of Poquoson will be relieved of any and all overtime
payment for on-duty time in excess of the allowed work period.

e The shift will be supervised by either one of the two officers or
the next senior person assigned to the shift. Shift trades cannot
and will not be allowed to disrupt this chain of command.

e The trade shall be limited to individuals of equivalent positions
and competency (i.e. Firefighter/medic — Firefighter/medic,
Officer — Officer, etc).

e Any employee with an approved exchange of duty (shift trade)
shall not be relieved from duty until his/her teplagement has
reported to duty.

e There will be no third party involvement.

IV. PROCEDURES

The individuals requesting the shift trade shall complete the
departments Shift Trade Request form and have it signed by the
second party agreeing to be part of the shift trade. The individual
requesting the shift trade shall then have this form signed by their
shift commander. All forms shall be maintained in the Battalion
Chiefs office and are available for copy upon request. Once the trade
time has been repaid, the official form may be destroyed.
