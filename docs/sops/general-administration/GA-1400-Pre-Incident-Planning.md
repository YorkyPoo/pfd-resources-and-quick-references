III. RESPONSIBILITIES

The Shift Supervisor, or designee, will be responsible to ensure
that all Pre-Incident Planning for the month is carried out. The
monthly schedule will be issued by the Fire Prevention Officer.

The Shift Inspector will be responsible for working with
business owners to ensure that both Inspections and Pre-Incident
Planning has a minimal impact on business operations. All Pre-
Incident Planning should be done at the convenience of the business
owner.

The Fire Prevention Officer is ultimately responsible for data
entry of all Pre-Incident paperwork.

IV. POLICY

Pre-Incident Planning shall be conducted by personnel on duty
and shall be conducted as a company.

The station that is in the jurisdiction of the occupancy to be
planned shall be the Engine Company responsible for the planning.

A completed Pre-Incident Plan will include the approved Pre-
Incident Planning form as well as a diagram or layout of the building
with the appropriate information included per NFPA 1620.

An Inspector DOES NOT have to be present for Pre-Incident

Planning to take place. The Pre-Incident Planning process should be
separate from an official Fire Inspection.
