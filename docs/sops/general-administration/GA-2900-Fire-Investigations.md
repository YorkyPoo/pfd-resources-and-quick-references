I. PURPOSE

This SOP ensures a systematic approach
to engaging Fire Investigators, clarifies
expectations for all personnel involved,
and emphasizes collaboration with law
enforcement when required. Adherence to
NFPA 921 standards ensures the integrity
and quality of fire investigations

II. POLICY

1. Notification Process:
a. Incident Commanders (IC) will assess the need for a fire
investigation based on the severity and nature of the incident.
* All working structure fires
* Fires with significant property damage.
* Fires with a potential for serious injury or loss of life.
* Fires with suspicious or unknown origins.
* Fires with potential criminal activity.

b. Ifa fire investigation is deemed necessary, the Incident
Commander will directly request the Fire Investigator through the
designated communication channel (radio, phone, or other
established means).

2. Expectations for Incident Commanders:
a. Clearly communicate the need for a Fire Investigator based on the
severity of the incident and potential for complex investigation.

b. Incident commanders should not delay notification if an
Investigator is needed, recognizing the importance of time-sensitive
evidence.

c. Provide relevant information to the Fire Investigator, including
incident location, nature of the fire, and any initial observations,
witness statements, and initial assessments.

d. ICs shall secure the fire scene and limit access to authorized
personnel only.

e. Preserve the integrity of potential evidence until the Investigator
arrives.

f. Notify relevant personnel of the impending investigation and
emphasize the importance of cooperation.

3. Expectations for Fire Personnel:
a. Recognize the indicators that may necessitate a fire investigation.

b. Preserve the fire scene integrity until the Fire Investigator arrives.
c. Cooperate fully with the Investigator upon their arrival.

d. Assist the Fire Investigator upon arrival, providing necessary
support for evidence collection and scene documentation.

4. Fire Investigator Initial Actions:

a. Upon notification, the Fire Investigator shall promptly respond to
the incident scene or designated meeting point.

b. Upon arrival, the Fire Investigator shall coordinate with the IC and
other relevant personnel to assess the scene and initiate the
investigation.

5. Collaboration with Law Enforcement:

a. If the investigation indicates potential criminal activity, the Fire
Investigator shall collaborate with law enforcement agencies as
necessary.

b. Share information and coordinate investigative efforts to ensure a
comprehensive approach to the case.

c. Ensure compliance with legal requirements and maintain the chain
of custody for potential evidence.

6. NFPA 921 Standard Compliance:
a. All fire investigations will be conducted in strict accordance with
the most current edition of NFPA 921 standard.

b. Investigators are responsible for staying informed about updates to
NFPA 921 and adjusting procedures accordingly.

7. Documentation and Reporting:

MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING

BETWEEN THE POQUOSON FIRE DEPARTMENT AND POQUOSON POLICE
DEPARTMENT FOR FIRE/EXPLOSIVES INVESTIGATIONS

This Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is entered into by and between the Poquoson Fire
Department (hereinafter referred to as "PFD") and the Poquoson Police Department (hereinafter referred
to as "PPD")

1. PURPOSE:

The purpose of this MOU is to establish guidelines and procedures for collaboration between the PFD
and PPD in the conduct of fire investigations within the jurisdiction of the City of Poquoson. This
collaboration aims to enhance public safety, promote efficient use of resources, and facilitate a
coordinated response to incidents involving fire or explosion.

2. DEFINITIONS:

a. Fire Investigation: The systematic process of determining the origin, cause, and circumstances of a
fire.

b. Lead Agency: The agency primarily responsible for conducting the fire investigation.

c. Supporting Agency: The agency that provides assistance and resources to the lead agency during a
fire investigation.

3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
a. Poquoson Fire Department (PFD):
i. Conduct initial fire scene assessments and determine the need for a formal investigation.
ii. Serve as the lead agency in fire investigations, unless otherwise agreed upon.
iii. Collect and preserve evidence related to the fire incident.
iv. Coordinate with PPD to ensure a seamless exchange of information.
b. Poquoson Police Department (PPD):
i. Provide timely and necessary assistance to the PFD in fire investigations.
ii. Assist in securing the fire scene and managing crowds if necessary.
iii. Conduct joint interviews with potential witnesses as needed.

iv. Collect and preserve evidence related to the fire incident.

v. Execute search warrants as needed.
vi. Collaborate with the PFD to share relevant information and findings.

vii. Assist in developing case files and moving cases to the Commonwealth Attorney’s office when
appropriate

4. COORDINATION AND COMMUNICATION:

a. Regular Meetings: Representatives from PFD and PPD shall meet regularly to discuss ongoing and
potential fire investigations, share updates, and address any challenges.

b. Information Sharing: Both parties agree to share relevant information related to fire investigations in a
timely and transparent manner.

5. TRAINING:

a. PFD and PPD may organize joint training sessions to enhance the skills and knowledge of their
personnel in fire investigation and related areas.

b. Both departments will share relevant training resources, materials, and expertise to improve the
overall competence of personnel involved in fire investigations.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY:
a. Both parties recognize the sensitive nature of information involved in fire investigations.

b. Information shared between PFD and PPD shall be treated as confidential and used solely for
investigative purposes.

7. TERMINATION:
Either party may terminate this MOU with a written notice of 30 days. Termination will not affect

ongoing investigations, and both parties will continue to cooperate until the completion of such
investigations.

This MOU shall become effective upon the signature of authorized representatives from both the
Poquoson Fire Department and Poquoson Police Department.
