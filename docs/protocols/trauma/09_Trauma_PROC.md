# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Trauma

Guidelines for the rapid assessment and management of suspected traumatic injuries, emphasizing hemorrhage control, airway management, and rapid transport to a trauma center.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Mechanism of Injury (MOI)
    - Suspected traumatic injury

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Hemorrhage
    - Shock
    - Altered mental status

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Calculate GCS (3-15)
    - Calculate Shock Index (HR/SBP)

### Pearls
• ⚠ Do not delay transport to establish IV lines or wait for ALS.
• ⚠ Shock Index > 1.0 suggests high risk of post-intubation hypotension/arrest - resuscitate first.
• Shock Index = Heart Rate / Systolic Blood Pressure. Higher shock indices correlate with increased risk of post-intubation hypotension and cardiac arrest.
• Glasgow Coma Score (GCS) ranges from 3 (Minimum) to 15 (Maximum).
