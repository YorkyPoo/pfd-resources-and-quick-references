# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Administrative - Communications

This protocol outlines the procedures for effectively communicating with Medical Control and receiving facilities using the HEAR and COR systems. It establishes standardized reporting criteria for pre-arrival notifications and clear contingencies for communications failure or Mass-Casualty Incidents (MCI).

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - **HEAR Criteria**: BLS/ALS patients NOT requiring on-line physician intervention.
    - **COR Criteria**: Need for physician consultation, medication authorizations, or Stroke/Trauma/STEMI alerts.

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - **Comm Failure**: Unable to contact medical control after 4 attempts (2 via radio, 2 via cell phone).
    - **MCI**: Mass-casualty or disaster incidents.

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Pre-arrival report needs
    - Unstable/unsecured airway needing closest hospital
    - Specialized team activation (Rape, Burn, STEMI)
    - Refusal consultation
    - ALS release to BLS authorization

### Pearls
1. ⚠ **Communication Failure Standing Orders:** In the event of communications failure, act on standing orders within your scope of practice based on the clinical situation.
2. ⚠ **Medication Authorization:** Medication and procedure authorizations must come directly from a Physician, PA, or NP.
3. **HEAR vs COR Distinction:** Use the HEAR system for brief hospital notifications where no orders are needed. Use the COR system for direct physician consultation, alerts (Stroke, Trauma, STEMI), and medication authorizations.
4. **MCI Protocol Rules:** During mass-casualty or disaster incidents, routine communications are suspended. The Transport group supervisor will communicate directly with the coordinating ED. Medical Control may grant a request to follow protocols as written.
