# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Administrative - Trauma Field Triage

### Overview
This protocol establishes the criteria and routing instructions to rapidly triage and transport trauma victims to definitive care. It outlines physiologic, mechanism of injury, and special considerations to ensure optimal patient outcomes through timely arrival at an appropriate trauma center.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    **Mechanism of Injury Criteria:**
    - Ejection (partial/complete) from automobile
    - Death in same passenger compartment
    - Auto vs pedestrian/bicyclist (thrown, run over, >20 mph)
    - Motorcycle crash >20 mph

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    **Physiologic Criteria:**
    - GCS < 14
    - Systolic BP < 90 mmHg
    **Immediate Life-Threats:**
    - Unmanageable airway
    - Uncontrolled hemorrhage
    - Traumatic cardiac arrest

=== "Differential Considerations"
    **Special Considerations:**
    - **Age:** >55 years old
    - **End-Stage Renal Disease (Dialysis)**
    - **Time-Sensitive Extremities:** Open fractures, neurovascular compromise
    - **Burns:** Absent other trauma, burns meeting criteria (full/partial >10% TBSA, circumferential, electrical/chemical, inhalation, face/hands/feet/genitals) go to Burn Center.
    - **Pregnancy:** >20 weeks pregnant → trauma center or OB-capable hospital.
    - **Pediatrics:** Severely injured go to pediatric trauma center (CHKD or VCU).

### Pearls
- ⚠ **Provider Judgment:** Providers have the expertise to make clinical judgments on atypical presentations and route accordingly.
- ⚠ **Anticoagulants / Bleeding Disorders:** Patients on anticoagulants or with bleeding disorders are at high risk for deterioration; contact Medical Control and consider trauma center routing.
- ⚠ **Pregnancy:** Patients >20 weeks pregnant require specialized assessment; route to a trauma center or an OB-capable hospital.
- ⚠ **Contacting Medical Control:** Contact Medical Control whenever destination determination is unclear, especially for mechanism-only criteria or high-risk comorbidities.
