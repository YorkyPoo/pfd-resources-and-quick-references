# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Administrative - Death & Criteria for Withholding Resuscitation

This protocol outlines the indications and legal requirements for withholding resuscitation efforts in the presence of death or valid Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) orders. It also establishes the clinical criteria and procedures for the termination of resuscitation (TOR) efforts when cardiac arrest is refractory to prehospital interventions.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Valid Virginia DDNR form (actual or copy/digital)
    - Valid DDNR from any State (actual or copy/digital)
    - VA approved DDNR ALERT bracelet/necklace
    - Decapitation or other obvious mortal injury
    - Rigor mortis
    - Dependent lividity
    - Decomposition

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - "Living will" or advanced directive only (without valid DDNR/POST)
    - POST, POLST, MOLST without specific DNR/DNI order
    - Any doubt regarding applicability or validity of DNR

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Cardiac arrest is refractory to appropriate prehospital interventions
    - Arrest not witnessed by EMS
    - 20 minutes of continuous CPR
    - No return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC)
    - No shocks delivered


### Interventions
| # | Action / Intervention | EMR | EMT | A | I | P |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **Withhold CPR** for obvious mortal injury, rigor mortis, dependent lividity, decomposition, or valid DDNR. | | • | | • | |
| 2 | **Initiate CPR** if doubt exists regarding the DNR validity, or if only a living will/advanced directive is present. | | • | | • | |
| 3 | **Provide full BLS/ALS care** prior to termination (BLS: AED, pulse ox, EtCO2, supraglottic airway; ALS: advanced airway, IV/IO, meds per ACLS). | | • | | • | |
| 4 | **Contact Medical Control** for termination decision or DNR questions. Provide thorough report of history, condition, and treatment. | | • | | • | |
| 5 | **Remain on scene** and notify law enforcement and Medical Examiner if terminated on scene. Support bystanders and family. | | • | | • | |

### Pearls
* ⚠ **Termination of resuscitation efforts is a Medical Control decision ONLY.**
* ⚠ **Revocation of DNR:** A DNR can be revoked at any time by the patient or authorized decision maker by physically destroying the DNR order form or oral expression of intent to revoke.
* ⚠ **20 Minute TOR Criteria:** Consider contacting Medical Control to request TOR if the arrest was not witnessed by EMS and after 20 minutes of continuous CPR: 1) There has been no return of spontaneous circulation (ROSC) AND 2) No shocks were delivered.
* • A DNR/DDNR/DNAR is applicable ONLY if the patient is in cardiac arrest.
* • During mass-casualty incidents, resources must be directed toward viable patients.
* • If resuscitation efforts are terminated at the scene, document time and physician name on the PCR. Be attentive to the emotional needs of any family members or bystanders present.
