# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Medical - Sepsis

### Overview
This protocol outlines the identification and management of sepsis, focusing on the early recognition of a suspected infection combined with abnormal vital signs, and the timely administration of fluids and vasopressors to maintain adequate perfusion.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Suspected infection
    - Immunocompromised states (e.g., cancer, transplant, HIV)
    - Recent surgery or hospitalization
    - Indwelling medical devices

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    **Temp >100.4°F or <96.8°F AND 2 or more of the following:**
    - Altered Mentation
    - Bradycardia
    - SBP/MAP thresholds by age
    - RR (Respiratory Rate) abnormalities
    - Capillary Refill > 2 seconds
    - EtCO2 < 25 mmHg

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Monitor for fluid overload
    - Congestive Heart Failure (CHF)
    - Pulmonary edema
    - Cardiogenic shock
    - Hypovolemia from other causes

| 5 | Push dose **[[Epinephrine]] 5-10 mcg** IV/IO every 2-3 mins to maintain MAP until infusion is ready (Adult) | | | | | |
| 6 | Contact Poquoson OMD / Medical Control / Prehospital Sepsis Alert | | | MC | MC | MC |

### Pearls
* ⚠ **CAUTION:** Administer IV fluids carefully in patients with a history of CHF or decreased ejection fraction. Monitor closely for signs of fluid overload and pulmonary edema.
* ⚠ **WARNING:** Vasopressor infusions (**Norepinephrine** and **[[Epinephrine]]**) should ideally be administered via an infusion pump to ensure accurate dosing.
