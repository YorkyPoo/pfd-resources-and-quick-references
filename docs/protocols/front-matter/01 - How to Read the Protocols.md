# How to Read the Protocols

## Levels of Certification
The protocols use the following abbreviations to indicate provider levels:
* **EMR:** Emergency Medical Responder
* **EMT:** Emergency Medical Technician
* **A:** Advanced Emergency Medical Technician
* **I:** Intermediate
* **P:** Paramedic

---

## Special Requirements

> **These items in the provider's scope of practice have the following special requirements:**
>
> 1. Documentation of EMS agency (post-affiliation) training, to the specific skills and medications, must be present and maintained in the provider's personnel file, **and**
>
> 2. EMS agency Operational Medical Director must provide specific written authorization, to include and/or limit, these specific skills and medications, in the provider's scope of practice. Written authorization must be kept and maintained in the provider's personnel file.

---

## Understanding the Protocol Format

The following are your new protocols. These have been formatted to maximize clarity and speed of use in the field. The new layout utilizes a matrix-style grid system, allowing providers to quickly scan down the page and immediately identify the interventions authorized for their specific certification level.

### The Intervention Matrix

Instead of listing paragraphs of text for each certification level, interventions are listed sequentially in a table. To determine your authorized actions, locate your certification level at the top of the columns (EMR, EMT, A, I, P) and read down the grid.

| # | Action / Intervention | EMR | EMT | A | I | P |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1 | **Perform general patient management.** | | • | | • | |
| 2 | **Administer [[Epinephrine]] Auto-injector** | | | • | | • |
| 3 | **Obtain IV/IO access.** | | | | • | |
| 4 | **Administer [[Epinephrine]] Infusion** | | | | MC | |

#### Matrix Key & Symbols:
* **Dot (•):** You are authorized to perform that intervention as a **Standing Order**.
* **Blank Space:** The intervention is **NOT** within your scope of practice or protocol authorization.
* **MC:** You must contact **Medical Control** for authorization prior to performing that intervention.
* **Caution (⚠):** Special considerations, critical safety warnings, or contraindications apply to this step.

### Protocol Structure

* **Overview:** A brief summary of the emergency or condition.
* **Assessment & Criteria:** A 3-column table breaking down the History of Present Illness (HPI), Signs and Symptoms, and Considerations (Differential Diagnoses) to guide your clinical judgment.
* **Interventions:** The sequential matrix of patient care.
* **PEARLS Section:** Each protocol contains a PEARLS section providing important considerations, contraindications, and points for taking your patient care from mediocre to exceptional.
