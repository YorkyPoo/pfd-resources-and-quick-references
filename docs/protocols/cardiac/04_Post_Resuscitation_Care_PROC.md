# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Post Resuscitation Care

Guidelines for managing patients following Return of Spontaneous Circulation (ROSC), focusing on hemodynamic support, airway management, and identifying reversible causes.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Cardiac arrest with ROSC

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Hypotension
    - Hypoxia
    - Arrhythmias

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Reversible causes: H's and T's
    - Target EtCO2 35-45 mmHg

### Pearls
• Most patients will require at least some vasopressor support to maintain a MAP > 65 following cardiac arrest.
• ⚠ **Push dose Epinephrine** is a bridge, not a replacement for a drip.
• ⚠ Administer vasopressors through the largest, most proximal IV or IO & monitor for extravasation.
• Maintain normal ventilation rate (target EtCO2 35-45 mmHg).
• Identify and treat potentially reversible causes of cardiac arrest (H's and T's): Hypoxia, Hyperkalemia or hypokalemia, Hyperglycemia or hypoglycemia, Hypovolemia, Hydrogen Ion (acidosis), Tension pneumothorax/hemothorax, Tamponade (Cardiac), Thrombosis (Cardiac/pulmonary), Toxins, Trauma, Tablets (overdose).
