# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Cardiac Arrest (Traumatic)

### Overview
Resuscitation guidelines for traumatic cardiac arrest, prioritizing hemorrhage control, reversible causes, and rapid transport over prolonged scene CPR.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Blunt or penetrating trauma
    - Mechanism of injury indicating severe trauma

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Pulseless
    - Apnea or agonal respirations

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Medical incident vs. Trauma
    - Obvious signs of death (excluded from protocol)
    - Withholding CPR for Asystole on EMS arrival

### Pearls
• ⚠ **Asystole on arrival survival <1%**: Consider withholding resuscitation if the rhythm is asystole on EMS arrival, as survival rates are extremely low unless loss of pulses was witnessed by EMS with immediate intervention.
• ⚠ **Prioritize reversible causes over prolonged CPR**: Efficacy of CPR in trauma is unclear. Focus on immediate reversible causes such as chest decompression, volume expansion, and airway management. CPR is needed to circulate any fluids/medications.
• ⚠ **Do not delay transport** for establishing IVs/ALS.
• ⚠ **Shock Index = Heart Rate / Systolic Blood Pressure**. Higher shock indices correlate with an increased risk of post-intubation hypotension and cardiac arrest.
• If available, blood products should be used rather than **0.9% Sodium Chloride** for the treatment of hemorrhagic shock.
• Assess the scene and patient closely. If the degree of sustained trauma appears disproportionately minor, consider the possibility that a medical incident may have preceded or been induced by the injury.
