# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Cardiac - Cardiac Arrest Non-Traumatic

### Overview
Cardiac arrest is the cessation of cardiac mechanical activity resulting in the absence of circulation. This protocol addresses the two primary categories of cardiac arrest: Ventricular Fibrillation (V-Fib) / Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia (V-Tach) which are shockable rhythms, and Asystole / Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA) which are non-shockable rhythms. Immediate high-quality CPR and rapid defibrillation (for shockable rhythms) are critical for survival.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Witnessed or unwitnessed collapse
    - Estimated downtime
    - Bystander CPR performed
    - Recent illness or trauma history
    - Medication history (especially cardiac meds)
    - Dialysis status

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Unresponsive
    - Absent or abnormal breathing (agonal respirations)
    - No pulse palpable (carotid or femoral)
    - V-Fib/V-Tach or Asystole/PEA on monitor
    - Cyanosis or pallor

=== "Differential Considerations"
    **H's and T's - Potentially Reversible Causes:**
    - **Hypoxia**
    - **Hyperkalemia or Hypokalemia**
    - **Hyperglycemia or Hypoglycemia**
    - **Hypovolemia**
    - **Hydrogen Ion (acidosis)**
    - **Tension pneumothorax/hemothorax**
    - **Tamponade (Cardiac)**
    - **Thrombosis (Cardiac/pulmonary)**
    - **Toxins**
    - **Trauma**
    - **Tablets (overdose)**

### Pearls
1. ⚠ **High-Quality CPR is Essential:** Push hard and fast at rate **100 – 120 bpm**. Allow complete chest recoil. Minimize interruptions. Change compressors every **2 minutes** or sooner if fatigued. Give continuous compressions with advanced airway.

2. ⚠ **EtCO₂ Monitoring:** Use quantitative EtCO₂ monitoring with BVM. If EtCO₂ < 10 mmHg, improve compressions. If sudden increase above **30-40 mmHg**, check for ROSC.

3. ⚠ **Medication Administration:** Give all medications without interruption of CPR. [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md) should be administered every **3–5 minutes** throughout the arrest.

4. ⚠ **Defibrillation Technique:** Ensure optimal pad placement. Consider adjustment of pad position if multiple shocks delivered without rhythm change.

5. ⚠ **DNR Status:** Patients with a valid durable DNR or injuries inconsistent with survival are excluded from this protocol. Determine DNR status early.

6. **Shockable vs. Non-Shockable Rhythms:**
   * **Shockable:** V-Fib and Pulseless V-Tach require immediate defibrillation followed by 2 minutes of CPR.
   * **Non-Shockable:** Asystole and PEA require continuous CPR with [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md) administration; defibrillation is NOT indicated unless rhythm converts to V-Fib/V-Tach.

7. **Special Circumstances for [Calcium Chloride](../pharmacology/Calcium%20Chloride.md) and [Sodium Bicarbonate](../pharmacology/Sodium%20Bicarbonate.md):** These medications are reserved for specific clinical scenarios only: renal dialysis patients, crush injuries, suspected calcium channel blocker overdose, or suspected hyperkalemia.

8. **Pediatric Considerations:** Maximum doses apply to pediatric patients: [Amiodarone](../pharmacology/Amiodarone.md) **300 mg**, [Calcium Chloride](../pharmacology/Calcium%20Chloride.md) **1 g**, [Sodium Bicarbonate](../pharmacology/Sodium%20Bicarbonate.md) **50 mEq**. Use pediatric-specific dosing for all medications.

9. **Termination of Resuscitation Criteria:** All three criteria must be met before requesting TOR from Medical Control: unwitnessed arrest by EMS, >20 minutes of CPR without ROSC, and no shocks delivered.

10. **Continuous Assessment:** Throughout resuscitation, continuously assess for and treat potentially reversible causes (H's and T's). This may be the key to achieving ROSC.
