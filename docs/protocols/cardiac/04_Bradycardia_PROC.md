# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Cardiac - Symptomatic Bradycardia

**OVERVIEW:**
Symptomatic bradycardia occurs when the heart rate is less than 60 beats per minute with signs of inadequate perfusion despite adequate oxygenation and ventilation. This protocol addresses unstable patients requiring immediate intervention to restore adequate cardiac output and tissue perfusion.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Sustained ventricular rate < 60 bpm
    - Age-based assessment
    - Onset and duration of symptoms
    - Recent medications
    - History of cardiac disease

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Hypotension (age based)
    - Signs of shock
    - Altered mental status
    - Pulmonary edema
    - Dyspnea
    - Chest pain

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Asymptomatic/stable bradycardia (excluded - monitor closely)
    - Neonates < 28 days (see Neonatal Resuscitation protocol)
    - Medication-induced bradycardia
    - AV blocks
    - Sinus node dysfunction

### Pearls
1. Close monitoring during fluid administration including frequent reassessment of respiratory effort, lung sounds, and edema.
2. ⚠ Asymptomatic/stable bradycardia patients are excluded from this protocol - monitor closely for any change in status.
3. ⚠ For neonates (less than 28 days of age), refer to Neonatal Resuscitation protocol.
4. [Atropine](../../pharmacology/Atropine.md) may be ineffective in high-degree AV blocks and transcutaneous pacing should be considered early in these cases.
5. Ensure adequate oxygenation and ventilation prior to medication administration - many cases of bradycardia in children are due to hypoxia.
