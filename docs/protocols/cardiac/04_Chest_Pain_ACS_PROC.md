# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Cardiac - Chest Pain / ACS

**OVERVIEW:**
Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) encompasses a spectrum of conditions resulting from reduced blood flow to the heart muscle, including unstable angina, non-ST elevation myocardial infarction (NSTEMI), and ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI). Prompt recognition and intervention are critical to reduce myocardial damage and improve patient outcomes. This protocol addresses both typical and atypical presentations of cardiac ischemia.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Chest pain or discomfort (pressure, squeezing, fullness)
    - Duration and onset of symptoms
    - Radiation of pain to arm, jaw, neck, back
    - Previous cardiac history
    - Risk factors: hypertension, diabetes, smoking, family history

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Chest pain/discomfort
    - Dyspnea
    - Diaphoresis
    - Nausea/vomiting
    - Syncope or near-syncope
    - Palpitations
    - Anxiety, sense of impending doom

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - **Acute Myocardial Infarction (STEMI/NSTEMI)**
    - **Unstable Angina**
    - **Aortic Dissection**
    - **Pulmonary Embolism**
    - **Pneumothorax**
    - **Pericarditis/Myocarditis**
    - **GERD/Esophageal spasm**
    - **Musculoskeletal pain**

### Pearls
1. **Atypical Presentations:** ACS may present as abdominal pain, nausea/vomiting, syncope, dyspnea, back pain, neck/jaw pain, or arm pain/discomfort without classic chest pain. Maintain high index of suspicion in at-risk populations.

2. **[Aspirin](../pharmacology/Aspirin.md) Administration:**
   * **Class:** Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)
   * **Action:** Inhibits platelet action preventing clot formation
   * **Indication:** Acute coronary syndromes (ACS)/STEMI
   * **Contraindications:** Allergy to **[Aspirin](../pharmacology/Aspirin.md)**/NSAIDs
   * **Precautions:** History of GI bleeding/peptic ulcers, chronic kidney disease, trauma
   * **Side Effects:** Indigestion

3. ⚠ **Medication Safety:** Confirm all "Rights" (Right patient, medication, dose, route, time), ensure medications not expired, assess for drug-drug interactions before administration.

4. ⚠ **STEMI Time-Critical:** Acquire 12-Lead ECG within 5 minutes and transmit within 10 minutes. Time is muscle - early intervention saves myocardium.

5. ⚠ **Patient Identification:** Do NOT provide patient identifiers over the HEAR radio. Use COR for "Pre-Hospital STEMI Alert" with name and DOB.

6. Refer to Death & Criteria for Withholding Resuscitation Poquoson Protocol if patient decompensates to cardiac arrest.

7. Associated factors affecting hemodynamics (trauma, intoxication, illness) must be considered when determining medication administration.

8. If law-enforcement restraints utilized, officer should accompany patient; consider exchanging for EMS-sanctioned restraints.

9. Patients in law enforcement custody must have officer present at all times.
