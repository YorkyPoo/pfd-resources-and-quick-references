# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Asystole and Pulseless Electrical Activity

Resuscitation guidelines for Asystole and Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA), emphasizing high-quality CPR and identifying reversible causes.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Any patient without a palpable pulse or respiration.

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Asystole or Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA) evidence on the cardiac monitor (check two leads).
    - AED advising not to shock.

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Patients with a valid durable DNR, rigor mortis, lividity, decomposition, or injuries inconsistent with survival are excluded.

### Pearls
• ⚠ Push hard and fast: rate 100 – 120 bpm. Allow complete chest recoil.
• ⚠ Minimize interruptions in chest compressions.
• ⚠ Termination of resuscitative efforts is a **Poquoson OMD / Medical Control** decision only.
