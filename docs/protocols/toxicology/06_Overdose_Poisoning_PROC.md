# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Toxicology - Overdose / Poisoning

### Overview
This protocol outlines the management of patients suffering from toxic ingestions, overdoses, or environmental poisonings. The primary focus is on immediate airway management, decontamination, removal from the exposure source, and the administration of specific antidotes based on the suspected toxidrome.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Substance ingested, inhaled, or exposed to
    - Time of exposure or ingestion
    - Amount or dose involved
    - Reason for exposure (intentional vs. accidental)
    - Bystander interventions prior to EMS arrival

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Altered mental status or coma
    - Respiratory depression or arrest
    - Pupillary changes (pinpoint or dilated)
    - Tachycardia, bradycardia, or arrhythmias
    - Nausea, vomiting, SLUDGEM symptoms
    - Hyperthermia or hypothermia

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Stroke or intracranial hemorrhage
    - Hypoglycemia or DKA
    - Head trauma
    - Sepsis or severe infection
    - Seizure disorder or post-ictal state
    - Hypoxia

### Pearls
• ⚠ **Provider Safety:** Ensure scene safety and utilize appropriate PPE. Do not become contaminated while attempting rescue or treatment.
• ⚠ **[Naloxone](../../pharmacology/Naloxone.md) Administration:** Administer only enough **[Naloxone](../../pharmacology/Naloxone.md)** to restore adequate respiratory drive. Complete reversal may cause acute withdrawal, agitation, or vomiting.
• ⚠ **CBRNE Exposures:** For specific chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear, or explosive exposures, refer to the applicable Poquoson Protocol(s) for CBRNE.
• ⚠ **Seizures:** If the patient experiences a seizure due to the overdose or toxic ingestion, refer to the Seizure protocol.
• **Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCA):** Wide QRS complexes >100ms on the 12-lead ECG in the setting of suspected TCA overdose is an indication for **[Sodium Bicarbonate](../../pharmacology/Sodium%20Bicarbonate.md)** administration.
