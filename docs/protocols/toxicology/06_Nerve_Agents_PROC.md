# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Toxicology - Nerve Agents

This protocol outlines the pre-hospital management of patients with suspected exposure to chemical nerve agents or organophosphates, characterized by cholinergic crisis and SLUDGE/DUMBBELS symptoms. Rapid identification, decontamination, and administration of antidotes are critical.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Suspected or confirmed exposure to chemical nerve agents
    - Exposure to organophosphate pesticides
    - Mass casualty chemical incidents
    - Onset of symptoms during or shortly after exposure

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    **SLUDGE Symptoms:**
    - Salivation
    - Lacrimation (tearing)
    - Urination
    - Defecation
    - GI distress
    - Emesis
    **DUMBBELS Symptoms:**
    - Diarrhea
    - Urination
    - Miosis (pinpoint pupils) / Muscle weakness
    - Bradycardia / Bronchospasm / Bronchorrhea
    - Emesis
    - Lacrimation
    - Salivation / Seizures

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Ensure scene safety and don appropriate PPE prior to patient contact
    - Identify agent if possible without risking exposure
    - Differentiate from other toxicological emergencies (e.g., opioid overdose, cholinergic crisis from other medications)
    - Patients may require significant amounts of airway suctioning and ventilation support

### Pearls
• **DuoDote** contains **[[Atropine]] 2.1 mg** and **Pralidoxime Chloride (2-PAM) 600 mg**.
• Examples of nerve agents include Sarin (GB), Soman (GD), Tabun (GA), and VX.
• ⚠ **WARNING:** Patients exposed to nerve agents can off-gas and contaminate providers. Decontamination must occur prior to transport.
• **[[Atropine]]** is the antidote of choice for organophosphate and nerve agent poisoning. Large and repeated doses may be required to dry secretions.
• If using **AtroPen** autoinjectors for pediatrics, utilize weight-based dosing guidelines.
