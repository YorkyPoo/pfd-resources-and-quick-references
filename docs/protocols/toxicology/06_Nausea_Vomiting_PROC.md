# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Toxicology / Medical - Nausea / Vomiting

This protocol outlines the assessment and management of patients experiencing persistent nausea or vomiting to prevent dehydration, aspiration, and further clinical deterioration.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Onset and duration of nausea/vomiting
    - Frequency and volume of emesis
    - Presence of blood or coffee-ground material
    - Associated symptoms (pain, fever, diarrhea, vertigo)
    - Recent trauma or head injury
    - Possible ingestions or toxic exposures
    - Current medications

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Nausea/retching/vomiting
    - Signs of dehydration (poor skin turgor, dry mucous membranes)
    - Tachycardia, hypotension
    - Altered mental status
    - Abdominal tenderness or rigidity

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Gastrointestinal disorders (gastroenteritis, obstruction, appendicitis)
    - Cardiac ischemia / Myocardial Infarction
    - Head injury / Increased Intracranial Pressure (ICP)
    - Toxicologic / Overdose
    - Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
    - Pregnancy / Ectopic pregnancy
    - Vertigo / Motion sickness

### Pearls
* ⚠ **Ondansetron (Zofran)** must be administered as a SLOW IV push (over 2-5 minutes) to avoid syncope or other adverse reactions.
* ⚠ Use caution in patients with known prolonged QT interval, as **Ondansetron** can cause QT prolongation and potentially lead to Torsades de Pointes.
* ⚠ Prophylactic use of antiemetics is generally not recommended unless specific clinical indications are present.
* ⚠ Maximum total dose of **Ondansetron** is **8 mg** for Adults and **0.3 mg/kg** for Pediatrics.
