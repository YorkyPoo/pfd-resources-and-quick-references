# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Respiratory - Airway / Inhalation Irritants

**OVERVIEW:**
This protocol addresses exposure to choking agents and irritant gases causing airway injury. Management focuses on: 1) Reduce exposure/rescue and decontamination, 2) Optimize airway/ventilation, 3) Administer treatments/antidotes, 4) Support cardiovascular system.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Exposure to choking agents:
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Chlorine
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Ammonia
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Phosgene
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Fuming Sulfuric Acid
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Hydrogen Sulfide
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Hydrochloric Acid
    &nbsp;&nbsp;– Hydrofluoric Acid
    - Duration of exposure
    - Enclosed vs. open space
    - Occupational exposure history

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Singed facial or nasal hairs
    - Burns or soot on face, nose, or mouth
    - Hoarse voice or stridor
    - Carbonaceous sputum
    - Difficulty breathing/respiratory distress
    - Hypoxia, Tachypnea
    - Throat pain/burning, wheezing
    - Laryngospasm
    - Non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Chemical pneumonitis
    - Reactive airways dysfunction
    - Delayed pulmonary edema (phosgene)
    - Upper airway obstruction
    - Lower airway bronchospasm
    - Systemic toxicity (hydrogen sulfide)
    - Co-existing thermal burns
    - Concomitant trauma

### Pearls
1. ⚠ **Decontamination Safety:** Victims must be decontaminated and/or confirmed "clean" prior to treatment and transport to prevent secondary contamination of EMS personnel and equipment.

2. ⚠ **[Sodium Bicarbonate](../../pharmacology/Sodium%20Bicarbonate.md) - Once Only:** For chlorine gas exposure, **[Sodium Bicarbonate](../../pharmacology/Sodium%20Bicarbonate.md) nebulizer is administered ONCE only** (2 mL diluted with 2 mL 0.9% Sodium chloride). Repeat dosing is not indicated.

3. ⚠ **RSI Contraindication:** Intubation of spontaneously breathing patients without rapid sequence induction (RSI) is generally not recommended. RSI is not addressed in Poquoson Protocols—refer to agency specific protocols.

4. ⚠ **Pediatric Intubation:** Intubation of children under 12 years old is a Paramedic "red dot" skill only requiring specialized training and authorization.

5. **Delayed Symptoms:** Phosgene exposure may cause minimal initial symptoms with delayed onset of pulmonary edema (up to 24 hours). All phosgene exposures require transport and monitoring.

6. **Hydrogen Sulfide Toxicity:** Hydrogen sulfide causes cellular asphyxia by inhibiting cytochrome oxidase. Severe exposures may present with "knockdown" (sudden collapse). Rescuers must use SCBA.

7. **Ammonia vs. Chlorine:** Ammonia causes upper airway and ocular irritation primarily. Chlorine penetrates deeper causing bronchospasm and alveolar injury. Both may cause laryngospasm.

8. **Chemical Pneumonitis:** Chemical pneumonitis from irritant gases can progress to acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS). Early aggressive airway management and transport to appropriate facility are critical.
