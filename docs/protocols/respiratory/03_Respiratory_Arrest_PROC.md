# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Respiratory - Respiratory Arrest / Airway Management

**OVERVIEW:**
Respiratory arrest (apnea) and respiratory failure represent life-threatening emergencies requiring immediate airway management and ventilatory support. This protocol provides a systematic approach to securing and maintaining a patent airway, from basic adjuncts through advanced airway techniques, with emphasis on continuous monitoring and prevention of complications such as hypoxia and hyperventilation.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Respiratory failure or arrest (apnea)
    - Inability to maintain patent airway
    - Altered level of consciousness
    - Witnessed choking or foreign body aspiration

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Absence of spontaneous respirations
    - Inadequate or ineffective breathing
    - Altered mental status
    - SpO2 desaturation
    - Absent breath sounds

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Airway obstruction (foreign body, trauma, edema)
    - Drug overdose or toxicity
    - Neuromuscular failure
    - Severe metabolic derangement
    - Cardiac arrest with respiratory component

### Pearls
1. **EtCO2 Target:** Maintain continuous box-waveform EtCO2 at **35–45 mmHg** once advanced airway is placed. This is the definitive indicator of adequate ventilation and perfusion.

2. **⚠ Over-Ventilation Avoidance:** Hyperventilation causes cerebral vasoconstriction, reduced coronary perfusion, and gastric insufflation. Deliver only enough tidal volume to produce visible chest rise (6-7 mL/kg).

3. **⚠ Pediatric Red Dot Skill:** Intubation of children <12 years is a **Paramedic "red dot" skill only**. Intermediate providers must defer to Paramedic or utilize SGA/BVM ventilation for pediatric patients.

4. **Confirmation of Tube Placement:** Always verify advanced airway placement with BOTH continuous box-waveform capnography AND auscultation. Absence of EtCO2 waveform indicates esophageal intubation or loss of perfusion.

5. **Two-Person BVM Technique:** Whenever possible, use two providers for BVM ventilation—one to seal the mask and open the airway, one to squeeze the bag. This significantly improves tidal volume delivery.

6. **⚠ Sedation Precautions:** Use **half doses** of **[[Fentanyl]]** and **[[Midazolam]]** for frail, hypotensive, underweight, and/or elderly (>65 years) patients. Monitor closely for respiratory depression.

7. **⚠ Opioid-Benzodiazepine Combination:** Due to risk of apnea, use **[[Fentanyl]]** in combination with **[[Midazolam]]** only after airway is definitively secured (ET tube or SGA in place).

8. **Foreign Body Management:** If complete airway obstruction is suspected and BVM ventilation fails, proceed immediately to laryngoscopy with Magill forceps removal or surgical airway if visualization is impossible.

9. **Continuous Reassessment:** Re-verify ET tube placement after every patient movement, transfer, or position change. Dislodgement can occur silently—continuous EtCO2 monitoring is essential.

10. **Documentation Requirements:** Document method of airway management, size of airway device, depth of ET tube placement, number of intubation attempts, confirmation methods used, and patient response to sedation/analgesia.
