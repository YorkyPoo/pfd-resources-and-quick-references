# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Respiratory - Respiratory Distress

This protocol addresses respiratory distress from obstructive (asthma/COPD), infectious (pneumonia), edematous (CHF), or traumatic etiologies. Critical objectives include immediate identification of life-threatening conditions (airway obstruction, respiratory failure, tension pneumothorax/hemothorax, cardiac ischemia), initiation of targeted therapy, and maintenance of oxygen saturation above 94%. Continuous EtCO2 monitoring guides therapy and alerts providers to impending respiratory failure.

---

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Respiratory distress (asthma, COPD, pneumonia, CHF, near-drowning, pulmonary embolism, infection)
    - Trauma to chest/neck
    - Allergic reaction history
    - CHF/cardiogenic pulmonary edema
    - Hypoventilation syndromes (obesity, neuromuscular disease)
    - Recent surgery/anesthesia
    - Smoke inhalation

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Accessory muscle use, retractions, nasal flaring
    - SpO2 <94% on room air
    - Respiratory rate >24/min (adult) or outside pediatric norms
    - Inability to speak in complete sentences
    - Pulmonary/peripheral edema, JVD
    - Productive cough, wheezing, stridor
    - Altered mental status
    - Cyanosis, diaphoresis
    - Asymmetric chest rise

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Pneumonia/Pneumocystis
    - Acute pulmonary embolism
    - Pleural effusion
    - Cardiac ischemia/MI
    - Anaphylaxis
    - Flail chest/hemothorax
    - Tension pneumothorax
    - Smoke inhalation
    - Bronchiolitis (pediatric)

### Pearls
1. Early use of CPAP reduces the need for intubation in appropriate patients with pulmonary edema
2. ⚠ **CPAP CONTRAINDICATIONS:** Apnea/agonal respirations; GCS ≤13; Pneumothorax (suspected or confirmed); Facial trauma/burns/recent surgery; Penetrating neck/chest trauma; Active vomiting; Hypotension (SBP <90 mmHg or MAP <65 mmHg); Inability to tolerate mask
3. ⚠ CPAP is authorized for EMT providers but requires Medical Control contact for A-level providers
4. ⚠ I/P providers may titrate CPAP PEEP up to 10 cmH2O
5. ⚠ **Breath Stacking Warning:** If breath stacking occurs during positive pressure ventilation, disconnect the BVM and allow for exhalation
6. If patient continues to deteriorate despite CPAP, consider supraglottic airway or prepare for endotracheal intubation
7. In CHF/pulmonary edema, avoid aggressive fluid resuscitation; consider [[Nitroglycerin]] (sublingual if SBP permits) before CPAP if experienced provider available
8. Close monitoring is required during CPAP for hypotension or development of pneumothorax
9. Pediatric respiratory distress: Consider bronchiolitis in infants with wheezing and respiratory distress; normal saline nebulizer can reduce work of breathing
10. Maintain continuous EtCO2 monitoring throughout care to detect early signs of respiratory fatigue and guide interventions
