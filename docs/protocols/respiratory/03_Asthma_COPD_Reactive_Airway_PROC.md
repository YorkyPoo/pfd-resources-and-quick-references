# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Respiratory - Asthma, COPD, and Reactive Airway Disease

**OVERVIEW:**
This protocol addresses acute bronchospasm associated with asthma, COPD, and reactive airway disease. The primary goal is to rapidly reverse bronchoconstriction through inhaled beta-agonists and anticholinergics, with escalation to systemic corticosteroids for moderate to severe symptoms and [Magnesium Sulfate](../../pharmacology/Magnesium%20Sulfate.md) for severe, life-threatening presentations. Prompt recognition of respiratory failure and appropriate intervention by all provider levels is essential.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - History of asthma, COPD, or reactive airway disease
    - Known triggers (allergens, exercise, infections)
    - Recent upper respiratory infection
    - Medication compliance and recent inhaler use
    - Prior intubations or ICU admissions for respiratory distress

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Moderate Symptoms: Wheezing, mild respiratory distress, speaks in full sentences
    - Moderate to Severe: Increased work of breathing, accessory muscle use, speaks in phrases
    - Severe: Silent chest, tripod positioning, single-word speech, altered mental status
    - Respiratory rate > 30, heart rate > 120
    - Pulse oximetry < 90% on room air

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - **Pneumonia**
    - **Pulmonary Edema / CHF**
    - **Pneumothorax**
    - **Anaphylaxis / Allergic Reaction** (see Allergic Reaction Protocol)
    - **Upper Airway Obstruction / Stridor** (see Stridor below)
    - **Pulmonary Embolism**

### Pearls
1. ⚠ **[Ipratropium](../../pharmacology/Ipratropium Bromide.md) Bromide is ONCE ONLY.** Do not administer repeat doses without consulting Poquoson OMD. Excessive anticholinergic dosing may worsen outcomes.

2. **[Albuterol](../../pharmacology/Albuterol.md) is Repeat-As-Necessary.** EMTs may administer multiple treatments based on clinical response. Continuous nebulized [Albuterol](../../pharmacology/Albuterol.md) may be initiated for severe cases with Poquoson OMD direction.

3. ⚠ **Route Selection Matters.** Nebulized medications deliver drug directly to airways for bronchospasm. Reserve IV/IO bronchodilators for patients unable to tolerate nebulizer or in impending respiratory failure.

4. **Pediatric Maximum Doses:**<br>• Methylprednisolone: **125 mg maximum** (regardless of weight-based calculation).<br>• [Magnesium Sulfate](../../pharmacology/Magnesium%20Sulfate.md): **2,000 mg (2 g) maximum** (regardless of weight-based calculation).

5. ⚠ **Stridor vs Wheezing:** Stridor indicates upper airway obstruction (epiglottitis, croup, anaphylaxis) and responds to **nebulized [Epinephrine](../../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md)**. Wheezing indicates lower airway bronchospasm (asthma/COPD) and responds to **nebulized [Albuterol](../../pharmacology/Albuterol.md)/[Ipratropium](../../pharmacology/Ipratropium Bromide.md)**.

6. **Severe Asthma Indicators:** Silent chest (no audible wheeze), tripod positioning, inability to speak in phrases, altered mental status, or SpO₂ < 90% despite supplemental oxygen warrant immediate escalation to **[Magnesium Sulfate](../../pharmacology/Magnesium%20Sulfate.md)** and preparation for airway support.

7. **Steroids Take Time.** Methylprednisolone onset is 1-2 hours; do not delay transport waiting for clinical improvement. Early administration improves outcomes but is not immediately life-saving in acute crisis.
