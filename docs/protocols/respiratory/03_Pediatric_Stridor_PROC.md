# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## Respiratory - Pediatric Stridor

**OVERVIEW:**
Stridor in pediatric patients indicates upper airway obstruction requiring rapid assessment and intervention. The priority is maintaining a calm environment as agitation worsens obstruction. Nebulized [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md) and corticosteroids are primary treatments with airway protection as the ultimate priority.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Recent upper respiratory illness (croup, epiglottitis)
    - Choking episode or foreign body aspiration
    - Congenital airway anomalies
    - Sudden onset vs. gradual progression
    - Vaccination status (H. influenzae type b)
    - Onset with fever or drooling

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Stridor (inspiratory > expiratory)
    - Hoarse voice or barking seal cough
    - Difficulty breathing / retractions
    - Tripod positioning
    - Drooling / inability to swallow
    - Agitation or altered mental status
    - Cyanosis

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - **Croup (Laryngotracheobronchitis):**
    - Gradual onset, barking cough
    - Low-grade fever, inspiratory stridor
    - **Epiglottitis:**
    - Sudden onset, toxic appearance
    - High fever, tripod position
    - No cough, severe drooling
    - **Foreign body aspiration**
    - **Bacterial tracheitis**
    - **Retropharyngeal abscess**

### Pearls
1. **⚠ Critical Intubation Warning:** Intubation of children under 12 years of age is a Paramedic "red dot" skill hazard only. Intermediate providers must contact Medical Control prior to attempting intubation in pediatric patients.

2. **Keep Child Calm:** Agitation, crying, and fighting increase oxygen demand and worsen upper airway obstruction. Allow the child to remain in the parent's arms and avoid unnecessary stimulation.

3. **Parental Presence is Critical:** Parental presence and reassurance are therapeutic interventions. Never separate a distressed child from their caregiver unless absolutely necessary for medical intervention.

4. **Steroid Onset Time:** Methylprednisolone and other corticosteroids require 30-60 minutes to demonstrate clinical effect. Nebulized [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md) provides rapid, temporary relief while steroids take effect.

5. **Croup vs. Epiglottitis Differentiation:**
   - **Croup:** Gradual onset, barking cough, low-grade fever, responsive to nebulized [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md)
   - **Epiglottitis:** Sudden onset, severe toxicity, high fever, tripod positioning, no cough, severe drooling - requires immediate advanced airway readiness

6. **Nebulized [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md) Specifics:**
   - Use **3 mg** of **1 mg/mL** [Epinephrine](../pharmacology/Epinephrine.md) solution (equivalent to 3 mL)
   - Can be repeated once if no improvement
   - Rebound stridor may occur after 1-2 hours - ensure appropriate transport and monitoring

7. **Do Not Examine the Oropharynx in Epiglottitis:** Visualizing the oropharynx may provoke laryngospasm and complete airway obstruction. If epiglottitis is suspected, avoid any unnecessary airway manipulation and prepare for immediate advanced airway management.

8. **Foreign Body Airway Obstruction:** If foreign body is suspected, follow appropriate choking protocol for age group. Blind finger sweeps are strictly prohibited - use direct visualization and Magill forceps only if trained.
