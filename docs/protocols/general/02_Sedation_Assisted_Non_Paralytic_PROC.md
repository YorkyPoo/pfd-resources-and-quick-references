# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Sedation Assisted (Non-Paralytic)

### Overview
Guidelines for the pharmacological facilitation of airway management and post-intubation sedation in patients who are not hypotensive.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Need for airway management / intubation
    - Intact gag reflex

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - Agitation
    - Hypoxia
    - Respiratory Failure

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Blood Pressure minimums:
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Infants 0 – 12 months: SBP > 70 mmHg
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Children 1 – 10 years: SBP > 80 mmHg
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Children & adults > 10 years: SBP > 100 mmHg
    - Asthma/COPD vs ACS

### Pearls
• ⚠ Concurrent use of **[[Fentanyl]]** and **[[Midazolam]]** significantly increases the risk of hypotension and respiratory depression.
• ⚠ **[[Ketamine]]** should be avoided in patients with suspected myocardial ischemia or infarction due to its sympathomimetic effects (increased heart rate and blood pressure).
• ⚠ Consider the use of lower doses of sedatives in frail, elderly, or opiate/benzodiazepine-naive patients.
• **[[Ketamine]]** is the preferred agent for sedation in patients with reactive airway disease (asthma/COPD) due to its bronchodilatory effects.
• Ensure adequate sedation is maintained during transport to prevent unplanned extubation or patient discomfort.
