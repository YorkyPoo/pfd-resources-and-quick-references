# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Behavioral Emergencies & Patient Restraint

### Overview
Guidelines for managing agitated or combative patients, emphasizing de-escalation, identifying underlying causes, and appropriate chemical restraint.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Psychiatric, medical, or traumatic emergency
    - Agitated, aggressive, or combative behavior
    - Risk of harm to self or others

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - **Agitation Levels:**
    - **Mild:** Anxious, apprehensive demeanor but non-aggressive
    - **Moderate:** Pulling at tubes/catheters or aggressive behavior without violence
    - **Severe:** Overt combativeness, violence, or self-harm / immediate danger to self or staff

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Potential medical or traumatic causes (SMASHED2)
    - Patients less than 25 kg who cannot be safely managed with de-escalation require Poquoson OMD / Medical Control guidance

### Pearls
1. ⚠ **Never place the patient prone** or in any position which compromises breathing, circulation, or constricts the neck or chest.
2. ⚠ **Patients in law enforcement custody** or under arrest must have a law enforcement officer present at all times.
3. ⚠ **Use of chemical restraint or sedating medications solely to facilitate an arrest by law enforcement is prohibited.**
4. Use of appropriate de-escalation techniques should take precedence over physical restraint or pharmacologic management whenever possible.
5. Mild agitation = anxious, apprehensive demeanor but non-aggressive.
6. Moderate agitation = pulling at tubes/catheters or aggressive behavior without violence.
7. Severe agitation = overt combativeness, violence or self-harm / immediate danger to self or staff.
8. Consider potential medical or traumatic causes (SMASHED2).
