# Poquoson Fire and Rescue Patient Care Protocols
## General - Pain Control

### Overview
Guidelines for assessing and managing pain using pharmacologic and non-pharmacologic methods while maintaining hemodynamic stability.

### Assessment & Criteria
=== "History of Present Illness"
    - Any patient with discomfort/pain caused by injury or illness
    - Assess pain using 0-10 scale
    - Use Wong-Baker (FACES) chart or FLACC scale for non-verbal patients (including infants and children)

=== "Signs & Symptoms"
    - **Blood Pressure Minimums** (Ensure patient is not hypotensive):
    - Infants 0–12 months: SBP > 70 mmHg
    - Children 1–10 years: SBP > 80 mmHg
    - Children & Adults > 10 years: SBP > 100 mmHg

=== "Differential Considerations"
    - Opiate side effects (respiratory depression, CNS depression, bradycardia, hypotension)
    - Associated factors affecting hemodynamics (trauma, intoxication, illness)
    - Nausea

### Pearls
* ⚠ Opiates cause respiratory/CNS depression, bradycardia, and/or hypotension - monitor vitals closely following any administration.
* ⚠ Use lower opiate doses (**25 mcg [Fentanyl](../../pharmacology/Fentanyl.md)** or **2.5 mg Morphine**) for frail, low weight (<50 kg), opiate naïve, or elderly (> 65 yr) adult patients.
* ⚠ Administer **[Naloxone](../../pharmacology/Naloxone.md)** if respiratory depression occurs. Refer to Overdose / Poisoning / Toxic Ingestions Poquoson Protocol.
* The goal of analgesia is not to completely alleviate pain, but to reduce pain to a manageable level which allows for safe and humane treatment and transport.
