PERSONNEL POLICY
City of Poquoson, Virginia

Policy Title: General Provisions Effective Date:
Section No.: 1 Supersedes Policy:

ACCESSIBILITY OF MANUAL:

Copies of the City of Poquoson Personnel Manual shall be made available on the City’s website
and a hard copy provided to each Department Head to be readily available for review by any
employee.

PURPOSE OF THESE RULES:

These rules set forth the principles and procedures to be followed by the City of Poquoson in the
administration of personnel policies. The objective of these rules is to provide reasonable, fair,
and equitable treatment for all City employees and to ensure that the citizens served by the City
derive the benefits and advantages which can be expected from a competent staff of City
employees.

POSITIONS COVERED BY THESE RULES:

These rules shall apply to all positions and offices in the City’s merit system service. The
following exceptions are not covered:

Members of City Council

City Manager

City Attorney

Contractual Employees

Appointed Members of Boards or Commissions
Constitutional Officers

Registrar

AMENDMENT AND REVISION OF RULES:

Amendments and revisions of these rules shall be initiated by the City Manager, with
recommendations made to the City Council for adoption.

APPLICATION OF THE RULES:

All positions existing and hereinafter created within the City’s service, except those listed above,
are placed within the jurisdiction of the City and shall be subject to the rules and procedures as

are indicated in this Manual, or as set forth by action of the City Council. This document is not
to be construed as creating a contract or expectation of employment for a definite term. An
employee is free to terminate employment at any time, preferably with notice, for any reason.

IMPLEMENTATION:

The responsibility for implementing the provisions of this Manual is hereby vested in the City
Manager, or his/her designee, except as specifically designated within the rules.

SAVINGS CLAUSE:

If any section or part of a section of these rules is held by any Court or legislation to be invalid or
unconstitutional, the same shal! not invalidate or impair the validity, force and effect of any other
section or part of a section of these rules unless it clearly appears that such other section or part
of a section is wholly or necessarily dependent for its operation upon a section or part of a
section so held invalid or unconstitutional.

Policy Title: Definitions Effective Date:
Section No.: 2 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

As used in these policies, unless the context clearly requires otherwise, the words
included in these definitions shall have the meaning herein given to them.
DEFINITIONS:

Allocate

The act of assigning each position to its proper classification.

Anniversary Date

The recurring date of original appointment or the date of promotion, except that such
shall be adjusted for suspensions, unauthorized leaves of absence and separation and re-
employment.

Appeal

An application for review of an alleged grievance by disciplinary action.
Appointment

The offer and acceptance by a person of a position either on a full-time, part-time,
temporary or seasonal basis. A temporary appointment shall terminate automatically
upon completion of the project necessitating the appointment.

Class/Classification

A position or group of positions having similar duties and responsibilities, requiring

similar qualifications, which can be properly designated by one title indicative of the
nature of the work and which carry the same salary range.

Class Specification

A written statement describing the duties and responsibilities and minimum qualification
requirements of a position.

Continuous Service

Employment in the classified service which is uninterrupted except for authorized
leave(s) of absence.

Demotion

The assignment of an employee to a position in a lower class.

Exempt Employee — Fair Labor Standards Act

Exempt employees generally fall into three major categories: executive, administrative,
and professional. To qualify, employecs must meet prescribed requirements regarding
salary, duties and responsibilities. These employees are not entitled to overtime
compensation.

Full-Time Permanent Position

A full-time permanent position is defined as a position in which the incumbent works a
full-time, 35 hour per week schedule for every seven calendar day period on a continuous
basis. Full-time permanent positions have no time limitation with respect to the duration
of job assignment. Full-time permanent employees are eligible to receive full fringe
benefits.

Grievance

A grievance shall be a complaint or dispute relative to an employee’s employment.

Lay-off

The involuntary, non-disciplinary separation of an employee from employment with the
City.

Non-Exempt Employee — Fair Labor Standards Act

Non-exempt employees are all employees that are not classified as “exempt” and who
must be compensated for all hours of work in excess of their identified work week.

Overtime

Overtime occurs when a non-exempt employee works more than 40 hours within a work
week in accordance with State and Federal law at a rate of time and a half. For
Policemen and Firemen overtime calculations will be performed in accordance with the
Fair Labor Standards Act as it pertains specifically to employees whose work period is
defined in Section 207(k) of the Act.

Part-Time Permanent Position

A part-time permanent position is defined as a position in which the incumbent works
less than a 30 hour per week schedule on a part-time, continuous basis. Hours will be
calculated on an annual basis over a 12-month period. Part-time permanent positions
have no time limitation with respect to the duration of job assignment. Part-time
permanent employees are not eligible to receive fringe benefits.

Temporary Part-Time Position

A temporary part-time position is defined as a position in which the incumbent works less
than 30 hours per week for a maximum period not to exceed twelve (12) months.
Temporary employees are not eligible to receive fringe benefits.

Seasonal Position

A seasonal position is short-term not to exceed four (4) months such as a lifeguard at the
Municipal pool. Hours worked are “‘as necessary and appropriate”.

Position

A group of duties assigned to one person or job.

Probationary Employee

Any employee who is serving a probationary period following original appointment
(hiring) or following promotion, prior to being regularly appointed to a regular position
and class in the classified service, with City Manager’s approval.

Probationary Period - New Hires

A period of twelve (12) months following hiring whereby the employee’s performance is
carefully evaluated in order to attain regular status. At any time during the probationary
period, the Department Head and City Manager may terminate the employee. Any

termination prior to expiration of the probationary period shall be final with no right of
appeal and with no severance compensation due to the employee.

Probationary Period - Promotions

A period of six (6) months following a promotion during which the employee’s
performance is carefully evaluated in order to attain regular status. At any time during
the probationary period, the Department Head and City Manager may return the
employee to their pre-promotional position based on unsatisfactory performance.
Probationary Period — Unsatisfactory Performance

A twelve (12) month period whereby an employee whose overall annual performance
evaluation is rated unsatisfactory or needs improvement. Should the employee receive a
second consecutive annual performance evaluation with an overall rating of needs
improvement or unsatisfactory, the employee’s service with the City shall be terminated
upon recommendation of the Department Head and with the concurrence of the City
Manager.

Promotion

The assignment of an employee to a position in a higher class than the position from
which the assignment is made.

Transfer

The voluntary or involuntary change of an employee’s assignment from one position to
another.

Work Day

The scheduled number of hours an employee is assigned to work in a 24-hour period,
which may vary depending on position.

Work Cycle

The scheduled number of hours an employee is assigned to work during a work cycle, for
which the employee receives a paycheck. This may also be known as the Pay Period.

Work Week
The scheduled number of hours an employee is assigned to work during a calendar week.
Vacancy

A position existing or newly created, which is not occupied, and for which funds are
available,

The official personnel file shall be the only file which is to be considered official and

The access, dissemination and purging of information contained within the file shall be in
accordance with the Privacy Protection Act of 1976, as amended.

The following individuals shall be designated as having regular access to the official
personnel files:

The City Manager or his/her designee.

The members of any Grievance panel.

The Virginia Employment Commission Unemployment Compensation Division.
Federal, State or local agencies to create additional personnel records after
employment.

5. Federal, State or local law enforcement agencies during the investigation of a
violation or potential violation of the law.

EMPLOYEE CLASSIFICATIONS:

Employment classifications are based on job assignments and responsibilities. They are
used to promote consistency and uniformity. Poquoson has adopted a comprehensive
position Classification Plan and Pay Schedule which encompasses all full-time regular,
and regular part-time positions within the City.

The Position Classification Plan groups into classes those positions which are essentially
similar in type of work performed, difficulty, and responsibility, so as to warrant being
placed in the same pay range. The Classification Plan provides a list of job titles,
descriptions of the nature and requirements of the work in each class, and the allocation
of specific positions to the appropriate classes. The job titles as listed in the
Classification Plan will be used in all personnel records and transactions. It is the duty of
the City Manager to amend the classification specifications when necessary so that
specifications for each class will accurately reflect the current duties and responsibilities
of the position. If a Department Head or individual employee feels that the Classification
Plan is inaccurate or that a position is improperly classified, the problem should be
directed to the attention of the City Manager. The City Manager may then make a further
investigation of the matter and take appropriate action.

RECLASSIFICATIONS:

Reclassification can occur in several ways. The most common is the reclassification of
an entire class of workers. Reclassification can also take place when the work performed
on a particular job changes substantially over a period of time. Often these changes take
place when new technology is introduced, or a mission of a department is changed in
response to changing legislation. A third type of reclassification is done for individuals
whose jobs change through design or evolution. Reclassification is generally initiated
through independent, outside review, but can be initiated at the request of the individual,
supervisor, Department Head or City Manager.

If an individual, supervisor, or Department Head initiates a request for a reclassification,
it will be accompanied by written supporting documentation. Reclassifications do not
take place independent of consideration of other positions within the overall classification
system.

When a request for reclassification is received a Position Analysis Questionnaire (PAQ)
should be completed by the incumbent(s) under review. Key questions concerning
critical job factors should be answered to assist in determining the proper level within the
overall pay and classification system. The analysis of all the information is then
compared to the current position descriptions for the various classification levels and
weighed to determine the correct classification assignment. If it is determined that a
reclassification of an individual is justified, the City Manager will direct appropriate
action to finalize the request.

UPGRADES:
An employee whose position is reclassified to a higher grade shall receive an increase in

pay per grade, with a maximum of 10 percent increase, or the beginning pay of the new
salary range, whichever is greater in accordance with the schedule below:

Grade Increase Salary Increase Percentage
1 2.5
2 5.0
3 7.5
4 or more 10

In cases where an employee would be reclassified from non-exempt to exempt status
additional flexibility in pay may be granted by the City Manager.

DOWNGRADES:

An employee whose position is reclassified to a lower grade shall retain his/her current
rate of pay if it falls within the salary range of the new position.

An employee whose position is reclassified to a lower grade and whose salary is above
the range of the new position shall retain his/her current rate of pay for a period of not
more than six (6) months. At the end of the six (6) month period, the employee’s salary
shall be adjusted to the end of the range of the new position.

PAY PLAN:

Poquoson has adopted a pay plan wherein jobs are graded and reviewed periodically.
When an employee is hired by the City he/she should understand that the City agrees to
review his/her job and pay status within a classification periodically. Further information
regarding the Pay Plan and the employee’s status therein may be obtained from the City
Manager’s office.

It is the policy of the City of Poquoson not to make advances on employee compensation.
The exception would be only for extreme emergencies with prior written approval of the
City Manager.

Policy Title: Code of Ethics Effective Date:
Section No.: 4 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to establish a written Code of Ethics
Policy to serve as a guide for employee conduct in supporting the City mission of
providing outstanding, professional public service to the residents and businesses of our
community.

SCOPE:

This policy applies to all City of Poquoson permanent full-time, permanent part-time and
seasonal part-time employees.

CODE OF ETHICS
Employees of the City of Poquoson shall be dedicated to the concepts of effective and
democratic local government and the essential partnership between responsible elected

officials and dedicated staff.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall always conduct themselves, on and off duty, in
a manner that positively reflects on themselves and the organization.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall accept responsibility for their actions and
inactions and for the consequences thereof.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall support the concept of faimess and the value of
diverse thoughts and opinions.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall avoid situations that would adversely affect
their credibility or the credibility of the organization.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall be truthful and honest at all times.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall conduct their personal affairs in a manner that
does not improperly influence the performance of their official duties or bring discredit to
the organization.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall recognize that they serve in a position of public
trust that requires stewardship in the honest and efficient use of publicly owned resources
and that these resources are to be protected from misuse, abuse and theft.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall demonstrate professionalism, integrity and
competence in the performance of their duties and use information obtained by virtue of
their position only to the benefit of the community.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall avoid financial investment, outside
employment, outside business interests and activities that create the perception of
impropriety.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall not propose or accept personal rewards, special
privileges, gifts or other benefits that may create a conflict of interest, or the appearance
thereof.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall not engage in activities involving alcohol or
other substance use or abuse that will impair their ability to perform official duties or
compromise the safety of others.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall treat all residents, visitors and fellow employees
with respect and in a non-discriminatory manner.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall not harass, intimidate or threaten any person
and shall promptly report persons engaging in such behavior.

Employees of the City of Poquoson shall responsibly use social networking, electronic
communication or other media in a manner that does not discredit, dishonor or embarrass
the organization and promptly report such activities by fellow employees.

Policy Title: Americans With Disabilities Act Effective Date:
Section No.: 6 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to comply with the provisions of the
Americans With Disabilities Act (ADA).

SCOPE:

This policy applies to all City of Poquoson employees and applicants for employment.
DEFINITIONS:

Americans With Disabilities Act (ADA)

The Americans With Disabilities Act is defined as the federal law that prohibits
discrimination against a qualified individual with a disability who can perform the
essential functions of a position, with or without accommodation.

Disability

A disability is defined as a temporary or permanent physical or mental impairment that
substantially limits one or more major life activities (i.e., sitting, standing, lifting,
reaching, hearing, speaking, seeing, walking, breathing, learning, caring for oneself or
working).

Essential Functions

Essential functions are defined as primary job duties that are fundamental to a position.
Reasonable Accommodation

Reasonable accommodation is defined as a modification to the application process, a job,

or work environment that permits a qualified applicant or employee with a disability to
participate in the job application process, to perform the essential functions of a job, or to

partake of the same benefits and privileges of employment as are enjoyed by employees
without disabilities.

Undue Hardship

Undue hardship is defined as any accommodation that would be unduly costly, extensive,
substantial or disruptive or would fundamentally alter the nature of operation of the
City’s business.

GENERAL PROVISIONS:

Qualified Individual

An individual is entitled to employment rights under the ADA if he/she is a qualified
individual with a disability. A qualified individual with a disability

1. possesses the skills, education, employment experience and/or licenses
required for a particular job and

2. is able to perform the essential functions of a particular job.
Disability
Under the ADA, a person has a disability if he/she:

1. has a temporary or permanent physical or mental impairment that substantially
limits a major life activity;

2. has a record of such an impairment; or

3. is regarded as having such an impairment.
Reasonable Accommodation
Reasonable accommodation is any change or adjustment to a job or work environment
that permits a qualified applicant or employee with a disability to participate in the job
application process, perform essential functions of a job, or enjoy benefits and privileges
of employment equal to those enjoyed by employees without disabilities.

1. A reasonable accommodation may include:

a. acquiring or modifying equipment or devices;

b. job restructuring;

c. part-time or modified work schedules,

d. reassignment to a vacant position;
e. adjusting or modifying examinations, training material or policies;
f. providing readers and interpreters; and

g. making the workplace readily accessible to and usable by people with
disabilities.

2. Identifying a Reasonable Accommodation
The process of determining an appropriate reasonable accommodation is
an interactive, problem solving technique involving both the employer and the

qualified individual with a disability which involves:

a. analyzing the particular job involved and determining its purpose and essential
functions;

b. determining the precise job-related limitations imposed by the individual’s
disability and how those limitations could be overcome with a reasonable
accommodation; and

c. identifying potential accommodations and assessing the effectiveness each
would have in enabling the individual to perform the essential functions of
the position.

Undue Hardship

|. The ADA does not require the provision of a reasonable accommodation if doing
so would cause an undue hardship.

2. Factors considered in determining whether an accommodation constitutes an
undue hardship include:

a. the cost of the accommodation;

b. the City’s size;

c. financial resources; and

d. the nature and structure of the City’s operation.

3. The City Manager shall make final decisions regarding reasonable
accommodation and undue hardship.

Policy Title: Recruitment, Hiring & Promotion Effective Date:
Section No.: 7 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

The objective of the City of Poquoson is to provide for an efficient and effective hiring
process that promotes equal employment opportunity and ensures a qualified workforce.

SCOPE:
This policy applies to all City of Poquoson full-time and permanent part-time positions.
RECRUITMENT PROCEDURES:

Department Heads wishing to initiate a hiring process shall submit a written request to
initiate recruitment with the City Manager for all permanent positions. Part-time
seasonal positions are exempt from this requirement, shall be initiated by the Department
Head and shall require external recruitment in all cases. The request shall detail the
necessity for filling the position, proposed recruitment process, including a request for
internal, external and promotional recruitment, proposed interview questions, selection
panel and any special selection processes such as physical agility testing, medical
examination, credit check, polygraph examinations, etc. appropriate to the position. All
testing procedures shall be consistent with State and Federal regulations concerning
employee selection. The costs of any pre-employment testing shall be borne by the City
of Poquoson.

The City Manager will approve or modify the request to initiate the hiring process.

Positions approved for external recruitment shall be advertised for a minimum of two
weeks and shall be advertised in a local newspaper, the City website and the Virginia
Employment Commission. In no case shall an initial position application period be
established as “until filled.” The second advertisement for the same position shall be
classified as “open until filled” if approved by the City Manager.

Positions approved for internal recruitment shall be advertised to all City employees
through inclusion in the City Manager’s weekly report to the employees. Notices for

promotional recruitment shall be provided to each member of the hiring department. In
both cases the minimum advertisement period shall be one week.

SELECTION PROCEDURES:

All persons applying for a vacant City position shall complete the appropriate City of
Poquoson Employment Application. Applications shall be accepted for posted vacancies
only. Falsification or omission of required information shall result in immediate
disqualification of the applicant and/or termination of employment. Applicants for
permanent City positions must be at least 18 years of age with the exception of applicants
for sworn law enforcement positions who must be at least 21 years old. Complete
applications must be received by the City Manager’s Office by the close of business on
the application period’s closing date.

The selection committee shall carefully review each application for employment.
Applications shall be evaluated and ranked based upon the qualifications, knowledge,
skills and ability necessary to perform those duties outlined for the position. Those
applicants whose qualifications, knowledge, skills and abilities most closely match those
established for the position shall be invited for an interview. In addition, applicants who
are residents of the City of Poquoson whose qualifications, knowledge, skills and abilities
meet or exceed those established for the position shall also be invited to interview. The
minimum number of persons to be interviewed shall be three.

All interview questions must be approved by the City Manager as part of the approved
request to initiate recruitment so as to ensure that there are no violations of the Equal
Employment Opportunity (EEO) laws.

When evaluating equally qualified applicants, preference shall be given to City residents.
HIRING PROCEDURES:

The Hiring Department Head shall prepare a written hiring recommendation for
consideration by the City Manager. The report shall include the number of applications
received, number of persons interviewed, the recommendation of the selection
committee, a summary of applicant qualifications and reference checks, the results of any
position specific additional testing as approved by the City Manager as part of the hiring
process, proposed starting date and proposed hiring salary including justification for any
proposed salary above entry level. All relevant testing and background checks shall be
completed prior to presentation to the City Manager.

It shall be the policy of the City of Poquoson to hire new employees at the entry level
salary for the position; however, initial compensation may be granted higher into the
range on a case-by-case basis based upon the qualifications of the successful applicant,
market conditions and the specific need to the City, upon approval of the City Manager.
For the purposes of determining compensation relating to a promotion the employee shall
be entitled to a 5% pay increase or the beginning compensation level of the new pay

grade, whichever is higher. The City Manager may approve a higher promotional
increase on a case-by-case basis depending upon the qualifications of the successful
applicant, market conditions and the specific need to the City.

Upon approval of the City Manager, the Department Head shall make a written offer of
employment to the successful applicant. The applicant shall have five work days from
the date of receipt to accept the offer in writing. Failure of the applicant to accept the
offer within the five working day period shall result in the offer being withdrawn.

NOTIFICATION OF UNSUCCESSFUL APPLICANT PROCEDURE:

Following acceptance of the position by the successful applicant, the Department Head
shall notify in writing each unsuccessful applicant. Applications shall be maintained and
considered active for 90 days following the date of acceptance.

RESIDENCY REQUIREMENT:

As a condition of employment, all persons hired into the position of Fire Chief, Police
Chief or Assistant City Manager shall establish and maintain residency within the City of
Poquoson within one year of employment. The establishment of residency shall be a
prerequisite for the completion of the probationary period. For all other Department Head
level positions the establishing and maintaining residency within the City of Poquoson is
strongly preferred.

REPORTING FOR DUTY:

New employees shall report to their Department Head on the established start date and
time. The Department Head shall ensure that the new employee schedules a convenient
time to meet with the Benefits Administrator to complete all necessary employment-
related forms.

HIRING FORMER EMPLOYEES:
A former employee may be rehired providing that all of the following conditions are met:

e His/her qualifications meet the requirements of the vacant position.
e He/she had been an employee in good standing at the time of separation.
e His/her work record as a City employee is determined by the City Manager to be
satisfactory.
A former employee who is rehired after a break in service of more than 30 consecutive
calendar days shall be considered a new hire for the purposes of employment benefits and
shall be required to serve a new hire probationary period.

A former employee who is rehired after a break in service of less than 31 consecutive
calendar days may, at the discretion of the City Manager, be reinstated without a break in

service with regard to benefits status and seniority; however, the employee shall be
required to serve a new hire probationary period.

HIRING OF FAMILY MEMBERS:

The City Manager may select an individual for emplacement who has a relative(s)
employed by the City provided that the hiring decision would not result in the new
employee being under the supervision of a family member. Further, at no time during
employment may an employee supervise a member of their family.

RELATIVE:

A relative is defined as the employee’s spouse; children, including step-children and
foster children; parents, including step-parents; parents-in-law; grandparents;
grandparents-in-law; sons-in-law; daughters-in-law; grandchildren; brothers, including
step-brothers; brothers-in-law; sisters, including step-sisters; sisters-in-law; and/or any
relative, either by blood or marriage, living in the employee’s household.

HIRING OF PART-TIME EMPLOYEES:

Part-time regular employces shall not be entitled to benefits offered to full-time
employees. Temporary part-time employees, also known as seasonal employees, are
recruited to assist with handling limited duration work and shall not accrue job rights or
grievance rights.

OPERATORS OF CITY-OWNED MOTOR VEHICLES:

Any employee whose position description requires operation of a City-owned motor
vehicle shall possess and maintain at all times the appropriate Virginia driver’s license.

EMPLOYMENT SERVICES PROHIBITED:

The City shall not pay placement fees associated with any full-time or permanent part-
time position.

ACTING PAY:

From time-to-time the City Manager may determine that it is in the best interest of the
City to designate an existing employee to serve in an acting capacity during a vacancy
period. In this instance the City Manager shall establish a temporary rate of pay
equivalent to a 5% pay increase or the beginning pay range of the position filled,
whichever is higher. Acting pay shall be effective at the beginning of the next month
following designation and shall be granted only in cases where the duration of the
vacancy is anticipated to be 30 calendar days or longer.

PROMOTION:

Promotions may be either inter- or intra-departmental and must be approved by the
Department Head(s) and the City Manager. All promotions shall be based on merit. A
promotion may take the form of a transfer from one department to another.

DEMOTION:

A demotion is the assignment of an employee to a position in a lower classification
having a lower maximum salary.

The City Manager shall have the authority to demote an employee. If it becomes
necessary to recommend demotion of an employee, the Department Head should make
the recommendation to the City Manager, in writing, citing the reasons. The causes for
demotion shall normally be due to the removal of higher-level duties and responsibilities
or the inability of the employee to assume or perform duties of the position, although the
City reserves the right to demote an employee for disciplinary, non-disciplinary or any
other reasons.

Employees demoted for reasons of reduction-in-force initiated to increase efficiency or to
meet budget restrictions should be assigned at the same dollar level in the new
classification range. Should the present salary of the employee exceed the maximum of
the assigned range, the employee’s salary should be capped or frozen at the top of the
range, with continued eligibility for any cost-of-living raises granted to general
employees.

An employee not on a probationary status in the position from which he has been
demoted has the right to appeal demotions through the grievance procedure as outlined in
the Policy Manual.

An employee who has not completed his/her probationary period prior to the demotion
will be required to serve a new probationary period. Regular employees may be required
to serve a new probationary period upon the recommendation of the Department Head.

LATERAL TRANSFER

A lateral transfer is the switching of an employee to a job on an equal level in another
department. An employee may submit a written request for consideration for a transfer to
another department or to another section within the department, if a position is vacant. A
transfer must be approved by the affected Department Head(s) and the City Manager.
Employee requests for transfer to another department shall be made, in writing, to the
Department Head with a copy to the City Manager.

The City Manager may transfer an employee temporarily, or permanently, when said
transfer is deemed to be in the best interests of the City following determination that the
employee possesses the minimum knowledge, skills and abilities (KSAs) for the new job
classification for which they are being considered.

NON-LATERAL TRANSFER

There are two types of non-lateral transfers, both involving reduction in classification
level: voluntary and involuntary.

Voluntary Transfer

An employee who requests a voluntary transfer to another position which involves a
reduction in classification levels should, with the approval of the City Manager, be placed
in the new range at the same step or percentage into the new range at his/her present level
in the former pay grade. If the employee’s present salary is above the maximum salary
level for the range, the salary of the employee should be reduced to the maximum salary
level indicated by the new range.

Involuntary Transfer

There are two types of involuntary transfer: one is for the good of the employee and the
other serves the best interest of the department. An example of a transfer for the good of
the employee would be an individual who is no longer physically capable of performing
his/her duties, and a reasonable accommodation cannot be made to allow the individual to
continue to perform the essential functions of the current position. Rather than
terminating such an employee, the department may determine that an involuntary transfer
to a less strenuous position would be acceptable. In this instance, should the transfer be
to a position classified at a lower level, the employee’s salary would be reduced to the
same percentage into the new range as he/she was in the former pay range.

Involuntary transfers for the best interest of the City are initiated by the City rather than
by the employee. Transfers may be of a temporary nature to address a particular problem
such as a personnel shortage. Assignments usually exceed thirty (30) days in length and
should not be confused with the routine assumption of higher level duties which occurs in
the absence of a supervisor on personal leave. Employees assigned, through this type of
involuntary transfer to a lower classification level, should not be penalized by a reduction
of the current salary. However, if the current salary is higher than the maximum amount
of the new pay range, the salary would be frozen until the range would be adjusted to
allow for an increase.

Section No: 9 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to provide convenient and consistent
hours of operation for citizens to transact business with the City while allowing its

SCOPE:

This policy applies to all permanent full-time and permanent part-time employees.
DEFINITIONS:

Work Week

A work week is defined as seven consecutive calendar days; currently identified as
Friday through Thursday.

Full-Time Work Week

A full-time work week is defined as a work week consisting of 30 or more hours.
Permanent Part-Time Work Week

A part-time work week is defined as a work week consisting of less than 30 hours.
Routine Work Schedule

A routine work schedule is defined as a work week where work hours are the same for
each of the days worked as determined by the City Manager. The routine work week

schedule hours for City employees are 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. with a one hour paid lunch
break. Short-term variations can be approved by the Department Head.

Alternative Work Schedule

An Alternative Work Schedule is defined as a work week that diverges from the routine
work schedule which has been approved by the City Manager as permanent.

Compressed Work Schedule

A compressed work week is defined as an alternate work schedule that allows an
employee to work a full-time schedule in fewer than five (5) days during a seven (7) day
work period or fewer than ten (10) days during a fourteen (14) day work period, by
working longer hours each day. Permanent, compressed work schedules shall be
approved by the City Manager. Whenever a non-exempt employee participates in a
compressed work week schedule, the department head shall be responsible for ensuring
that applicable FLSA regulations and personnel policies and procedures are strictly
adhered to.

Certain employees in the Fire and Police Departments are assigned to a compressed work
schedule due to the nature of their positions. These employees include those permanent,
full-time public safety employees who are primarily responsible for responding to
emergencies, answering 911 calls or work in positions where the compressed work
schedule meets the operational needs of the department.

Flexible Work Schedule

Department Heads have authority to implement flexible work schedules.

Non-exempt Employee

A non-exempt employee is defined as an employee who is subject to the overtime
provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act.

Exempt Employee

An exempt employee is defined as an employee who is not subject to the overtime
provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act.

WORK SCHEDULES

Routine Work Schedules

1. Full-time employees shall work a regularly scheduled work week consisting of 30
hours or more, depending upon their specific job positions.

2. Department Heads/Constitutional Officers may establish specific work hours to
meet the requirements of their departments. Any adjustment from the routine
work schedule that affects service to the public must be approved by the City
Manager.

Alternative Work Schedules

With the approval of the City Manager, Department Heads/Constitutional Officers may
implement an Alternative Work Schedule for an employee, or a group of employees, that
does not affect service to the public or the department’s customers. The approval process
is as follows:

!. An employee or group of employees wishing to obtain an alternative work
schedule shall make written request to his/her immediate supervisor for review
and approval or disapproval. A non-exempt employee shall not be permitted to
request an alternative work schedule that exceeds 40 work hours within a work
week.

2. Upon approval of the request by the immediate supervisor, the request will be
submitted by the supervisor to the Department Head/Constitutional Officer for
approval or disapproval.

3. Upon approval of the request by the Department Head/Constitutional Officer, the
request is submitted to the City Manager for approval or disapproval.

4. Once a determination is made by the City Manager, the alternative work schedule
request will be returned to the Department Head/Constitutional Officer for
implementation, revision, or dismissal of the request.

An approved, alternative work schedule will be implemented at an agreed upon date
between the Department Head/Constitutional Officer and the employee or group of
employees, which must be at the beginning of a forthcoming pay cycle.

Department Heads/Constitutional Officers shall have final authority to resolve any
matters that occur as a result of implementation of an alternative work schedule to ensure
effective service to the public and the department’s customers.

Termination of an alternative work schedule may occur in any of the following manners:

1. Department Heads/Constitutional Officers may terminate an alternative work
schedule at their discretion by providing a written notice to the employee or group
of employees at least two weeks in advance, except in the case of a department
emergency.

2. Anemployee must provide a written request, at least two weeks in advance, to the
immediate supervisor to terminate an alternative work schedule.

3. Emergency requests for termination of an alternative work schedule initiated by
the employee shall be considered by the immediate supervisor and Department
Head/Constitutional Officer on the merits of each request.

4. Anemployee’s or group of employees’ return to a routine work schedule must
occur at the beginning of a forthcoming pay cycle.

Participants of an alternative work schedule shall comply with all applicable statutes,
rules, policies and procedures. Failure to do so may result in termination of the
alternative work schedule for that participant and may include disciplinary action.

Attendance

1. Employees are required to adhere to their work schedules by reporting to work at
the specified starting time, departing at the specified ending time, and taking
lunch periods and breaks as authorized.

2. Should an illness or personal emergency prevent an employee from reporting for
work or result in a later report time, the employee is expected to notify his/her
supervisor prior to the employce starting time, or as soon as possible thereafter.

Breaks

1. Breaks are not mandated by law and are granted at the discretion of the
Department Head/Constitutional Officer.

2. A one hour lunch is recommended for full-time employees during their work day.
3. Breaks cannot be used to change an employee’s start or ending time.

4. Breaks cannot be combined with an employee’s lunch break to extend lunch
privileges.

PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to provide eligible employees with

Departmental schedules should be prepared to provide for the completion of work tasks

assigned to the department.

DEFINITIONS:
Exempt Employee

An exempt employee is defined as an employee who is not subject to the overtime
provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act. The City Manager shall have the authority to
provide reasonable and commensurate work schedule flexibility, when not in conflict
with the essential work and delivery of public services of the City, to salaried personnel
whose duties and responsibilities regularly require substantial hours of work beyond a
normal workweek.

Non-Exempt Employee

A non-exempt employee is defined as an employee who is subject to the overtime
provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act.

AUTHORIZATION REQUIRED:

Overtime hours are permissible only by an actual emergency, or preapproved and
scheduled overtime for such other unusual circumstance, requiring the assignment of
employees to overtime hours of work, certified by the Department Head and approved by
the City Manager. The only exception to prior approval shall be in emergency situations

staffing level. For purposes of this section, the term “emergency” shall be construed to

mean circumstances including, but not limited to, civil disturbances, natural disasters
such as hurricanes, or a significant shortage of personnel.

OVERTIME/COMPENSATORY TIME CALCULATION:

All hourly employees and employees who are non-exempt from the provisions of the Fair
Labor Standards Act (FLSA) shall be compensated for overtime at one and one-half (I-
1/2) times the regular rate of pay for that employee. The regular rate is the equivalent
hourly rate at which the employee is actually paid for normal, non-overtime hours.
Compensatory time at the rate of one and one-half (1-l/2) times base pay, in lieu of paid
overtime, may be granted upon request of the employee, subject to approval of the
Department Head and City Manager. Accrual of compensatory leave shall be governed
by the provisions of FLSA. Accrued compensatory time will be cleared out annually
(June of each year) and the employee compensated for said time at their current hourly
rate of pay.

Hours worked includes all time that an employee is required to be on duty and all time
that the employee is permitted to work. Annual leave, sick leave, bereavement leave,
compensatory time, holiday leave and workers’ compensation hours are not counted as
hours worked even though they are paid hours except for eligible employees of the
uniformed services (Police, Fire, Public Works and Utilities) whose total compensable
hours shall be used for the basis of overtime calculation. This does not apply for
emergencies or scheduled events such as the bulky item disposal services. Lunch breaks
where the employee is completely relieved of his/her duties for at least sixty (60)
uninterrupted minutes are also not counted as hours worked. Employees violating this
policy will be subject to appropriate disciplinary action. Except as otherwise provided by
law, exempt employees required to work during declared emergencies shall receive one
hour of compensatory time for each hour worked.

ACCUMULATION OF COMPENSATORY LEAVE:

e When an employee accumulates one hundred and twenty (120) hours or more of
compensatory leave (two hundred and forty (240) hours for law
enforcement/public safety personnel), the Department Head/Constitutional
Officer and employee shall develop a plan to reduce/eliminate the accrued
compensatory leave which shall be approved by the City Manager.

e Employees may not accrue more than two hundred and forty (240) hours of
compensatory time during a calendar year (four hundred and eighty (480) for law
enforcement/public safety personnel).

TREATMENT OF COMPENSATORY LEAVE UPON CHANGE IN STATUS:

Change from Non-Exempt Status to Exempt Status

e Upon change from non-exempt to exempt status, employees shall be paid a lump
sum for the unused portion of their accrued compensatory leave.

¢ The lump sum payment shall be calculated based on the non-exempt salary rate.

e Upon change from non-exempt to exempt status or transfer to a new department,
employees shall be paid the lump sum for the unused portion of their accrued
compensatory leave by their previous department.

Separation

Upon separation, employees shall be paid a lump sum for the unused portion of their
accrued compensatory leave balances.

Employees who have been notified at the time that the request for leave is made that they

FMLA Leave Events:

e when the employee is unable to work because of the serious health condition of

e the employee is needed to care for an employee’s spouse, son, daughter, persons

e the birth of, or to care for, an employee's son, daughter or person under their

care (provided that the leave is requested and used within 12 months following

e a “qualifying exigency” arises due to the employee’s spouse, son, daughter,
person under their guardianship, or parent being on active duty and is called for
deployment or is notified of an order to report to active duty in the armed forces
(including the Reserves and National Guard) in support of a “contingency
operation”; or

e when the employee is needed to care for a spouse, son, daughter, parent, person
under their guardianship or next of kin who is a service member undergoing
medical treatment, recuperation or therapy, is on outpatient status, or is on the
temporary disabled retired list for a serious illness or injury.

Leave for a Serious Health Condition

A serious health condition is an illness, injury, impairment or physical or mental
condition that involves inpatient care in a hospital, hospice, or residential medical care
facility, or an incapacity lasting more than 3 consecutive days and requiring continuing
treatment by a health care provider. Continuing treatment involves 2 or more treatments
(or 1 treatment when the condition is such that continuing follow-up is or will be
required) by a healthcare provider. Treatment could also be for pregnancy, prenatal care,
chronic or long-term serious health conditions, or hospice care.

To qualify for leave due to the serious health condition of:

e a family member or member under the employee’s guardianship — the family
member or person under guardianship, must be incapable of self-care (e.g. the
individual requires active assistance or supervision in daily self-care or needs
psychological support);

e the employee — the employee must be unable to work at all or unable to perform
any of the essential functions of the employee’s position.

Leave for Exigency Circumstances

The following are the exigency circumstances recognized under the law: short notice
deployment, military events, urgent need for child care or unexpected school activities,
financial arrangements, counseling, rest and relaxation, and certain post deployment
activities. The exigency circumstances should be stated in the deployment or call to
active duty notice.

Leave to Care for Injured Service Member

Leave can be taken to care for a parent, son, daughter, spouse, person under the
guardianship of the employee, or closest next of kin who is on active duty and
undergoing medical treatment, who is receiving recuperation, therapy, or who is on the
temporary disability retirement list. Leave can be taken to care for more than 1 member.

Amount of Leave
An eligible employee is entitled to use the following amounts of FMLA leave as follows:
e¢ Employee/Family Member or Person Under the Employee’s Gurdianship with A
Serious Health Condition: 12 weeks during a rolling 12-month period beginning

on the first day leave is taken and looking back over the past 12 months.

e Exigency Circumstances: 12 weeks during a rolling 12-month period beginning
on the first day leave is taken and looking back over the past 12 months.

e Care for Injured Service Member: 26 weeks to care for an incapacitated service
member during the 12-month period beginning with the first day of leave and
ending 12 months later.

e Birth, Placement, Adoption: 12 weeks in the 12 months following the birth of a
son or daughter or the placement of a son or daughter for adoption or foster care.

Except for leave taken for the serious health condition of the employee or for exigent
circumstances, a husband and wife working for the same employer may not collectively
use more than the allotment allowed if only one had been employed by the City (e.g. 12
or 26 weeks for both together).

When a request for leave is made, or the employee is absent for one of the qualifying
events, the City will provide the employee with an Eligibility Notice.

Designation of Leave

Once eligibility for leave is determined, an employee will receive a notice designating (or
denying) the leave. The City can designate leave without a request from an employee.

Use of Paid Leave

If an employee has accrued paid leave, the employee must use the paid leave
concurrently with the FMLA leave.

Intermittent or Reduced Schedule Leave

FMLA leave may be taken intermittently in single blocks of time or as a reduced work
hour schedule. When taken intermittently for the serious health condition of the
employee, employee’s family member or person under the employee’s guardianship, it
must be substantiated with evidence of a medical reason for this type of schedule.
Certification from a health care provider will be required in order to establish the medical
necessity for the leave.

When leave is taken after the birth or placement of a child for adoption or foster care,
intermittent or reduced scheduled leave will be granted only with the permission of the
Department Head.

Employees are expected to schedule planned health care provider appointments outside
their scheduled work hours whenever possible, and if not possible, every effort should be
made to have the appointment times at hours that would not unduly disrupt operations
(beginning or end of a shift, or during lunch break).

An employee who is on intermittent or reduced schedule leave may be temporarily
transferred to an alternative position with equivalent pay and benefits for which the
employee is qualified and which better accommodates the scheduling of the leave.

Fire Department employees who are placed on light duty shall be converted to a 40-hour
per week work schedule. Accrual of leave during this period will be based on the 40-
hour schedule. Once the employee returns to active duty in the Fire Department the
normal leave accrual for that Department will be restored.

Job Restoration

At the conclusion of the FMLA leave, an employee is entitled to be restored to the same
position that was held before the start of the FMLA leave, or to an equivalent position
with equivalent benefits, pay, and other terms and conditions of employment. An
employee is entitled to reinstatement only if he/she would have continued to be employed
had FMLA leave not been taken. Thus, an employee is not entitled to reinstatement if,
because of a layoff, reduction in force or other reason, the employee would not be
employed at the time job restoration is sought.

Job Restoration — Key Employee

Key employees are entitled to FMLA leave but are not entitled to job restoration if re-
employment after the conclusion of the leave would cause a substantial and grievous
economic injury to the City. A key employee is a salaried employee who is among the
highest paid 10 percent of the workforce. A key employee will be notified in writing of
his or her status in response to the employee’s notice of intent to take FMLA leave,
unless circumstances do not permit such notice. Ifa key employee is already on FMLA

leave when notice of key-employee designation is received, the employee will be given a
reasonable time to return to work before losing the right to job restoration.

Health Benefits

While on FMLA leave, the employee will maintain the same health plan benefits as if
working. If the employee fails to make the employee’s share of the health plan
payments, the employee will be provided written notice of this failure and will be given
an additional 15 days to make payment in full. If payment is not made after this notice,
health benefit coverage will cease.

If an employee does not return to work after the conclusion of the FMLA leave, the
employee is responsible for reimbursing the City for the health care premiums paid
during the period of FMLA leave.

Required Documentation for Leave Requested

Employees are required to submit completed Certification forms for all absences for
which FMLA benefits are being requested. These forms should be provided to the
employee along with the Notice of Eligibility, additional copies are available from the
City Manager’s office. A chronic or long-term health condition will not require a new
Certification form for each absence, however, the statement by the health care provider
initially provided must state that the absence was due to the chronic condition.

When leave is taken to care for a family member or a person under their guardianship, the
employee may be asked to provide documentation of family or guardianship relationship
(e.g., birth certificate or court document).

If the information provided on the Certification form is not adequate, the employee will
be allowed to provide supplemental information or, with the employee’s permission,
another health care provider will contact the employee's health care provider in an effort
to clarify or authenticate the initial certification. In the alternative, the employee may be
requested to obtain a second opinion from a designated health care provider; this second
opinion will not be at the employee’s expense or charged to the employee’s health plan.
If the initial and second certifications differ, a third certification from a jointly-selected
health care provider may be required (at the City’s expense).

FMLA leave or return to work may be delayed or denied if the appropriate
documentation is not provided in a timely manner. Also, a failure to provide requested
documentation of the reason for an absence from work may lead to termination of
employment.

If the employee informs the City that there is no intent to return to work, the employee
will be considered to have voluntarily resigned effective the date that the notice is given.

Updates During Leave

During FMLA leave, an employee may be requested to provide a recertification at
intervals in accordance with the FMLA. In addition, during FMLA leave, the employee
may be required to provide periodic reports regarding the employee’s status and intent to
return to work. If the employee’s anticipated return to work date changes and it becomes
necessary for the employee to take more or less leave than originally anticipated, the
employee must provide reasonable notice (i.e. within 2 business days advance notice) of
the changed circumstances and new return to work date.

Procedures for Requesting Leave

An employee is required to request FMLA leave at least 30 days before the leave is to
start if the need for the leave is foreseeable. In circumstances under which the leave is
not foreseeable 30 days in advance, an employee must request the leave as soon as
practicable. Although eligibility for leave will be determined once a request is made,
FMLA leave may be designated without a request from an employee.

An employee requesting FMLA leave should request it in writing, if possible, and state
the reason for the leave, the start date for the leave, and the anticipated date for return to
work (if known). When a supervisor receives the request (even if the request is made
orally), the supervisor must notify the City Manager immediately in order for the request
to be processed. Within five days of the request, the employee will be provided with an
“Eligibility Notice,” and if enough information was presented at the time of the request,
with a “Designation Notice.” However, if the employee is eligible for FMLA leave,
more information may be needed before the leave can be designated as FMLA leave.
With intermittent FMLA leave, the procedures for taking leave must be followed, e.g.,
calling in to the supervisor in advance of the shift, requesting permission for the leave,
etc,

Policy Title: Military Leave ~ Effective Date:
Section No.: 22 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

An employee who is a member of the armed services or is a member of the organized
reserve forces of any of the armed services of the United States, National Guard or Naval
Militia (collectively “military duty”) is entitled to a leave of absence with the City
without the loss of seniority, accrued leave, or efficiency rating. For each tour of duty, an
employee will receive fifteen days of paid leave but with multiple tours of duty, the
amount of paid leave in a single fiscal year shall not exceed fifteen days.

Leave Entitlement

Upon presentation of a copy of final orders or other equivalent notice, military leave will
be granted for all days that an employee is engaged in federally funded military duty to a
maximum of 5 years cumulative.

An employee who is leaving for military duty must provide the administrator with
advance notice either orally or in writing unless it would be unreasonable to provide
notice or the employee is precluded by military necessity from providing such advance
notice. If such delay in notifying the City of the need for military leave occurs, the
employee must provide a copy of their military orders to the City Manager as soon as
possible thereafter.

Military leave is cumulative which means that all leave taken for military purposes is
added together to determine whether the 5-year maximum has been reached. Under
certain circumstances, military leave may extend beyond the 5-year period, e.g. being out
of the country when the time limit is reached or being called for training as a reservist.

Benefits

When an employee is on military leave, the employee will not accrue annual or sick leave
or be covered under the health benefit plan (unless such coverage is elected under
COBRA). An employee may continue health insurance coverage by electing under
COBRA and have continuation coverage for a maximum of 24 months (if the employee
becomes disabled, coverage can be extended to up to 36 months). Under COBRA the

employee is expected to make the full monthly premium payments. When the City is
notified of the employee’s need for military leave, the employee will be provided with a
notice of the COBRA rights and an election form.

When an employee returns to work at the conclusion of military leave all time served on
military duty will count as years of service for City retirement benefit purposes provided
the employee returns to work at the City. Military leave will also count as years of
service with the City for all purposes relating to seniority in employment.

Job Restoration Rights

An employee who is on military leave shall be entitled to all reemployment rights and
benefits as set forth in the federal Uniformed Service Employment and Reemployment
Rights Act and other applicable federal or state laws.

In accordance with the time frames and requirements established by federal law,
employees must promptly return to employment after military service is completed. The
period an individual has to report back to work or notify the employer of his or her intent
to return to work after military service is based on time spent on military duty. For
service of less than 31 days, the employee must return at the beginning of the next
regularly scheduled work period on the first full day after release from service taking into
account safe travel home plus an 8 hour rest period. For service of more than 36 days but
less than 181 days, the employee must either return to work or submit notice of intent to
return to work within 14 days of release from service. For services of more than 180
days, the employee must either return to work or submit notice of intent to return to work
within 90 days of release from service. Returning employees must provide
documentation from the military branch of service, stating the date the employee was
released from military service and whether the service was performed under honorable
conditions.

Full time employees may have the ability to purchase active duty military service to
count towards eligibility for retirement. Please refer to the VRS Handbook for the
applicable Retirement Plan, VRS Plan 1, VRS Plan 2 or Hybrid Plan under the section
Purchasing of Prior Service for the requirements.

legs, eyes, or any two in the same compensable accident, or compensable job-
related incidents resulting in permanent paralysis or severe brain injury.

6. Death Benefits: A specific sum payable for a specified period to qualifying
dependents of an employee who dies as the result of a compensable job-
related injury or disease.

DEFINITIONS:
Injury

An injury is defined as an injury by accident arising out of and in the course of
employment.

Occupational Disease

An occupational disease is defined as a disease arising out of and in the course of
employment, but, unless otherwise provided by the Virginia Workers’ Compensation
Act, not an ordinary disease of life to which the general public is exposed outside of
employment.

Workers’ Compensation

Workers’ Compensation is defined as a type of leave from employment which results
from an employee’s incapacity to work, and which has been determined to have resulted
from an injury or occupational disease such that the employee is entitled to benefits
required by the Virginia Workers’ Compensation Act.

Policy Title: Safety Program & Procedures Effective Date:
Section No.: 28 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the intent of the City to provide safe equipment, procedures and surroundings for all
employees and the general public and to provide safe and healthful working conditions as
clearly outlined in the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970. The City Manager
shall have responsibility for the establishment of safety education and training.

Some of the reasons for establishing an effective safety policy are listed below:

1. To provide a base from which to implement and enforce safe practices and
procedures.

2. To reduce the number of on-the-job accidents, thus reducing costs by:
a. Holding down Workers’ Compensation claims and public liability costs; and
b. Reducing uninsured costs such as:

(1) Lost time to injured workers.

(2) Cost due to equipment damage.

(3) Cost to employer under employee benefits.

(4) Cost to employer in wage contributions of the injured worker.
(5) Overhead costs per injured worker.

The purpose of the City’s accident control program, therefore, is to locate and correct
conditions responsible for these losses. The responsibility of this program must be shared.
by all members of the municipal work force.

Supervisor’s Responsibility
The success of any safety program rests primarily with our supervisors, who control

personnel and oversee much of the City business. This statement is predicated on
continuing and positive support from all levels of management.

It is the supervisor’s responsibility to recommend procedures to reduce the frequency of
job accidents by identifying, correcting, and reporting all unsafe conditions or unsafe acts
as they occur in their areas of control. It is most important that supervisors understand
that they must eliminate hazardous conditions themselves, or by reporting them to higher
management if beyond their control.

Some of the actions required to implement an effective safety program include:

1. Thorough knowledge of employees related to job assignment, job instruction, and
the maintenance of discipline.

2. Pre-job planning.

3. Frequent inspections of job sites.

4. Close checks with employees to advise them of safety procedures.
5. Prompt action to correct unsafe conditions and work practices.

To summarize, supervisory job analysis as applied to safety may be defined as planning,
analyzing hazards, arranging operations, providing equipment, providing instruction, and
supervising in a manner and to a degree necessary to adequately ensure safety throughout
the job.

Safety Instructions for Employees

Employment by the City of Poquoson will be limited to those who accept the full
responsibility for their own safety and who cooperate fully in eliminating all accidents
and injuries. Full acceptance of the following set of safety rules will be a condition of
employment.

1. Employees must report all injuries and accidents to their supervisor and the office
of the Finance Department immediately. Appropriate medical treatment must be
obtained no matter how slight the injury. This requirement is mandatory for the
protection of the employee.

2. All safety devices and equipment provided to employees by the City must be used
properly.

3. Be safety conscious and report to the supervisor any unsafe conditions or
practices. At all times the employee is free to offer suggestions that would

improve safety conditions.

4. Horseplay is not permitted at any work site at any time by an employee.

5. Observe all safety precautions in the use of equipment which could cause injury
to yourself or other employees.

6. Ifan employee does not understand the safety rules, work practices, procedures,
or instructions, the supervisor should be consulted.

7. Proper housekeeping and maintenance of work areas and equipment is essential to
job safety.

8. City employees operating vehicles in or near construction areas shall operate in
accordance with VDOT Work Area Protection standards and requirements.

All employees are expected to familiarize themselves with these policies and procedures.
Failure to observe proper safety procedures, failure to utilize safety equipment, or failure
to report an accident, injury, or defective equipment or vehicles will result in disciplinary
action.

GENERAL DRIVING POLICY:

All City drivers will be examples of safe and courteous driving. Every effort will be
made to prevent accidents, to protect City assets and to make public roads safer for
everybody with whom we share the roads.

Driver Qualifications and Training

Department Heads are responsible for ensuring that their employees are properly licensed
and trained to operate City vehicles. A valid Virginia driver’s license is required to
operate City vehicles. The basic license requirements for passenger vehicles apply, with
the following additional requirements for heavy vehicles operated on the highway.

Those persons required to operate or maintain a vehicle weighing 26,001 pounds or more,
gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR); or designed to carry 16 or more passengers,
including the driver; or used to transport hazardous materials required to be placarded by
federal law, must have a Virginia Commercial Motor Driver’s License (CDL). Such
license is required for volunteer drivers, heavy vehicle mechanics, and public school bus
operators. Commercial license requirements do not apply to operators of emergency
vehicles, such as fire fighters. However, operators of emergency vehicles must meet
special State and Federal skill requirements for driving certification.

Driving Records

A valid Virginia driver’s license for the appropriate class of vehicle or equipment which
will be operated is required if such vehicle or equipment operation is specifically required
in the job description. Prospective employees and volunteers who will be required to
operate a City vehicle during the course of their employment shall possess a current valid
Virginia driver's license during their tenure of employment. A current driving record
(less than 30 days old) of these employees/volunteers must be provided to the hiring
supervisor by the employee/volunteer or permission granted to the City to obtain this
information on their behalf and at the employee’s or volunteer’s own expense. This
record will be forwarded to the City Manager’s Office in conjunction with the request
for an employment offer to be made.

A Virginia driver’s license for current and prospective employees and volunteers may not
have DMV restriction codes which limit the driving privilege to the extent that
requirements of the jobs applied for or occupied cannot be met. Ifthe individual will be
required to operate a bus capable of carrying more than 15 passengers, then the license
must have the coded endorsement of “S”.

The City Manager’s Office shall maintain a program to obtain the DMV records for
operators of City vehicles. The DMV record shall be maintained in confidential,
individual personnel files and updated annually. DMV driving records for City
employees may be obtained from the Division of Motor Vehicles by the City Manager’s
Office at no expense to the City or individual concerned. The City Manager’s office shall
notify the appropriate Department Head when an employee’s or volunteer’s record does
not meet the minimum standards described above.

Driving Standards

All current and prospective City of Poquoson employees or volunteers who are required
to operate a City-owned vehicle according to their job description shall be required to
submit to a Division of Motor Vehicles (DMV) record check. All other employees and
volunteers who may occasionally drive a City vehicle must also submit to a DMV records
check prior to vehicle operation. For current employees and volunteers this check will
occur, at a minimum, on an annual basis. In addition to the annual DMV records check,
all current employees and volunteers must inform their supervisor, by the next scheduled
workday, of any licenses suspensions, or revocations, or charges being placed for any of
the offenses listed below, and the final disposition of the case. Failure to do so may be
grounds for immediate dismissal. Drivers must meet minimum standards as determined
by the City. The City’s standards are as follows:

e¢ No more than two (2) moving violations within the previous twelve (12) months.

e No more than three (3) moving violations within the previous twenty-four (24)
months.

¢ Norecord of convictions associated with drugs/intoxicants, driving under the
influence (DUD), reckless driving, eluding police, leaving the scene of an accident,
or manslaughter (voluntary or involuntary) within the previous sixty (60) months.

¢ No record of current revocations or suspensions or previous revocations or
suspensions associated with moving violations within the last sixty (60) months.

If an employee or volunteer fails to meet driving standards and is in a position where
operating a vehicle is essential to the City, appropriate action will be taken depending on
the severity and number of the conviction(s). At the recommendation of the Department
Head, appropriate action may range from putting the employee or volunteer on a “watch
status,” with DMV checks conducted on a more frequent basis, to termination of
employment or termination of voluntary assignment with the City.

This procedure does not prohibit Department Heads from issuing reasonable standard
operating procedures with more stringent requirements.

Accidents
a. Driving Practices. Operators should practice “defensive driving” and
anticipate and observe the actions of other drivers and control their own

vehicle in such a manner so as to avoid an accident involvement. An operator
of a City vehicle and all passengers therein shall properly use seat belts.

Injury resulting from failure to wear seat belts may constitute gross negligence
on the part of the individual and upon case review may jeopardize an
employee’s eligibility for relief normally provided under Workers’
Compensation and disability claims.

. Accident Involving City Vehicle. In the event an accident should occur
involving a City-owned or City-insured vehicle, the following procedures
should be followed:

(1) Call for an ambulance for anyone seriously injured. (Dial 911 in most
areas.) When calling from a cellular/pcs phone the caller should remain
on the line and provide their exact location.

(2) Notify the police/fire department of the accident as circumstances dictate.
The driver is required to immediately give notice of the accident by the
quickest means of communication to a State trooper, sheriff, or other
police official if the accident resulted in injury to or death of any person.

(3) Get the names, phone numbers, and addresses of all persons in the other
vehicle(s), the driver’s license data of other operators, the license number
of other vehicle(s) and the name of the insurance carrier for other
vehicle(s) involved.

(4) Complete the data on the accident report form located in the glove
compartment of the vehicle and submit this form to the Finance
Department within 24 hours. A copy of this form shall also be sent to the
Fleet Manager, who will be responsible for getting estimates to repair
vehicle.

(5) DO NOT ADMIT RESPONSIBILITY and make no statement regarding
the accident except to authorized insurance claims representatives of the
City’s insurance carrier and the police.

(6) DO NOT DISCLOSE INSURANCE policy details to anyone. However,
you may inform the other driver of the name of the City’s insurance
carrier and policy number.

(7) If there are no injuries involved in the accident, the driver may still be
required to file a report with the Division of Motor Vehicles as to the
extent of the property damage involved. The driver is responsible for
contacting the Division of Motor Vehicles within 5 days of the accident to
determine if a report will be required.

Rendering Assistance. Upon reaching the scene of an accident, the operator
of a City vehicle is authorized to render such assistance as he can by caring
for the injured first, calling or sending for the fire, rescue, or police and taking

measures to prevent other vehicles from becoming involved in the accident.
When stopping at the scene of an accident, the driver should ensure that the
vehicle is parked in a safe location so as not to interfere with traffic.

Accident Reporting. A City Vehicle Accident Report form is required and
shall be completed by the operator or assigned driver of a City-insured vehicle
involved in an accident when:

e An injury occurs, regardless of property damage.

e An accident or collision involves a vehicle or property not owned by the
City regardless of fault or damage sustained by either party.

e Another City-owned vehicle or item of property is involved and the
probable cost of total repairs (vehicle and/or property) will exceed $100 as
determined by the City’s Fleet Manager.

e Mysterious or unexplained damage to a City vehicle is clearly visible and
the probable cost of total repairs will exceed $100 as determined by the
City’s Fleet Manager.

The accident report shall be reviewed by the Department Head or other
supervisor who shall forward copies of the report to the Finance Department
and the Fleet Manager prior to the close of the next City workday after the
accident.

Case Review. A copy of the Vehicle Accident Report shall be provided to
the City Manager by the Finance Department within three working days. The
Finance Department shall conduct an investigation of the facts and
circumstances of accidents involving City-owned vehicles which have
resulted in a reported injury or when property damage in excess of $500 is
involved. The Finance Department is authorized to gather evidence, relevant
documents and solicit written statements from City employees. Within ten
(10) working days from receipt of the accident report, the Finance Department
shall forward to the Department Head and City Manager the results of their
inquiry. If additional reports concerning the accident are expected from
outside agencies, i.e., State Police, non-City employees involved in the
accident, etc., then the report shall be provided within ten (10) working days
from receipt of all such anticipated information. If delays are anticipated, the
Finance Department shall notify the Department Head and City Manager as
soon as possible. The Department Head should review the inquiry as soon as
practicable and render an opinion as to whether negligence or operating
practices were a probable contributing factor to the accident. The Department
Head may also recommend changes to safety policies or procedures to the
City Manager. The City Manager will determine the appropriate corrective
action. Corrective action may be administrative in nature such as additional

driver training or may include disciplinary measures. The City Manager shall
review the report, determine the appropriate disposition of the case, and
conclude the inquiry process.

Failure to Report Vehicular Accident Damage. Using the Accident Inquiry
form, the Fleet Manager shall notify the City Manager and the Finance
Department if a vehicle Accident Report has not been submitted when
required or upon discovery of previously unreported damage to a City vehicle.

Driver Responsibilities

a.

The basic principles of defensive driving will be provided for all employees
during their initial orientation. Employees are expected to be familiar with
and observe all defensive driving guidelines.

An employee shall not drive or operate a vehicle unless assigned or granted
permission. Vehicles are to be used only for conducting official City
business. No personal use is allowed. Family members or other non-
employees are not allowed to drive City vehicles.

Drivers must have in their possession a proper valid operator’s license when
operating City vehicles. Drivers must be familiar with the specific vehicle
they will be driving. Orientation training should provide an opportunity to
learn about any special equipment or unusual aspects regarding the vehicle.

Seat belt use is mandatory for vehicle operators and all occupants.

Drivers must operate their vehicle in a safe manner at all times. Drivers
should be prepared to change unaccepted driving habits and become familiar
with and follow the principles of defensive driving.

Drivers must have and maintain a favorable driving record as determined by
review of their MVR. In order to maintain a good driving record, employees
may be required to complete a Defensive Driving Course recognized by the
Commonwealth of Virginia Department of Motor Vehicles.

All drivers who operate vehicles in excess of 26,001 pounds gross vehicle
weight and/or designed to carry 16 or more passengers, including the driver,
are required by Virginia law to have a CDL. Drivers must pass a drug/alcohol
test.

The driver will perform pre-trip and post-trip inspections. The
Manufacturer’s service schedule should be followed. If the vehicle is
assigned to a driver then that individual is responsible for scheduling the
vehicle for periodic service and repairs.

No one shall ever operate a vehicle when there is a known safety defect. Any
defects shall be reported immediately. All objects shall be secured inside the
vehicle to avoid distractions and “missiles” inside the vehicle.

If a vehicle is involved in an accident, the driver’s first duty is to stop the
vehicle. The police must be called. Employees must not make or promise
settlements of damage. The driver’s supervisor shall be notified immediately
and accident-reporting procedures outlined in the Vehicle Accident Card shall
be followed.

All drivers are cautioned to devote their full attention to driving. Many
vehicle accidents can be contributed to driver distraction. Keep your mind on
driving.

Drivers must signal their intentions when slowing down, stopping, or making
a turn.

. Always give pedestrians the right-of-way.
All drivers must yield to emergency vehicles.

Flasher lights on trucks, cars, and equipment should be used only as
emergency or if work conditions require. Flashing lights are not to be used as
an excuse to gain the right-of-way or break traffic rules. Their purpose is for
the protection of employees and work areas and as a warning device for
pedestrians in vehicular traffic.

Keep windshields and door windows clean for good visibility.

Be certain you have a complete picture of all objects behind your vehicle
before moving in reverse. When visibility is impaired, drivers must walk-
around the vehicle before starting and moving the vehicle.

The person directing a driver must remain on the ground near the front or rear
side of the vehicle to be in full view of the driver. Use hand signals only
when directing.

Do not leave the vehicle unattended with the motor running.

All vehicles will be supplied with fire extinguishers. Drivers may offer the
use of fire extinguishers to owners of civilian vehicles on fire. With the
exception of fire department officers, employees should not personally use a
fire extinguisher on civilian vehicles.

All drivers must report all moving violations or accidents on or off the job to
their immediate supervisor.

Any driver involved in an at-fault accident may be required to complete a management
approved defensive driving course. This course should be completed within 60 days of
the accident.

PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to make every effort possible to
comply with the Fair Labor Standards Act. Yet, errors in record-keeping and payroll can
occur. Because mistakes can happen, employees should verify that all hours worked
have been properly recorded and should review their paycheck to see that no improper
deductions or other errors have been made. If you believe that an error has occurred, you
are to report it immediately to your Department Head or to the Payroll Manager.

SCOPE:
This policy applies to all City of Poquoson permanent full-time employees.

Employees who are not exempt from overtime must not work overtime hours unless the
holdover hours have been authorized. Holdover hours should be recorded on your time
sheet. In addition, you should immediately let your supervisor know that the extra hours
were worked so that your schedule can be adjusted during that work period in order to
avoid overtime compensation.

If you are an employee in an exempt salaried position (bona fide executive,
administrative, or professional position, or earning over $100,000 per year) only limited
deductions from your salary can be taken. These deductions include the reductions
required by law (state and federal taxes, Social Security, Medicare ) as well as the
deductions you voluntarily elect to have taken from your salary (e.g. participation in the
401(k), health and dental insurance, etc.)

Permissible deductions include:

e Absence from work for one or more full days for personal reasons other than
sickness or disability;

e Absence for one or more full days due to sickness or disability if the deduction is
made in accordance with a bona fide plan, policy or practice of providing
compensation for salary lost due to illness;

e Absence for Family and Medical Leave;

e Offsets for amounts received as witness or jury fees, or for military pay;

e Unpaid disciplinary suspensions of one or more full days imposed in good faith
for workplace conduct rule infractions;

e Days not worked during the first or last week of employment; or

e Suspensions imposed for an infraction of safety rules of major significance.

Because the City provides a paid leave plan, employees will be required to use available
paid leave for absences for personal reasons, sickness or disability.

If you believe that an improper deduction has been made to your salary you should
immediately report this information to your supervisor or to the Payroll Manager.

Reports of improper deductions will be promptly investigated. Every report will be fully
investigated. If it is determined that an improper deduction has occurred or that there was
an improper calculation of hours worked or wages received, you will be promptly
reimbursed for any error that was made. Any person violating this policy will be subject
to corrective action up to and including discharge.

The City will not tolerate any form of retaliation against a person who has reported an
alleged violation of this policy or who cooperates in the investigation..

Policy Title: Sexual Harassment Effective Date:
Section No.: 33 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to provide a work environment
conducive to the performance of job duties and free from intimidation or coercion in any
form. The City upholds a stringent policy against all forms of discrimination, including
sexual harassment.

SCOPE:

This policy applies to all City of Poquoson employees and applicants for employment.
DEFINITIONS:

Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is defined as a violation under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of
1964 that could subject the City, departments and individuals to legal and financial
liability. Sexual harassment includes sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and

other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:

1. Submission to such conduct is made explicitly or implicitly a term or
condition of an individual’s employment;

2. Submission to or rejection of such conduct by an individual is used as a basis
for employment decisions affecting that individual; and/or

3. Such conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an
individual’s work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive
work environment.

PROCEDURES:
Prohibited Conduct
1. Sexual harassment is a prohibited employment practice.

2. The following examples of conduct may or may not constitute sexual harassment
under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 depending upon the circumstances
under which they occur. These examples do, however, represent inappropriate
behavior and shall constitute misconduct under the Personnel Policies of the City
of Poquoson for which appropriate disciplinary action shall be taken. The
examples do not represent all forms in which misconduct under this policy or
sexual harassment may occur.

a. Explicit or implicit promises of career advancement or preferential treatment
in return for sexual favors including, but not limited to, hiring, promotion,
training and other opportunities or benefits; acceptance of a lower standard of
work performance; providing higher performance evaluations; and lax time-
keepingy when similarly qualified persons are not afforded such benefits or
opportunities.

b. Explicit or implicit threats that an employee’s job shall be adversely affected
if sexual demands are rejected, including, but not limited to, lower
evaluations, denial of promotions or other employment opportunities, punitive
transfers, terminations, and altered or increased work assignments.

c. Denial of employment opportunities or otherwise adversely affecting a
person’s employment based on rejection of sexual attention or demands.

d. Deliberate, repeated or unsolicited verbal comment(s) and gesture(s) of a
scxual nature. This includes any type of sexually suggestive remarks, pictures
or jokes.

c. Deliberate, repeated or unsolicited, and unwanted touching of any kind,
including the touching, patting, kissing or pinching of another person and the
repeated brushing against another person’s body.

f. Propositions of a sexual nature; continued or repeated verbal comments of a
sexual nature; degrading, abusive, or sexually suggestive comments about an
employee’s appearance; whistling or catcalling of a sexual nature; and the
display of sexually suggestive objects or pictures.

Responsibilities

1. Department Heads and supervisors have the responsibility to:

a. Ensure that no City employee is subjected to sexual harassment or actions
which could be perceived as sexual harassment as a result of his/her own
actions, those of other employees, or those of third parties such as customers
or vendors;

b. Provide a working environment which is free of harassment and/or
intimidation;

c. Promptly report any observed behavior that violates this policy;
d. Make all employees aware of this policy;

e. Make all employees aware that false accusations shall result in disciplinary
action, up to and including termination.

Department Heads and supervisors who allow sexual harassment to continue or
fail to take appropriate corrective action upon becoming aware, may be
considered a part to the offense, even though they may not have engaged in such
behavior.

Department Heads and supervisors who fail to respond appropriately to
allegations of sexual harassment may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and
including termination.

All employees are responsible for conducting themselves in a manner that shall
ensure proper performance of City business and maintenance of public trust and
confidence without harassment of any kind.

Victims of sexual harassment have the responsibility to make it clear to the
harasser, directly or through the complaint process, that such behavior is
offensive and to provide notice of the harassment in a timely manner as outlined
in the complaint procedure that follows.

The City Manager’s Office is responsible for developing and managing this
policy and investigating complaints.

Complaint Procedure

1.

Any employee who believes that he or she has been the subject of sexual
harassment should report the alleged charge immediately in accordance with the
following procedure. Under no circumstances shall it be necessary for the
employee to make the complaint to the person who has allegedly harassed the
employee.

All information disclosed in the complaint procedure, including the name of the
complainant and the accused, as well as the existence of the complaint, shall be

held in confidence and shall only be disclosed on a need-to-know basis in order to
investigate and resolve the matter. Any violation of confidentiality by any person
shall constitute misconduct and result in appropriate disciplinary action.

3. Step 1:

a,

It is recommended that the employee alleging sexual harassment (the
complainant) report the incident to his/her immediate supervisor (if the
immediate supervisor is not the accused). If the complainant is not

supervisor, the complainant may report the incident to any of the following
individuals:

e Department Head/Constitutional Officer
e City Manager

For the purpose of this policy the City Manager shall be designated as the

The person informed of the alleged harassment shall be required to inform the

The City Manager shall review the complaint, and may obtain such further
information concerning the complaint as may be necessary to determine
whether an investigation is warranted. If an investigation is not warranted, the

Charges of sexual harassment against the City Manager shall be referred to the
City Attorney who shall be designated as the Equal Opportunity Officer in

4. Step 2:

a.

If an investigation is warranted, the City Manager shall notify the accused

who may have relevant information. The alleged harasser shall be informed

b. The City Manager shall review the personnel files of the complainant and the
accused for information related to the complaint.

In the event that a third party such as a contractor, citizen or vendor is

the complainant, to determine the appropriate action to be taken.

5. Step 3:

¢ notification to the complainant and the accused of the action taken.

If it is determined that there is no foundation for the allegation, the

False accusations may result in severe disciplinary action, including

If there is a foundation for the allegations, the determination of the action

2. Employees who take reprisal, or encourage others to do so, shall be in

Other Reporting Mechanisms

Employees seeking to redress sexual harassment claims may also file a complaint with
the United States Equal Employment Opportunity Commission.

Training

The City Manager’s Office and individual Department Heads/Constitutional Officers

Policy Title: Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Effective Date:
Section No.: 35 Supersedes Policy:

PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson to provide a Tuition Reimbursement/Education
Assistance Program to (i) encourage employees to take courses leading to a formal
professional accreditation or degree relating to their specific job duties; (ii) provide for
optimal potential for advancement; and (iii) attract and retain the best employees.

SCOPE:

All permanent full time employees who have received a “Satisfactory” or higher rating
on their last performance evaluation and who have completed their initial probationary
period, are eligible to apply for tuition reimbursement/education assistance.

DEFINITIONS:
Allowable Costs

Allowable costs comprise registration fees, tuition, lab fees, and course books as required
on the course syllabus. No other expenses shall be considered for reimbursement.
Employees are prohibited from requesting tuition reimbursement for allowable costs for
which they have received financial support from other sources (with the exception of
family support), such as grants, scholarships, etc.

Education Plan
The education plan shall list and describe:

e The courses to be taken to achieve a degree or professional accreditation.
The courses and program listed must enhance the skills of the employee in
his or her immediate position, or prepare the employee for another
position in the city;

¢ The degree/accreditation program to be entered; and

e The name of the institution where classes will be taken.

Eligible Courses

e Courses that are directly related to an employee’s present position, a
promotional objective, or another position in the City as confirmed by the
Department Head/Constitutional Officer’s concurrence, and are part of an
undergraduate, graduate or professional accreditation program.

e Eligible courses are available only through an accredited college,
university, business school, graduate school, trade school, or a
technical/professional institute.

e Electives which are necessary in order to obtain a degree, or other
professional accreditation, and complete the education plan but are not
directly applicable to an employee’s occupation, are eligible for
reimbursement.

Successful Completion of Coursework is defined as:

e A grade of “C” (or 2.0) or better for an undergraduate course
e A passing grade or better for a graduate course
e A grade of “Pass” for a pass/fail course

Technical/Professional Institute

An establishment whose sole or primary function is education and training. It does not
include professional associations.

Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program

The Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program is defined as a training
program that is funded out of a dedicated City reserve and administered by the City
Manager.

PROCEDURES

Requests for Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance

1. Prior to registering for courses, an employee must make the request for
tuition assistance by submitting an education plan through his/her

immediate supervisor and the Department Head/Constitutional Officer to
the City Manager.

The City Manager shall review the request for participation in the tuition
assistance program and shall approve/deny the request. Upon City
Manager approval, and pending availability of funds, the employee will be
eligible to submit requests for reimbursement as he/she progresses through
the degree/accreditation program.

Requests for individual course reimbursement must be submitted to the
City Manager prior to registering for that course. The request must
include the total cost of the course and all information concerning the
course, such as the name of the institution offering the course, course title,
number of credit hours pursued, degree/certification program, etc.

The City Manager shall review the course for policy compliance and
eligibility for reimbursement, and will inform the employee of the
outcome of this review. If approved, the employee must bear the initial
cost of registration, books, tuition and other fees necessary for enrollment.
Expenses associated with courses that do not comply with and/or do not
meet the criteria for eligibility as outlined in this policy, or are not
consistent with the approved education plan, shal! not be reimbursable.

Prior to the City’s reimbursement to the employee for the cost of
successfully completed, approved courses, the City Manager and the
employee shall execute a Tuition Reimbursement Agreement (Appendix
A). This agreement, made between the employee and the City, stipulates
the terms for repayment for the course(s) for which the City has paid
should the employee voluntarily terminate employment, be dismissed, or
be terminated for unsatisfactory service. The employee shall provide
proof of grade(s) and original, itemized receipts for allowable costs.

When the Tuition Reimbursement Agreement has been executed by the
employee, the City Manager shall request the reimbursement check.

Preferred Educational Provider(s)

To make the most efficient use of tuition reimbursement funds, it is recommended that
state universities and colleges be utilized for educational purposes; however, employees
may take courses at any institution accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges
and Schools and the State Board of Education in Virginia, or similarly recognized
accrediting agencies, or other approved institutions.

Benefit

1. All reimbursements are subject to the availability of funds. In the event that
funding appropriated for tuition assistance is not adequate to accommodate
all requests, funding consideration shall be granted first to those employees
who have previously been approved to participate in the program and are
currently working to complete their degree programs or courses of study.
Thereafter, reimbursement shall be granted on a first come, first served basis,
with those not receiving funding being placed on a waiting list.

2. The maximum reimbursement a full-time, permanent employee shall receive
within one fiscal year is $2,500.

Course Attendance During Scheduled and Non-Scheduled Work Hours

1. Efforts should be made to attend courses during non-scheduled work hours. If,
however, this is not possible, courses may be attended during scheduled work
hours, subject to Department Head/Constitutional Officer approval.

2. In the event that approval is granted for class attendance during an employee’s
scheduled work hours, the employee shall make up the missed time or use
annual or compensatory leave to cover time away from the office.

Training As A Condition of Employment

The Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program is a benefit for which
employees may apply and not a condition of employment. Training and education
mandated as a condition of employment shall be addressed under the City Training and
Performance Evaluation policies.

Records Maintenance

A copy of all education and training courses completed by employees requiring official
City approval for expenditure of City funds shall be placed in the employee’s official
personnel record.

Program Responsibility

The administration of the City’s Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance Program
shall be the responsibility of the City Manager.

The administration of the repayment plan, should the employee voluntarily terminate
employment, be dismissed, or be terminated for unsatisfactory service, within the time
period specified in the executed Tuition Reimbursement/Education Assistance
Reimbursement Agreement, shall be the responsibility of the City Manager.

Policy Title: Employee Conduct & Progressive Discipline Effective Date:
Section No.: 39 Supersedes Policy:

PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson to provide a well-defined system of discipline
which sets forth standards of employee conduct and guidelines for disciplinary actions to
ensure a fair and objective process for correcting or treating unacceptable employee
conduct; to distinguish between less serious and more serious incidents of employee
misconduct; and to provide appropriate disciplinary action.

It is the intent of the City of Poquoson that discipline should be characterized as
progressive, corrective and constructive rather than punitive; however, it is recognized
that certain employee misconduct is of such a significant nature that termination of
employment is the necessary and appropriate disciplinary action.

BASIS FOR PROPER CAUSE FOR DISCIPLINARY ACTION:

The need for disciplinary action may arise as a result of different kinds of actions on the
part of an employee, such as, but not limited to:

1. Failure to perform job responsibilities in a satisfactory manner.

2. Infractions of the rules, regulations, policies or procedures as established by the
City and/or department.

3. Commission of an act which would constitute a felony or misdemeanor involving
moral turpitude that would negatively impact the employee’s ability to perform
his/her job duties.

4. Dishonesty or fraud.

MANAGEMENT DISCRETION/ADMINISTRATION OF DISCIPLINARY
POLICY:

Internal consistency within departments and within the City generally is an important part
of a disciplinary policy; however, it is expressly noted that there are numerous factors
that will be considered in determining disciplinary actions, including but not limited to
overall work performance, prior disciplinary history and job attitude. It is further noted
that disciplinary actions for similar offenses may differ from one employee to another
based upon the employee’s job responsibilities and/or grade level. At the City Manager’s

discretion an employee may be placed on non-disciplinary leave with pay during an
investigation of an employee’s conduct.

GROUP OFFENSES:

Unacceptable employee conduct shall be divided into three types of offenses according to
their severity. The examples of employee misconduct identified in each group offense
category are illustrative and are not intended to be all inclusive. Disciplinary actions
denoted in each offense category are guidelines. Actual disciplinary action(s) shall be
determined on a case-by-case basis and may proceed directly to suspension or dismissal
in certain circumstances based upon the seriousness of the offense.

Group One Offense:
e First Offense - Documented Counseling
e Second Offense - Written Reprimand
© Third Offense - 3 to 5 Day Suspension Without Pay
e Fourth Offense -Termination

1. Neglect or carelessness in observation of departmental safety rules or disregard of
common safety practices including but not limited to the operation of equipment
or vehicles.

2. Mischievous actions such as horse play, wrestling or similar conduct.

3. Creating or contributing to unsanitary or unsafe conditions or poor housekeeping.

4. Distracting the attention of others or causing confusion by unnecessary shouting,
catcalls or demonstrations.

5. Failure to report any work-related personal injury, auto or equipment damage to
one’s immediate supervisor within twenty-four (24) hours.

6. Failure to use and wear proper safety equipment, as required.
7, The use of obscene or abusive language toward a fellow employee.

8. Sleeping during work hours (Does not apply to Fire and Rescue personnel
assigned to 24 hour shift.)

9. Loafing or idleness during work hours.
10. Habitually arriving late for work, from lunch or approved break.

11, Leaving job without permission.

12. Unexcused absence from work/failure to report. Employees absent from work for
three consecutive scheduled work days without supervisory approval are deemed
to have resigned without official notice.

13. Engaging in outside employment while on sick leave without written pre-approval
from the City Manager.

14. Inaccurate work, failure to comply with standard procedures, recurrent errors,
carelessness, or failure to perform assigned tasks.

15. Unfriendly or uncooperative in dealing with co-workers or the members of the
public.

16. Violation of the Code of Conduct contained within a City Manager approved
departmental policy and procedures manual/code of conduct policy.

17. Violation of the Code of Ethics or any other duly authorized City policy or
procedure not otherwise specified in this policy.

e First Offense -Written Notice and 3 to 5 day Suspension Without Pay
e Second Offense -Dismissal

1. Reporting to work while intoxicated or under the influence of intoxicants or

2. Operation of City vehicles in excess of posted speed limits (shall not apply to

DISCIPLINARY ACTION PROCEDURES:

Most disciplinary actions are initiated by an employee’s supervisor or department head;

required. No disciplinary action which includes suspension, demotion or dismissal may
be instituted against an employee without a hearing between the Department Head and
the employee. The City Manager or Assistant City Manager shall also be present. In
cases where the City Manager has initiated the disciplinary action, the City Manager shall
be substituted for the Department Head in the following procedure. The conduct of a
disciplinary hearing shall be held in accordance with the following procedure:

1. The Department Head shall advise the employee of the specific offenses he or she
is alleged to have violated.

2. The Department Head shall advise the employee of the evidence including
witnesses, if any, to the violation.

3. The Department Head shall afford the employee the opportunity, if so desired, to
respond to the charges of alleged violation.

4. The Department Head shal! inform the employee within 3 working days of what
specific disciplinary actions, if any, will be taken.

5. Ifafter an appropriate hearing, the Department Head takes disciplinary action
against the employee, the subject employee may appeal said disciplinary action
subject to the City’s Grievance Policy.

PERSONNEL POLICY
City of Poquoson, Virginia

Policy Title: Drug and Alcohol Policy Effective Date:
Section No.: 40 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to ensure that all employees are
mentally and physically fit for duty during working hours. Strict adherence to this policy
will reduce the potential for accidents, absenteeism, substandard performance, poor
employee morale and will increase the public’s confidence in the City’s workforce.

SCOPE:

The City of Poquoson is committed to maintaining a work environment that is completely
free from illegal drugs and alcohol and their effects. City employees also have a personal
obligation to refrain from use of illegal drugs and alcoholic beverages on the job in order
to be fit for duty while performing employment duties for the City. City employees who
use or are under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs while on the job pose serious
safety and health risks, not only to themselves, but also to individuals who work or come
into contact with such employees. Accordingly, any City employee who possesses, uses,
consumes, purchases, sells, transfers, distributes, or manufactures illegal drugs or
alcoholic beverages while at work or on the job shall be immediately terminated from the
City’s employment. Any City employee who is under the influence of illegal drugs or
alcohol while at work or on the job shall be immediately terminated from the City’s
employment. The illegal use, sale or possession of narcotics, drugs, alcohol or controlled
substances while on the job or on City property is prohibited and is a dischargeable
offense. Any illegal substance will be turned over to the appropriate law enforcement
agency and may result in criminal prosecution.

Off-the-job illegal drug use which could adversely affect an employee’s job performance
or which could jeopardize the safety of others, the public, or City equipment, is proper
cause for administrative or disciplinary action up to and including termination of
employment.

Employees who are arrested for off-the-job drug activity may be considered to be in
violation of this policy. In deciding what action to take, management will take into
consideration the nature of the charges, the employee’s present job assignment, the

employee’s record with the City and other factors relating to the impact and
circumstances of the employee’s arrest.

Use of illegal drugs at any time is prohibited. Off duty use of alcohol that is detected just
prior, during or immediately following performance of duties for the City, at a level of
.04 BAC/BRAC or higher is prohibited. Use of prescription medications without a valid
prescription issued to the tested employee is also prohibited. Use of any legal drug
whether it is prescribed by a physician or over the counter, is prohibited, if the drug
renders the employee unsafe in the employee’s job performance.

DEFINITIONS:
Alcohol or Alcoholic Beverages

Alcohol, spirits, wine, and beer and any one or more of such varieties containing one-half
of one percent or more of alcohol by volume, including mixed alcoholic beverages and
every liquid or solid, patented or not, containing alcohol, spirits, wine, or beer and
capable of being consumed by a human being.

At Work/On the Job

Period of time when an employee (i) is being paid to work, or (ii) has been notified of
being subject to being called into work, or (iii) is operating any vehicle or piece of
equipment owned or leased by the City, or (iv) is on unpaid breaks or lunch periods while
on the job. The words include all on-duty functions performed from the time an
employee begins work or is required to be ready to work until he/she is relieved from
work and all responsibility for performing work.

All buildings and other facilities used by the City to conduct its operations plus all work
sites to which employces are assigned in the course of the performance of their duties for
the City.

Employee

Any person employed by the City, in either a full or part-time capacity and also includes
such non-City employees as volunteer firefighters and auxiliary police.

Illegal Drugs

Those substances identified as “controlled substances”, “marijuana” and “Schedules I, II,
Ill, IV, V and VI” as those terms are used or defined in the Drug Control Act, Chapter 34
of Title 54.1 of the Code of Virginia (1950), as amended or in Title 18.2 of the Code of
Virginia (1950), as amended. The terms include any substance whose possession is
prohibited by law or which is used in a manner different from that which is authorized by
law or which has been illegally obtained.

Any drug (a) not legally obtainable or (b) legally obtainable but not legally obtained or
used. Therefore, the term includes prescription drugs obtained illegally and prescription
drugs not being used for prescribed purposes. It also includes marijuana, cocaine and
heroin and derivative of those drugs, among other illegal drugs.

Legal Drug

Includes prescribed and over-the-counter drugs legally obtained and being used for the
purpose for which they were prescribed and/or manufactured.

Reasonable Suspicion

A belief based on observed, specific, objective facts where the natural inference to be
drawn under the circumstances is that the person is under the influence of drugs or
alcohol. An unexplained workplace accident may be considered to provide reasonable
suspicion.

Safety Sensitive
All employment positions whose work duties involve:

(i) hazardous or potentially hazardous functions or requires the use or handling of
hazardous or potentially hazardous equipment or materials that could cause
injury to an employee or to the public

(ii) direct responsibility for the care or safety of children (i.e. 18 years of age or
younger)

(iii) duties of such a nature that a lapse in constant attention or alertness may
jeopardize the health or safety of other employees or the public

Under the Influence

A condition in which a person has ingested, injected, inhaled or otherwise consumed
enough alcohol or illegal drugs to observably affect his manner, disposition, speech,
muscular movement, general appearance or behavior.

The employee is affected by a drug or alcohol or the combination of a drug and alcohol in
any detectable manner. The symptoms of influence are not confined to those consistent
with misbehavior, or to obvious impairment of physical or mental ability, such as slurred
speech or difficulty in maintaining balance.

APPLICATION OF POLICY:

Post-Offer/Pre-Employment Testing for Employees Performing Safety-Sensitive
Functions

After an offer of employment has been made, all applicants for City employment
involving the performance of safety-sensitive functions shall be required to be tested for
illegal drugs. These tests shall be required of a job applicant only after he/she has been
offered employment; however, as part of the job application process, all applicants shall
execute releases and consent forms which authorize the City to obtain the results of such
testing. All applicants for safety-sensitive positions shall also be required to execute
releases and consent forms which authorize the City to contact all employers whom the
applicant has worked for during the prior two (2) years in order to obtain all records
pertaining to the applicant’s drug and alcohol use during that time, including any records
regarding the applicant’s refusal to submit to testing for drug and alcohol use. Any offer
of employment will be withdrawn from an applicant who refuses to submit to the
required pre-employment drug test or who tests positive for illegal drugs in such test.

Each applicant for employment will be required as a condition of employment to undergo
a urine drug screen. Applicants will be asked to read the policy and sign the Pre-
Employment offer and Employee Consent to Alcohol and Drug Screening. If an
applicant tests positive and is determined to be in violation of this Policy, applicant will
be ineligible for employment until; (a) the expiration of 60 days following the date of
the test; and (b) the applicant submits to a second urine drug screen which does not
disclose that the applicant is in violation of this Policy. If an applicant as described
above is hired, another urine drug screen test may be conducted within sixty (60) days
following the date of employment and the employee may be subjected to unannounced
drug testing for the first twelve (12) months as a condition of employment. Thereafter,
the employee will be subject to testing, the same as current employees.

It is preferred that a new employee be tested prior to reporting for work. However, it is
realized that under certain circumstances a new employee may start before test results are
known. In such circumstances the employee's hiring is conditional upon passing the drug
test.

All employment offer letters are to contain a statement that employment is conditional
upon passing a drug test. Employees will be required to read and sign the pre-
Employment Offer and Employee Consent to Alcohol and Drug Screening (Exhibit A)
immediately prior to the first day of employment.

Safety-Sensitive Position Transfer Drug Testing

Employees shall be subject to drug testing when initially promoted, demoted or otherwise
transferred to a safety-sensitive position.

Post-Accident Testing

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted as soon after a vehicular accident as practicable
on any employee who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the
vehicle involved in the accident if:

1. the accident involved loss of human life; or

2. the accident resulted in bodily injury to a person who, as a result of the injury,
immediately received medical treatment away from the accident scene; or

3, the accident caused disabling damage to one or more motor vehicles which
required the vehicles to be transported away from the scene of the accident by
a tow truck or similar vehicle and the employee received a citation under state
or local law for a moving traffic violation arising from the accident.

The term “accident” shall not include occurrences which:

1. involve only boarding and alighting from a stationary motor vehicle; or
2. involve only the loading or unloading of cargo.

In the absence of the need for immediate medical treatment following an accident, all
employees shall make themselves readily available for the required testing. Any
employee involved in a motor vehicle accident which requires post-accident testing
pursuant to this policy shall not use alcohol for eight hours after the accident or until
he/she undergoes a post-accident alcohol test, whichever occurs first. Employees will not
be tested, however, unless the test for alcohol can be administered within eight hours
afier the accident and the test for drugs can be administered within thirty-two hours. If an
alcohol test is not administered to an employee within two hours after the accident or if a
drug test is not administered within thirty-two hours, the employee’s immediate
supervisor shall prepare and maintain records explaining why such tests were not
conducted. Tests conducted by authorized federal, state or local officials shall fulfill
post-accident testing requirements provided such tests conform to applicable legal
requirements and the results of such tests are obtained by the City in a timely manner.

Reasonable Suspicion Alcohol/Drug Testing

All employees of the City shall be subject to testing for drugs and/or alcohol based upon
a reasonable suspicion of the employee’s violation of this policy. “Reasonable
suspicion” shall be based on specific, contemporaneous, articulable observations
concerning the employee’s appearance, behavior, speech or body odors. Such
observations may include indications of the chronic and withdrawal effects of controlled
substances, Reasonable suspicion tests shall be authorized only if the required
observations are made during, just before or just after the period of the work day. A
reasonable suspicion test may not be conducted by the person who determines that

reasonable suspicion exists to conduct such a test. No attempt to conduct alcohol tests
shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of eight hours after
the determination of reasonable suspicion. No attempt to conduct drug tests shall
continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of thirty-two hours after
the determination of reasonable suspicion. If an alcohol test is not performed within two
hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion or a drug test is not performed within
thirty-two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion, the employee’s immediate
supervisor shall prepare and maintain a record explaining why such a test was not given.
A supervisor who makes observations leading to a reasonable suspicion test for alcohol
or drugs shall make a written record of his/her observations within twenty-four hours of
the observed behavior or before the results of the drug test are released, whichever is
earlier.

Testing Following Rehabilitation and/or Rehire

An employee who returns to work following rehabilitation shall be required to submit to
a return to duty drug/alcohol test prior to returning to work for the City. Furthermore,
such employee shall be subject to unannounced, follow-up, fitness-for-duty testing during
the year following the employee’s return to work. The frequency of such testing shall be
determined by a substance abuse professional. Follow-up testing may extend beyond a
year but shall not exceed a sixty month period.

EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES:
No employee shall:

1. Report to work or perform work duties when their ability to perform job duties
is impaired by alcohol or illegal drugs or they are under the influence of
alcohol or illegal drugs, or a combination of either.

2. Drink alcoholic beverages for four hours prior to reporting for work.

3. Report to work or perform work duties after taking any medication or legal
drugs, whether prescription or over-the-counter, which, by indication on the
prescription label or packaging, may interfere with the employee’s
performance of his job duties, without immediately notifying his immediate
supervisor of the matter. Employees shall inform their supervisors if they are
using a controlled substance which their physician has prescribed for
therapeutic purposes.

4. Possess or use illegal drugs or alcohol at work or on the job.

5. Refuse to immediately submit to directives for the alcohol and/or drug testing
required and to complete all paperwork associated with such testing.

6. Consume alcoholic beverages during the eight hours following an accident for

which post-accident testing is required, if an alcohol/drug test has not been
performed following the accident.

7. Refuse to provide, within twenty-four hours after a drug test indicates the
presence of a drug, proof of a valid, current prescription, in the employee’s
name, for any drug identified.

8. Fail to notify the employee’s immediate supervisor, within five days, of any
conviction of a criminal law involving alcohol or illegal drugs.

9. Not attend and satisfactorily complete any required substance abuse treatment
program.

SUPERVISOR RESPONSIBILITIES:

Supervisors are responsible for the fair and consistent enforcement of this policy. Any
supervisor who knowingly permits a violation of this policy by City employees under
his/her direct supervision shall be subject to discipline, up to and including, dismissal.
Upon reasonable suspicion and after an accident requiring post-accident testing,
supervisors shall direct an employee to participate in a drug/alcohol test. In conjunction
with such directive, the supervisor shall:

1. Document, in writing, the facts constituting the reasonable suspicion that the
employee is using or under the influence of drugs or alcohol.

2. Communicate the facts giving rise to the suspicion to the employee.

3. Advise the employee of the consequences of refusal to submit to a required
drug and/or alcohol test.

4. Provide for the employee to be transported to a testing facility.

5. Notify the Police Department when a reasonable suspicion exists that the
employee has possession of an illegal drug.

EMPLOYEE REQUEST TO RETEST URINE SPECIMEN:

Urine specimens collected from employees shall be split into two specimens, a “primary”
and a “split” specimen. If the analysis of the primary specimen confirms the presence of
illegal drugs, the employee will have seventy-two hours to request the split specimen be
sent to another approved and certified laboratory for testing. The employee shall pay the
cost of the additional test and all handling and shipping costs associated with the
additional test at the time such test is requested. Any costs associated with such split

sample testing which are not paid by an employee who tests positive for alcohol or drugs
shall be deducted from any funds or pay due to the employee.

SUSPENSION PENDING RECEIPT OF TEST RESULTS:

Employees will be suspended, with pay, pending receipt of the “primary” drug/alcohol
test results.

ENFORCEMENT OF POLICY:

Applicants for employment performing safety-sensitive functions with the City who test
positive for illegal drugs shall be denied employment. Such applicants shall be notified,
in writing, that they were rejected on the basis of a positive test result. Employees who
test positive for drugs or alcohol under the provisions of this policy shall be terminated
from employment. Employees who refuse to participate in a drug or alcohol test or
refuse to execute the required consent forms or fail to complete all appropriate paperwork
shall be terminated from employment. Refusing to produce sufficient urine or breath for
analysis shall be deemed to be a refusal to participate in the test. Any attempt to alter,
substitute or tamper with the testing procedure or the test specimen shall also be
considered a refusal to participate in the test.

UNPAID LEAVE FOR EVALUATION AND TREATMENT:

Employees who voluntarily admit having a problem with alcohol or illegal drugs prior to
being apprehended for a violation of this policy may be allowed to take annual leave, sick
leave or leave without pay to participate in a rehabilitation program if the City will not
suffer any undue hardship as a consequence of granting such leave. If such leave is
granted, the employce shall immediately be evaluated by a substance abuse professional
who shall determine what help, if any, the employee needs in resolving his/her problem.
The employee shall execute a consent form allowing the substance abuse professional to
release information concerning the employee’s evaluation and rehabilitation program to
the City. Prior to returning to work, a substance abuse professional familiar with the
employee’s evaluation and treatment shall render a report to the City certifying that the
employee has properly followed and completed any prescribed rehabilitation program.

REHIRING TERMINATED EMPLOYEES:

Former employees who are terminated as a result of a violation of this policy may be
eligible for consideration for future job openings in accordance with routine City hiring
practices provided the applicant presents; (1) a statement from a qualified substance
abuse professional which certifies that the former employee has successfully completed a
proper rehabilitation program, and (2) satisfactory evidence that the former employee
has not used the problem substance(s) during the preceding six months.

Such evidence may be in the form of rehabilitation program reports, support group
attendance records, test results, successful employment in another job without

performance, conduct or attendance deficiencies or similar information. This paragraph
does not, however, impose any obligation upon the City to rehire any former employee or
give any former employee any preference for employment.

TESTING RECORDS:

Employee drug and alcohol test results and related records shall be maintained under
strict confidentiality. Upon written request, an employee shall receive copies of any
records pertaining to his/her use of drugs or alcohol, including any records pertaining to
his/her drug or alcohol tests. Records shall be made available to subsequent employers or
other identified persons only as expressly requested in writing by the employee.

DRUG AND ALCOHOL TESTING POLICY FOR
EMPLOYEES REQUIRED TO POSSESS COMMERCIAL DRIVER’S
LICENSES AND OTHER EMPLOYEES PERFORMING
SAFETY-SENSITIVE FUNCTIONS

City of Poquoson employees who are required to possess a commercial driver’s license as
part of their job duties and all other employees of the City of Poquoson (“the City”) who
perform “safety-sensitive functions” (“‘covered employees”) shall be subject to a drug and
alcohol testing program that fulfills the requirements of Title 49, Part 382 of the Code of
Federal Regulations. Testing procedures and facilities used for such tests shall conform
with the requirements of Title 49, Section 40, et seq. of the Code of Federal Regulations.

Pre-Employment Drug Tests

A drug test shall be conducted prior to an applicant for employment as a covered
employee being hired to perform any safety-sensitive function for the City. The
definition of “safety-sensitive function” includes all on-duty functions performed from
the time an employee begins work or is required to be ready to work until he/she is
relieved from work and all responsibility for performing work. It includes driving;
waiting to be dispatched; inspecting and servicing equipment; supervising, performing or
assisting in loading and unloading; repairing or obtaining and waiting for help with a
disabled vehicle; performing driver requirements related to accidents; and performing any
other work for the City.

The drug test shall be required of a job applicant only after he/she has been offered
employment, however, as part of the job application process, all applicants shall execute
releases and consent forms which authorize the City to obtain the results of such testing.
All applicants shall also be required to execute releases and consent forms which
authorize the City to contact all employers whom the applicant has worked for during the
prior two years in order to obtain all records pertaining to the applicant’s drug and
alcohol use during that time, including any records regarding the applicant’s refusal to
submit to testing for drug and alcohol use. Any offer of employment will be withdrawn
from an applicant who refuses to submit to the required pre-employment drug test or who
tests positive or drugs in such test.

Post-Accident Tests

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted as soon after a vehicular accident as practicable
on any employee:

1. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle
involved in the accident if the accident involved loss of human life; or

2. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle
involved in the accident if the accident resulted in bodily injury to a person
who, as a result of the injury, immediately received medical treatment away
from the accident scene; or

3. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle
involved in the accident if the accident caused disabling damage to one or
more motor vehicles which required the vehicle(s) to be transported away
from the scene of the accident by a tow truck or similar vehicle and the
employee received a citation under state or local law for a moving traffic
violation arising from the accident.

The term “accident” shall not include occurrences which:
1. Involve only boarding and alighting from a stationary motor vehicle; or
2. involve only the loading or unloading of cargo.

In the absence of the need for immediate medical treatment following an accident, all
covered employees shall make themselves readily available for the required testing. Any
covered employee involved in a motor vehicle accident which requires post-accident
testing pursuant to this policy shall not use alcohol for eight hours after the accident, or
until he/she undergoes a post-accident alcohol test, whichever occurs first. Employees
will not be tested, however, unless the test for alcohol can be administered within eight
hours after the accident and the test for drugs can be administered within thirty-two
hours. If an alcohol test is not administered to a covered employee within two hours after
the accident or if a drug test is not administered within thirty-two hours, the City shall
prepare and maintain records explaining why such tests were not conducted. Tests
conducted by authorized federal, state or local officials shall fulfill post-accident testing
requirements provided such tests conform to applicable legal requirements and the results
of such tests are obtained by the City in a timely manner. Breath tests will only be used
to determine the presence or absence of alcohol and will! not constitute a test for the
presence or abuse of drugs.

Random Tests

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted on covered employees on a random basis at
unannounced times throughout the year. Tests for alcohol shall be conducted just before,
during or just after the performance of safety-sensitive functions. The number of random
alcohol tests annually shall equal at least 25% of the average number of employee
positions. The number of random drug tests annually shall equal at least 50% of the
average number of covered employee positions. Covered employees shall be selected by

a scientifically valid random process and each employee shall have an equal chance of
being tested each time selections are made.

Reasonable Suspicion Tests

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted when a supervisor or City official, trained in
accordance with the requirements of Section 382.201 et seq of Title 49 of the Code of
Federal Regulations has reasonable suspicion that a covered employee has violated the
City’s alcohol or drug prohibitions. “Reasonable suspicion” shall be based on specific,
contemporaneous, articulable observations concerning the employee’s appearance,
behavior, speech or body odors. Such observations may include indications of the
chronic use and withdrawal effects of controlled substances. Alcohol tests are authorized
for reasonable suspicion only if the required observations are made during, just before or
just after the period of the workday when the covered employee must comply with
alcohol prohibitions. An alcohol test may not be conducted by the person who
determines that reasonable suspicion exists to conduct such a test. If an alcohol test is not
administered within two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion, the City shall
prepare and maintain a record explaining why this was not done. No attempt to conduct
alcohol tests shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of
eight hours after the determination of reasonable suspicion.

A supervisor or City official who makes observations leading to a reasonable suspicion
test for drugs shall make a written record of his/her observations within twenty-four
hours of the observed behavior or before the results of the drug test are released,
whichever is earlier.

Enforcement

Any covered employee who refuses to submit to a post-accident, random, reasonable
suspicion, return to duty or follow-up tests shall not perform or continue to perform
safety-sensitive functions and will be subject to discipline up to, and including, dismissal
from employment. Covered employees who test positive for alcohol or drugs shall be
subject to disciplinary action up to, and including, dismissal from employment. Covered
employees who test positive for an alcohol concentration of 0.02 or greater but less than
0.04 shall not be allowed to perform safety-sensitive functions for twenty-four hours
following administration of the test. At a minimum, a covered employee will be
suspended from work without pay for that time period.

Referral, Evaluation and Treatment

A covered employee who violates the City’s prohibitions related to drugs and alcohol
shall receive from the City the names, addresses and telephone numbers of substance
abuse professionals and counseling and treatment programs available to evaluate and
resolve drug and alcohol-related problems. Such information shall be provided even if
the employee is terminated from employment with the city. If an employee is not
terminated after a violation of this policy, the employee shall be evaluated by a substance

abuse professional who shall determine what help, if any, the employee needs in
resolving his/her problem. Any substance abuse professional who determines that an
employee needs assistance shall not refer the employee to a private practice, person or
organization from which the substance abuse professional receives remuneration or in
which he/she has a financial interest. Any covered employee identified as needing help
in resolving a drug or alcohol problem shal! be evaluated by a substance abuse
professional to determine that he/she has properly followed the prescribed rehabilitation
program and shall be subject to unannounced follow-up tests after returning to duty.

Return-to-Duty Tests

A drug or alcohol test shall be conducted if a covered employee who has violated the
City’s drug or alcohol prohibition returns to performing safety-sensitive duties.
Employees whose conduct involved alcohol will not be allowed to return to duty in a
safety-sensitive function until the return-to-duty alcohol test produces a verified result
that meets all federal, state and local standards. Covered employees whose conduct
involved drugs shall not be allowed to return to duty in a safety-sensitive function until
the return-to-duty drug test produces a verified, negative result.

Follow-up Tests

A covered employee who violates the City’s drug or alcohol prohibition and is
subsequently identified by a substance abuse professional as needing assistance in
resolving a drug or alcohol problem shall be subject to unannounced, follow-up testing as
directed by the substance abuse professional in accordance with the provisions of Section
382.605 of the Code of Federal Regulations. Follow-up alcohol testing shall be
conducted just before, during or just after the time when the covered employee is
performing safety-sensitive functions.

Employee Request To Retest Urine Specimen

Urine specimens collected from covered employees shall be split into two specimens, a
“primary” and a “split” specimen. If the analysis of the primary specimen confirms the
presence of illegal, controlled substances, the employee will have seventy-two hours to
request the split specimen be sent to another approved and certified laboratory for testing.

DRUG AND ALCOHOL TESTING POLICY
FOR CDL AND SAFETY SENSITIVE POSITIONS

City of Poquoson employees who are required to possess a commercial driver’s license as
part of their job duties and all other employees of the City of Poquoson (“the City”) who
perform “safety-sensitive functions” (“covered employees’) shall be subject to a drug and
alcohol testing program that fulfills the requirements of Title 49, Part 382 of the Code of
Federal Regulations. Testing procedures and facilities used for such tests shall conform
with the requirements of Title 49, Section 40, et seq. of the Code of Federal Regulations.

Pre-Employment Drug Tests

A drug test shall be conducted prior to an applicant for employment being hired to
perform any safety-sensitive function for the City. The definition of “safety-sensitive
function” includes all on-duty functions performed from the time an employee begins
work or is required to be ready to work until he/she is relieved from work and all
responsibility for performing work. It includes driving; waiting to be dispatched;
inspecting and servicing equipment; supervising, performing or assisting in loading and
unloading; repairing or obtaining and waiting for help with a disabled vehicle;
performing driver requirements related to accidents; and performing any other work for
the City.

The drug test shall be required of a job applicant only after he/she has been offered
employment, however, as part of the job application process, all applicants shall execute
releases and consent forms which authorize the City to obtain the results of such testing.
All applicants shall also be required to execute releases and consent forms which
authorize the City to contact all employers whom the applicant has worked for during the
prior two years in order to obtain all records pertaining to the applicant’s drug and
alcohol use during that time, including any records regarding the applicant’s refusal to
submit to testing for drug and alcohol use. Any offer of employment will be withdrawn
from an applicant who refuses to submit to the required pre-employment drug test or who
tests positive for drugs in such test.

Post-Accident Tests

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted as soon after a vehicular accident as practicable
on any employee:

1. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle
involved in the accident if the accident involved loss of human life; or

2. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle

involved in the accident if the accident resulted in bodily injury to a person
who, as a result of the injury, immediately received medical treatment away
from the accident scene; or

3. Who was performing safety-sensitive functions with respect to the vehicle
involved in the accident if the accident caused disabling damage to one or
more motor vehicles which required the vehicle(s) to be transported away
from the scene of the accident by a tow truck or similar vehicle and the
employee received a citation under state or local law for a moving traffic
violation arising from the accident.

The term “accident” shall not include occurrences which:
1. Involve only boarding and alighting from a stationary motor vehicle; or
2. involve only the loading or unloading of cargo.

In the absence of the need for immediate medical treatment following an accident, all
covered employees shall make themsclves readily available for the required testing. Any
covered employee involved in a motor vehicle accident which requires post-accident
testing pursuant to this policy shall not use alcohol for eight hours after the accident, or
until he/she undergoes a post-accident alcohol test, whichever occurs first. Employees
will not be tested, however, unless the test for alcohol can be administered within eight
hours after the accident and the test for drugs can be administered within thirty-two
hours. If an alcohol test is not administered to a covered employee within two hours after
the accident or if a drug test is not administered within thirty-two hours, the City shall
prepare and maintain records explaining why such tests were not conducted. Tests
conducted by authorized federal, state or local officials shall fulfill post-accident testing
requirements provided such tests conform to applicable legal requirements and the results
of such tests are obtained by the City in a timely manner. Breath tests will only be used
to determine the presence or absence of alcohol and will not constitute a test for the
presence or abuse of drugs.

Random Tests

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted on covered employees on a random basis at
unannounced times throughout the year. Tests for alcohol shall be conducted just before,
during or just after the performance of safety-sensitive functions. The number of random
alcohol tests annually shall equal at least 25% of the average number of employee
positions. The number of random drug tests annually shall equal at least 50% of the
average number of covered employee positions. Covered employees shall be selected by
a scientifically valid random process and each employee shall have an equal chance of
being tested each time selections are made.

Reasonable Suspicion Tests

Alcohol and drug tests shall be conducted when a supervisor or City official, trained in
accordance with the requirements of Section 382.201 et seq of Title 49 of the Code of
Federal Regulations has reasonable suspicion that a covered employee has violated the
City’s alcohol or drug prohibitions. “Reasonable suspicion” shall be based on specific,
contemporaneous, articulable observations concerning the employee’s appearance,
behavior, speech or body odors. Such observations may include indications of the
chronic use and withdrawal effects of controlled substances. Alcohol tests are authorized
for reasonable suspicion only if the required observations are made during, just before or
just after the period of the workday when the covered employee must comply with
alcohol prohibitions. An alcohol test may not be conducted by the person who
determines that reasonable suspicion exists to conduct such a test. If an alcohol test is not
administered within two hours of a determination of reasonable suspicion, the City shall
prepare and maintain a record explaining why this was not done. No attempt to conduct
alcohol tests shall continue if such tests cannot be conducted prior to the expiration of
eight hours after the determination of reasonable suspicion.

A supervisor or City official who makes observations leading to a reasonable suspicion
test for drugs shall make a written record of his/her observations within twenty-four
hours of the observed behavior or before the results of the drug test are released,
whichever is earlier.

Enforcement

Any covered employee who refuses to submit to a post-accident, random, reasonable
suspicion, return to duty or follow-up tests shall not perform or continue to perform
safety-sensitive functions and will be subject to discipline up to, and including, dismissal
from employment. Covered employees who test positive for alcohol or drugs shall be
subject to disciplinary action up to, and including, dismissal from employment. Covered
employees who test positive for an alcohol concentration of 0.02 or greater but less than
0.04 shall not be allowed to perform safety-sensitive functions for twenty-four hours
following administration of the test. At a minimum, a covered employee will be
suspended from work without pay for that time period.

Referral, Evaluation and Treatment

A covered employee who violates the City’s prohibitions related to drugs and alcohol
shall receive from the City the names, addresses and telephone numbers of substance
abuse professionals and counseling and treatment programs available to evaluate and
resolve drug and alcohol-related problems. Such information shall be provided even if
the employee is terminated from employment with the city. If an employee is not
terminated after a violation of this policy, the employee shall be evaluated by a substance
abuse professional who shall determine what help, if any, the employee needs in
resolving his/her problem. Any substance abuse professional who determines that an
employee needs assistance shall not refer the employee to a private practice, person or

organization from which the substance abuse professional receives remuneration or in
which he/she has a financial interest. Any covered employee identified as needing help
in resolving a drug or alcohol problem shall be evaluated by a substance abuse
professional to determine that he/she has properly followed the prescribed rehabilitation
program and shall be subject to unannounced follow-up tests after returning to duty.

Return-to-Duty Tests

A drug or alcohol test shall be conducted if a covered employee who has violated the
City’s drug or alcohol prohibition returns to performing safety-sensitive duties.
Employees whose conduct involved alcohol will not be allowed to return to duty in a
safety-sensitive function until the return-to-duty alcohol test produces a verified result
that meets all federal, state and local standards. Covered employees whose conduct
involved drugs shall not be allowed to return to duty in a safety-sensitive function until
the return-to-duty drug test produces a verified, negative result.

Follow-up Tests

A covered employee who violates the City’s drug or alcohol prohibition and is
subsequently identified by a substance abuse professional as needing assistance in
resolving a drug or alcohol problem shall be subject to unannounced, follow-up testing as
directed by the substance abuse professional in accordance with the provisions of Section
382.605 of the Code of Federal Regulations. Follow-up alcohol testing shall be
conducted just before, during or just after the time when the covered employee is
performing safety-sensitive functions.

PERSONNEL POLICY
City of Poquoson, Virginia

Policy Title: Grievance Procedure Effective Date:
Section No.: 41 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson to provide eligible employees with an
expeditious and impartial method for the resolution of employee grievances.

SCOPE:

This policy concems the grievance procedure available to eligible employees, as further
defined herein. This policy shall conform to Virginia Code Section 2.2-3000 et seq and
Virginia Code Section 15.2-1506 et seq.

DEFINITIONS:
Grievance

A grievance is defined as a complaint or dispute by an employee relating to his/her
employment, including, but not necessarily limited to:

1. Formal disciplinary actions, including suspensions, demotions, transfers and
assignments, and dismissals provided that such dismissals result from formal
discipline or unsatisfactory job performance.

2. The application of all written personnel policies, procedures, rules and
Regulations where it can be shown that policy was misapplied or unfairly
applied.

3. Discrimination on the basis of race, color, religion, political affiliation, age,
disability, national origin or sex.

4. Acts of retaliation as a result of the use or participation in the grievance
procedure or because the employee has complied with any law of the United
States or of the Commonwealth of Virginia, has reported any violation of such
law to a governmental authority, has sought any change in law before the
Congress of the United States or the General Assembly or has reported an
incidence of fraud, abuse, or gross mismanagement. For the purposes of this

section, three shall be a rebuttable presumption that increasing the penalty
that is the subject of the grievance at any level of the grievance shall be an act
of retaliation.
Personnel Director
For the purpose of this Policy, the Assistant City Manager is designated as the Personnel
Officer.
PROCEDURES:
Coverage of Personnel
1. All full-time and permanent part-time employces occupying authorized
positions, as approved by the City Manager, are eligible to file grievances
under this procedure with the following exceptions:
a. Key officials of the City. For purposes of this procedure, a key official
is defined as an individual occupying the position of Department
Head, Assistant City Manager, or Assistant to the City Manager. In
lieu of grievance rights a key official may be eligible, upon approval
of the City Manager, to receive severance pay equal to three
(3) months of regular pay in cases of involuntary separation where
the separation was not due to a violation of law or gross subordination.
b. Members of boards or commissions.

c. Employees whose terms of employment are limited by law.

d. Officials and employees who serve at the will or pleasure of an
appointing authority.

e. Appointees of elected individuals or elected groups.
f. Probationary employees.
g. Temporary, limited term and seasonal employees.

h. After the effective date of the resignation, any employee who has
voluntarily resigned his/her position within the City workforce.

i. Law-enforcement officers as defined in Chapter 5 (Section 9.1-500 et
seq.) of Title 9.1 whose grievance is subject to the provisions of

Chapter 10.1 and who have elected to proceed pursuant to those
provisions in the resolution of their grievance, or any other employee

electing to proceed pursuant to any other existing procedure in the
resolution of his/her grievance.

2. The City Manager shall determine the Key Officials of the City (by position
title) excluded from this grievance procedure and shall maintain in the City
Manager’s Office a list of such excluded positions.

City Management Rights and Prerogatives

The City reserves the exclusive right to manage the affairs and operations of the City

government. Accordingly, complaints involving the following management rights and
prerogatives are not grievable:

1. Establishment and revision of wages or salaries, position classification or
general benefits.

2. Work activity accepted by the employee as a condition of employment, or

work activity which may reasonably be expected to be a part of the job
content.

3. The contents of ordinances, statutes or established personnel policies,
procedures, rules and regulations.

. The failure to promote except where the employee can show that established
promotional policies or procedures were not followed or applied fairly.

5 The methods, means, and personnel by which work activities are to be carried
on.

6. Except where such action affects an employee who has been reinstated within
the previous six (6) months as the result of the final determination of a
grievance, termination, layoff, demotion or suspension from duties because of
lack of work, reduction in workforce, or job abolition. In any grievance
brought under this exception, the action will be upheld upon a showing by the
City that: (i) there was a valid business reason for the action and (ii) the

employee was notified of the reason in writing prior to the effective date of
the action.

7. The hiring, promotion, transfer, assignment and retention of employees within
the City government.

8. The relief of employees from duties of the City in emergencies.

Grievabililty and Access
1. Determinations of Grievability and Access

a. Decisions regarding grievability and access to the procedure shall be
made by the City Manager or his/her designee, at any time prior to the
panel hearing, at the request of the City or the grievant, within ten (10)
calendar days of the request.

b. Determinations of grievability and access shall be made by the City
Manager. No City Attorney or Commonwealth Attorney shall be
authorized to decide the question of grievability.

c. The City Manager shall provide a written ruling about grievability and
access to the grievant, the affected Department Head and the Personnel
Officer.

2. Grievabililty and Access Determination Appeals

a. Decisions of the City Manager, or his/her designee, may be appealed to
the York/Poquoson Circuit Court for a hearing on the issues of
grievability and access.

b. Proceedings for review of the decision of the City Manager or his/her
designee shall be instituted by the grievant by filing a notice of appeal
with the City Manager within ten (10) calendar days from the date of
receipt of the decision and giving a copy thereof to all other parties.

c. Within ten (10) calendar days thereafter. the City Manager or his/her
designee shall transmit to the Clerk of the York County/City of
Poquoson Circuit Court the following: a copy of the decision of the
City Manager, a copy of the notice of appeal, and the exhibits. A list
of the evidence furnished to the Court shall also be furnished to the
grievant. The failure of the City Manager or his/her designee to
transmit the record shall not prejudice the rights of the grievant. The
court, on motion of the grievant, may issue a writ of certiorari requiring
the City Manager to transmit the record on or before a certain date.

d. Within thirty (30) days of receipt of such records by the clerk, the
court, sitting without a jury, shall hear the appeal on the record
transmitted by the City Manager or his/her designee and such
additional evidence as may be necessary to resolve any controversy as
to the correctness of the record. The court, in its discretion, may
teceive such other evidence as the ends of justice require. The court

may affirm the decision of the City Manager or his/her designee, or
may reverse or modify the decision. The decision of the court shall be
rendered no later than the fifteenth 1s") day from the date of the
conclusion of the hearing. The decision of the court is final and is not
appealable.

Documents Relating to the Actions Grieved

Absent just cause, all documents, as defined in the Rules of the Supreme Court of
Virginia, relating to the actions grieved shall be made available, upon request from a
party to the grievance, by the opposing party, in a timely fashion. Upon such request a
party shall have a duty to search its records to ensure that all such relevant documents are
provided. Documents pertaining to nonparties that are relevant to the grievance shall be
produced in such a manner as to preserve the privacy of the individuals not personally
involved in the grievance. A party shall not be required to create a document if the
document does not exist.

c. The immediate supervisor shall have authority to resolved the
complaint if it involves actions within his/her control.

d. A decision regarding resolution of the grievance by the immediate

grievant within ten (10) calendar days of the identification of the

Rules Governing the Conduct of Grievance Hearings

Except as otherwise noted, the following rules apply to all levels of grievance
hearings.

1. Role of the Personnel Officer.

a. The Personnel Officer, or his/her designee, shall advise both employees
and supervisors in matters concerning this grievance procedure.

b. Where a grievant appeals to either Step 3 or Step 4, the Personnel
Officer, or his/her designee, shall transmit the grievance record to the
City Manager or grievance panel at the next higher level.

c. During Step 3 and Step 4 hearings, the Personnel Officer, or his/her
designee, shall serve as facilitator to the City Manager and grievance
panel and he/she shall also advise the City Manager and grievance
panel on personnel-related matters.

d. Neither the Personnel Officer nor his/her designee shall be present
during the grievance panel’s private deliberations and decision-making
process.

e. The Personnel Officer shall not serve on behalf of the City Manager
for Step 3 hearings and the Personnel Officer shall not serve as a
member of the grievance panel for Step 4 hearings.

2. Timelines, Deadlines and Hours for Holding Grievances

a. Time intervals specified in Steps | through 4 may be extended by
mutual consent of the parties.

b. When a deadline falls on Saturday, Sunday, or a City holiday, the next
calendar day that is not a Saturday, Sunday or a City holiday shall be
considered the last calendar day.

c. As far as practical, all grievance hearings shall be held during normal
City working hours as defined by the City of Poquoson Personnel
Policy Manual.

3. Participants in the Process

City employees who are necessary participants at grievance hearings shall
not lose pay for time lost from their jobs and shall not be charged leave because of
attendance at such hearings.

4. Recording Devices

a. The use of either a recording devices or a court reporter is not
permitted at Step 1, 2 or 3 hearings.

b. Only Step 4 hearings may be recorded.

c. Where a Step 4 hearing is recorded, it shall be the responsibility of the
Personnel Officer, or his/her designee, to either make the recording or
hire a court reporter to transcribe the hearing. There shall be no other
recording permitted.

d. Ifthe grievant desires a transcript of the hearing, he/she shall bear the
costs thereof,

5. Hearing Protocol

a. Hearings are not intended to be conducted like proceedings in court
and the rules of evidence do not necessarily apply.

b_ The grievant shall present his/her evidence first.

c. The grievance panel shall determine the propriety of and the weight to
be given the evidence submitted.

d. Both the grievant and the City may call appropriate witnesses. All
witnesses including the grievant shall be subject to examination and
cross-examination.

e. Witnesses shall be present only while actually giving testimony.

f. The City enjoys a rebuttable presumption of correctness and it shall be
the responsibility of the grievant to demonstrate that the actions taken
by the City were contrary to established policy or law.

6. Relief

a. The grievant shall not be entitled to recover more than that which
he/she has lost.

b. Where a grievant has obtained partial relief at one level of this
grievance procedure but decides to appeal to the next higher level, the
filing of a “Request for Grievance Hearing” form to the next higher
level shall constitute a rejection and relinquishment of any claim to

any and all relief granted at the previous level.
7. Expenses

Each party shall bear the costs and expenses, if any, of his/her legal counsel or
representative.

Rules Governing the Conduct of Grievance Panels and Panel Hearings

1. Selection of Three Member Grievance Panel

a.

Within ten (10) calendar days of receipt of the Step 4 portion of the
“Request for Grievance Hearing” form, the City Manager, or his/her
designee, shall appoint one of the three members to serve on the
grievance panel; the grievant shall appoint one of the three members

to serve on the grievance panel; and these two panel members shall
jointly select a third member who shall serve as the panel chair within
ten (10) calendar days.

In the event that agreement cannot be reached as to the final panel
member, the Chief Judge of the 9" Judicial Circuit, which is the
judicial district that encompasses the City of Poquoson, shall select
the third panel member.

The third member selected by the respective parties’ panel members
shall serve as the chairperson of the panel.

2. Eligibility to Serve on a Grievance Panel

a. The panel shall not be composed of any persons having direct

involvement with the grievance being heard by the panel, or with the
complaint or dispute giving rise to the grievance.

Managers who are in a direct line of supervision of a grievant, persons
residing in the same household as the grievant, and the following
relatives of a participant in the grievance process or a participant’s
spouse are prohibited from serving as panel members: spouse, parent,
child, descendants of a child, sibling, niece, nephew and first cousin.

No attorney having direct involvement with the subject matter of the
grievance, nor a partner, associate, employee or co-employee of the
attorney shall serve as a panel member.

Panel Hearing Protocol

I.

Hearing Date, Time and Location - The panel chairman shall promptly set the
date, time and location for hearing the grievance and shall notify the parties.

Privacy of Hearing — The panel has the discretion to determine the propriety
of attendance at the hearing of persons not having a direct interest in the
hearing, and, at the request of either party, the hearing shall be private.

Prior to the hearing, neither the grievant, grievant’s attorney, nor the
city’s attorney shall discuss with members of the grievance panel the
substantive issues to be presented to the panel. Any violation of this
provision shall disqualify the panel member from participating in the
hearing.

4. Grievance Record and Associated Documentations

a. The Personnel Officer shall provide the panel with copies of the
grievance record prior to the hearing.

b. The Personnel Officer shall provide the grievant with a list of the
documents furnished to the panel, and the grievant and his attorney, at
least ten (10) days prior to the scheduled hearing, shall be allowed
access to and copies of all relevant files intended to be used in the
grievance proceeding.

c. The grievant and City shall simultaneously exchange copies of all
documents, exhibits and a list of witnesses intended to be used at the
panel hearing at least five (5) days in advance of the hearing.

5. Representation

Both the grievant and the City may be represented by legal counsel and/or
other representative at the panel hearing. Such representative may
examine, cross-examine, question, and present evidence on behalf of the
grievant or the City before the panel without being in violation of the
provisions of Virginia Code Section 54.1-3904. (“Penalty for practicing
without authority’).

6. Evidence

a. The panel shall have the authority to determine the admissibility of
evidence without regard to the burden of proof, or the order of
presentation of evidence, so long as full and equal opportunity is
afforded to all parties of the presentation of their evidence.

b. All evidence shall be presented in the presence of the panel and the
parties except by mutual consent of the parties.

7. Panel Authority to Award
a. The panel shall have the authority, if it finds based on the greater
weight of the evidence that the grievant has been denied a benefit or
wrongly disciplined without just cause (where such cause is required),
to order appropriate remedies.

b. Relief may include:

1. Reinstatement to the same position, or if the position is filled, to
an equivalent position;

2. Back pay; however, the back pay shall not exceed pay for time
actually lost due to suspension or discharge and any award of
back pay shall be offset by interim unemployment earnings the
grievant earned during the period of separation.

3. Full reinstatement of fringe benefits and seniority rights;

4. Mitigation or reduction of the agency disciplinary action; or,

5. Any combination of these remedies.

c. Inno case does a panel have authority to award damages or attorney
fees.

d. The panel has the authority to sustain the City’s action.

8. Panel Award Limitations

The panel shall not have authority to do any of the following:
(1) Formulate policies or procedures;
(2) Alter existing policies or procedures;

(3) Circumscribe or modify the rights of the City as outlined in
this procedure; or

(4) Exonerate an employee from all discipline when the guilt of

the employee is admitted or is beyond question in the view of
the panel.

9. Panel Decision

a. The decision of the panel should be rendered as soon as possible, but in

any case, not later than five (5) calendar days following the conclusion
of the hearing.

b. The decision shall be in writing and contain findings of facts as to the
material issues in the case and the basis for those findings.

c. The majority decision of the panel, acting within the scope of its
authority, shall be final and binding, subject to existing policies,
procedures and law.

d. The question of whether the relief granted by a panel is consistent with
written policy shall be determined by the City Manager or his/her
designee except if he/she has a direct personal involvement with the
event or events giving rise to the grievance, in which case the decision
shall be made by the Commonwealth’s Attorney for the City of
Poquoson.

e. Either party may petition the York County/City of Poquoson Circuit
Court for an order requiring implementation of the panel decision.

8. Expenses

Compliance

lL.

a. The grievant shall bear the reasonable costs and expenses, if any, of
his/her panel member.

b. The City shall bear the reasonable costs and expenses, if any, of its
panel member and those of the third panel member unless the grievant
objects. Upon objection, the reasonable costs and expenses of the
third panel member shall be shared equally between the City and the
grievant.

c. No person shall receive any compensation, whether monetary or
otherwise, for his/her time in serving as a member of a grievance
panel. Notwithstanding this prohibition, a City employee serving as a
member of a grievance panel may receive his/her usual City salary for
the period he/she serves on such panel.

After the initial filing of a “Request for Grievance Hearing” form, failure
of either party to comply with all substantial procedural requirements of
the grievance procedure, including the panel or administrative hearing,
without just cause shall result in a decision in favor of the other party on
any grievable issue, provided the party not in compliance fails to correct

the noncompliance within five (5) workdays of receipt of written
notification by the other party of the compliance violation. Such written
notification by the grievant shall be made to the City Manager.

. The City Manager, or his/her designee, at his/her option, may require a

clear written explanation of the basis for just cause extensions or
exceptions. The City Manager, or his/her designee, shall determine
compliance issues. Compliance determinations made by the City Manager
shall be subject to judicial review by filing petition with the York
County/City of Poquoson Circuit Court within thirty (30) days of the
compliance determination.

Voluntary Mediation

1. Voluntary mediation is offered as an alternative means to addressing and
resolving workplace disputes. Mediation is available to all City
government employees including those in probationary status, and is
independent of anygrievance rights an employee may have.

2. The Employee Mediation program is a means by which a neutral third
party assists employees and management experiencing conflict in
discussing issues in an open, honest and confidential forum to arrive at a

mutually satisfactory Agreement.

3. The Personnel Officer, or his/her designee, functions as the Program
Coordinator and oversees the City’s Employee Mediation program. All
requests for mediation must be referred to the Program Coordinator.

Policy Title: Resignations and Separations Effective Date:
Section No.: 42 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

It is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to identify types of separation from
City government employment

SCOPE:

This policy applies to all City of Poquoson employees.
DEFINITIONS:

Dismissal

Dismissal is defined as the involuntary separation of a probationary employee or other
employee who has no appeal rights.

Lay-Off

Lay-off is defined as separation from City government employment as a result of lack of
funds or work or the elimination of one or more program functions.

Resignation

Resignation is defined as the voluntary separation of an employee from City government
service.

Retirement

Retirement is defined as separation from City government employment wherein an
eligible employee receives a fixed benefit payment.

Unsatisfactory Service Separation

Unsatisfactory service separation is defined as the involuntary separation of an employee,
who has appeal rights, for unsatisfactory performance or violations of policy.

PROCEDURES:

A. Types of Separations

Employee separations shall be designated as one of the following types:

Gace bi GS

death;

dismissal;

layoff;

resignation;

retirement; or

unsatisfactory service separation.

B. Death

The death of an active employee shall be reported to the City Manager as soon as
a department is aware. All compensation and benefits to include accumulated
annual leave and any sick Icave duc the deceased employee as of the date of death
shall be paid in accordance with applicable laws.

C. Dismissal

L.

In situations of dismissal, whenever possible, the employee shall be given
advance notice of the dismissal.

2. An employee who is dismissed shall be entitled to payment for compensatory
leave; shall not be entitled to any accrucd but unexpended sick leave; and may
be entitled to accrued annual leave in accordance with policies governing
leave and upon approval of the City Manager.

D. Layoff

lL.

The City Manager shall have the authority to lay-off any employee(s) because
of lack of funds, lack of work, or when there has been an insufficient
appropriation to meet salary requirements necessary to maintain existing
personnel in any department. A reduction in the number of employees in a
job classification shall be made by recommendation of the City Manager with
the concurrence of City Council. The City Manager shall give written notice
to any employee being separated at least one month prior to the effective date
contained in the notice. Whenever it becomes necessary to reduce the number
of employees in a given job classification and notice of such lay-off is given
to the affected employee(s) for any of the reasons enumerated herein, this
notice shall be final and not subject to appeal or grievance.

2. All permanent and permanent part-time employees, except grant funded
positions, who are laid off will be entitled to receive payment for their
recorded, accrued time, the same as if they had resigned in good standing. In
addition, one month’s severance pay will be granted.

E. Resignation

An employee who desires to resign in good standing shall submit his/her written
resignation to his/her supervisor or Department Head at least fourteen (14)
calendar days prior to leaving, and must state the date the resignation shall
become effective. Failure to comply with this procedure shall be considered
cause for denying such employee future employment by the City.

F. Retirement
1. Service Retirement

Retirement benefits are available to all full-time employees through the
Virginia Retirement System. Retirement guidelines are disclosed in the VRS
Benefits Manual and requirements are set forth by the State of Virginia. More
detailed information is found on the VRS website at www.varetire.org. The
City pays its required employer share to VRS for each employee. Employees
contribute 5% of their salary towards retirement on a pre-tax basis.

The retirement plan provides retirement benefits for full-time employees.
Employees are classified in one of three categories: Plan 1, Plan 2 or Hybrid
employees.

Employees whose membership date is before July 1, 2010 or who were
vested as of January 1, 2013 are covered under the provisions of the VRS Plan
I.

Employees whose membership date is on or after July 1, 2010 and before
January 1, 2014, or their membership date is before July 1, 2010 and they
were not vested as of January 1, 2013 are covered under the provisions of the
VRS Plan 2. Employees who were previously employed in a covered position
and withdrew their member contribution and interest, or their full account
balance in an optional retirement plan authorized or administered by VRS,
will be rehired under Plan 2 if they return to covered employment.

Under either Plan | or Plan 2, for full-time employees, there are Enhanced
Hazardous Duty coverage for local law enforcement officers and firefighters.

Employees, excluding hazardous duty positions, whose membership date is
January 1, 2014 or later or previous Plan | or Plan 2 employees who opt into

the VRS Hybrid Plan are covered under the provisions of the VRS Hybrid
Plan

2. Disability Retirement/Lifetime Disability Income

If Plan 1 or Plan 2 full-time employees cannot work because of a disability
that is likely to be permanent, they may be eligible to retire on a disability
under the Virginia Retirement System (VRS). The disability may be non-
work related or work related. VRS administers disability retirement benefits
in conjunction with a medical board comprised of practicing board-certified
physicians and other healthcare professionals. More detailed information is
found on the VRS website at www.varetire.org. Hybrid employees will
receive disability benefits equivalent to or better than the VRS Plan.

Policy Title: Retiree Health Insurance Coverage Effective Date: 9/12/2016
Section No.: 43 Supersedes Policy:
PURPOSE:

[t is the objective of the City of Poquoson, Virginia to provide health insurance to its
retired employees through the City’s Health Insurance Plan. The following policies
govern administration of this plan. Any policies not specifically addressed here will be
as delineated in the policy by the Administrator of the City’s Health Insurance Plan.

Retirees Under 65

1. Eligibility

In order for the retired employee to be eligible for health insurance coverage
he/she must have a combination of years of service with the City of Poquoson
and age which add up to seventy. For example, an employee at the age 50 must
have 20 years of service, at age 55 must have 15 years of service, and at age 60
must have 10 years of service. Retired employees in all cases must have a
minimum of 5 years of service to become eligible for post retirement health
benefits. When the retiree turns 65, the type of coverage and employee
contribution for retirees over 65 become effective.

2. Type of Coverage

The retired employee may continue enrollment in the health insurance plan
offered by the City under which he/she is covered at the time of retirement.

Ie

Employee Contribution

The retired employee pays the full amount of the retiree premium

Enhanced Retiree Health Insurance Benefit
1. Eligibility

On or after July 1, 2017 the retiring employee must have a minimum of
twenty (20) years of creditable VRS service with the City of Poquoson and a
minimum of 90 days of unused sick leave at the time of retirement from City
service. Further, the retiring employee must give the employer a minimum
of six (6) months’ written notice of their intention to retire and must have
participated in the City health care plan for a minimum of five (5) years
immediately prior to retirement.

2. Employer Contribution

The City will contribute fifty (50) percent of the employee health insurance

premium for retirees wishing to continue coverage under the City health care
plan up to age 65. The City’s fifty (50) percent contribution will be reduced
by the amount of any employer-paid health insurance credit that the retiree
may qualify for under the VRS program.

Retirees Over 65
1. Eligibility

The retired employee must be age 65 or older or otherwise eligible for Medicare.
Retired employees in all cases must have a minimum of 5 years of service to
become eligible for post retirement health benefits. The City has clected to
participate in the VRS Health Insurance Credit Program. For more information
contact the Finance Department.

2. Type of Coverage

The employee may only enroll in the Medicare Complementary Plan or an HMO
Medicare Plan offered by the administrator of the City’s Plan.

3. Employee Contribution

The retired employee pays the full amount of the retiree premium.

Retirees on Work Related Disability
1. Eligibility

The retired employee must be retired on a job related disability as defined by the
Virginia Retirement System and be eligible for workers’ compensation for the job
related disability. The retired employee must be totally disabled. Ifa retired
disabled employee works in another job where eligible for group medical benefits,
then eligibility in the City plan will cease. Retired disabled employees in all cases
must have a minimum of 5 years of service to become eligible for post retirement
health benefits. When the retiree turns 65, the type of coverage and employee
contribution for retirees over 65 will become effective.

2. Type of Coverage

The retired disabled employee may only enroll in an HMO plan offered by the
administrator of the City’s plan.

3. Employee Contribution

The City will contribute towards health insurance premiums on a graduated
scale depending on years of service as follows:

20 plus years - 80%
15-19 years - 70%
10-14 years - 60%
5-9 years - 50%

4. Negotiations

The City Manager may negotiate with the retired disabled employee to
supplement the employee’s payment to another health insurance plan in lieu of
paying the City’s share of premiums if it is beneficial for the City to do so.

Premium Payments

Retirees will pay by cash, check or money order prior to the first of the month of
coverage. Retirees who fail to pay their premium by the last day of the month preceding
the first of their coverage month will be given a five-calendar day grace period. If the

premium is not paid by then they will be notified by certified mail of the cancellation of
their medical benefits.

Enrollment in Plan

The only opportunity for the employee to enroll in the plan is upon retirement, with one
exception. The exception to this rule is the retiree who is enrolled in a spouse’s active
employee health benefits membership through the City of Poquoson. When the active
employee terminates or retires, the retired spouse may elect to come into the Retiree
Group within 31 days of the event. Retiring employees who do not wish to enroll in the
City’s Retiree Health Insurance Plan should sign the waiver portion of the Enrollment
Form.

Employees must submit an application for retiree health insurance coverage within 31
days of the retirement application or notice.

Employees who terminate from active service and defer retirement will not be eligible for
the City’s Retiree Health Insurance Plan.

A retiree may change plans at retirement, during an open enrollment period or at the time
of an eligibility status change. However, an employee may change plans upon becoming
eligible for Medicare.

Coverage of Dependents

An employee may apply for employee plus one or family membership at retirement,
whether or not the eligible family members were covered in the active employee group
while the retiree was actively employed. A retiree may change their type of coverage
upon enrollment or during any open enrollment period. The retired employee will pay
for the cost of the premium for family coverage, except in the case of the job related
disabled employce where the City will contribute towards health insurance premiums on
a graduated scale depending on years of service.
