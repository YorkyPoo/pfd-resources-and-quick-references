# Succinylcholine
**Trade Name:** Anectine

## Classification & Action
- **Drug Class:** Depolarizing neuromuscular blocking agent / paralytic
- **Mechanism of Action:** Rapidly depolarizes skeletal muscle cells, causing them to briefly contract, followed by flaccid paralysis (30 – 45 seconds)

## Indications
- Rapid sequence induction

## Contraindications
- Hypersensitivity / allergy
- Prior malignant hyperthermia
- Muscular dystrophy
- Myopathies
- Hyperkalemia (including up to 21 days after burn or crush injuries)
- Stroke / spinal cord injury / denervation syndromes

## Precautions
- PARALYZING AGENT. PATIENT MUST BE VENTILATED.
- Anesthetic / induction agent must be given prior to paralytic when performing RSI.
- May induce hyperkalemia leading to ventricular dysrhythmia and cardiac arrest.
- Does not provide analgesia or amnesia.
