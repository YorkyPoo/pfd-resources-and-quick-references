# **[Amiodarone](../pharmacology/Amiodarone.md)**
**Trade Name:** **Cordarone**

## Classification & Action
- **Drug Class:** Antidysrhythmic
- **Mechanism of Action:** Sodium and potassium channel blocker. Prolongs cardiac action potential and repolarization time.

## Indications
- Cardiac Arrest: VT/VF refractory to defibrillation
- Wide Complex Tachycardia (VT with a pulse)

## Contraindications
- 2nd or 3rd degree AV block
- Known hypersensitivity

## Precautions
- Caution in use with patients taking **Beta blockers**, **Calcium channel blockers**

## Dosage
### Adults
- **300 mg** IV/IO slow push — for Cardiac Arrest (may repeat **150 mg**).
- **150 mg** IV/IO over 10 minutes — for Wide Complex Tachycardia with a pulse.

### Pediatrics
- **5 mg/kg** IV/IO — for Cardiac Arrest (max **300 mg**).

## Pharmacokinetics
| Onset | Duration |
| :--- | :--- |
| Immediate | Variable (days to weeks) |

## Side Effects
- Hypotension
- Bradycardia
- Nausea/vomiting
- Flushing

## Interactions
- **Beta blockers** and **Calcium channel blockers** may potentiate bradycardia and hypotension.

## Pearls
- ⚠ **Warning:** Caution in use with patients taking **Beta blockers** or **Calcium channel blockers**.
- May cause profound hypotension or bradycardia, particularly if given too rapidly to a patient with a pulse.
