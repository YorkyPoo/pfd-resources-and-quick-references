    Responsibilities for REHAB A. Incident Command (IC) shall:

    e Assure adequate steps are taken to provide for each person’s health and safety by maintaining an awareness of:

    © The duration of an incident

    o The labor intensities of the various activities being performed.

    o The environmental conditions on the incident scene. (See: Tables 1-1 and 1-2)

    e Incident command shall establish a REHAB Division or Group for rest and rehabilitation of members operating at an emergency incident scene or training evolution.

    o REHAB should be considered during the initial planning stages.

    o Itshall be the responsibility of Incident Command to rotate members into REHAB as a company/unit.

    o Incident Command will be notified when a company/unit has been released from REHAB.

    e Assure that his/her entire company/unit reports to REHAB as directed by Incident Command.

    D. Members shall be encouraged to drink water and activity beverages throughout the workday. During any emergency incident or training evolution, all members shall advise their Company Officer when they believe their level of fatigue or exposure to heat or cold is approaching a level that could affect them, their crew or the operation in which they are involved. Members shall also remain aware of the health and safety of other members assigned to their crew.

    Location of REHAB

    A. REHAB should be in a location that will provide physical rest by allowing the body to recuperate from the environmental demands and hazards of the emergency incident scene or training evolution. REHAB should:

    e Be far enough away from the scene that members may safety remove their turnout gear/protective equipment and SCBA, and be afforded mental rest from the stress and pressure of the emergency incident scene or training evolution.

    e Provide suitable protection from the prevailing environmental conditions.

    o During hot weather, REHAB should be located in a cool, shaded area.

    o During cold weather, REHAB should be in a warm, dry area.

    e Be located in an area free of exhaust fumes and noise from apparatus, vehicles or equipment.

    e Be of sufficient size (based on the size of the incident) to accommodate multiple companies/units.

    e Be located to facilitate prompt reentry to the emergency incident scene or training evolution, upon completion of the REHAB process.

    Phases of REHAB A. Rest:

    e Members having utilized two (2) full SCBA cylinders, or worked for 45 minutes, should be immediately rotated into REHAB for re-hydration, rest and medical evaluation.

    e Members should (when possible) re-hydrate while SCBA cylinders are re-filled or changed.

    e Inall cases, the objective evaluation of a person's fatigue level by REHAB shall be the criteria for REHAB time.

    e Rest shall be no less than ten (10) minutes, and may

    exceed thirty (30) minutes, based on the medical evaluation of the member’s physical survey components.

    B. _Re-hydration :

    e Acritical factor in the prevention of heat injury is the maintenance of water and electrolytes.

    o Water must be replaced during exercise periods and at emergency incident scenes or training evolutions.

    o During heat stress conditions, members should consume at least one (1) quart of water per hour.

    o The re-hydration solution should be a mixture of

    EMERGENCY OPERATIONS

    water and a commercially prepared activity beverage and administered at about 40°F.

    e Re-hydration is important during cold weather operations where, despite the ambient air temperature, heat stress may occur during any strenuous activities when turnout gear/protective equipment is worn.

    Note: Alcohol, caffeine, and carbonated beverages should be avoided before and during heat stress conditions. These substances interfere with the body's water conservation mechanisms.

    Cc. Recovery :

    e Members in the REHAB area should maintain a high level of hydration.

    o Members should not be moved from a hot environment directly into an air-conditioned area because the body's cooling system could shut down in response to the external cooling.

    o An air-conditioned environment is acceptable after a cool-down period at ambient temperature with sufficient air movement.

    e Certain drugs impair the body's ability to perspire and extreme caution must be exercised if a person has taken antihistamines, such as Actifed® or Benadry1®, or has taken diuretics or stimulants.

    D. Nourishment - Food should be provided at the scene of an emergency incident when multiple units are engaged for extended periods of time.

    e Acup of soup, broth, or stew is highly recommended

    because it is digested much faster than sandwiches and fast food products.

    F. Physical Survey Components (results)

    e Status o — No condition exists that prevents the member

    from performing any task on an emergency incident scene or training evolution.

    Status 1 — A minor condition exists that will need to be re-evaluated. During the re-evaluation period, the member will stay at rest while recovering and hydrating (if appropriate). A medical evaluation will be repeated twice in fifteen (15) minute intervals. Should a member’s condition remain unchanged or worsen, then the member will be placed into Status 2.

    Status 2 — A moderate condition exists that will not allow the member to return to physically demanding or stressful assignments. These members will stay at rest while recovering and hydrating (if appropriate) for an additional re-evaluation period, during which a medical evaluation will be repeated twice in fifteen (15) minute intervals. Should a member’s condition remain unchanged or worsen, then the member will be placed into Status 3.
